CHAPTER 002 (Variation Under Domestication) — Translation Notes
================================================================

MAJOR CHANGES AND RATIONALE
----------------------------

1. SENTENCE STRUCTURE: Darwin's longest and most deeply nested sentences were
   broken into shorter ones while preserving logical flow. The pigeon description
   passage was kept in long, rolling sentences where the accumulation of detail
   IS the point — breaking those into short fragments would destroy the effect
   of Darwin cataloguing breed after breed. The summary paragraph at the end of
   the pigeon section (beginning "From these several reasons") was kept as one
   long sentence with em-dashes, preserving Darwin's rhetorical structure of
   piling up evidence before delivering the conclusion. Semicolons used as
   period-substitutes were converted to actual periods throughout.

2. HEDGING REDUCTION (~30-40%):
   - "As far as I am able to judge" → "As far as I can judge"
   - "it seems clear that organic beings must be exposed" → "It seems clear
     that organisms must be exposed" (kept "seems" — genuine caution here)
   - "There is, also, some probability in the view propounded by" → "There
     is also some support for the view proposed by"
   - "it seems to me not improbable" → "it seems possible to me"
   - "I am fully convinced" → "I'm fully convinced" (kept in the pigeon
     passage — this is Darwin staking his credibility)
   - "I cannot doubt" → "I have no doubt"
   - "it is not a little remarkable" patterns → simplified to direct statements
   - "we are driven to conclude" → kept (characterful, not mere hedging)
   - Removed double/triple qualifications where they appeared adjacent
   - Kept Darwin's "I believe" and "I think" throughout — these are his
     characteristic voice, not mere hedging

3. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION (per text analysis mappings):
   - "organic beings" → "organisms" throughout
   - "productions" (meaning organisms) → "organisms" / "creatures"
   - "intercrossing" → "crossbreeding" / "crossing"
   - "mongrels" → "hybrids" / "crossbreeds"
   - "aboriginally" → "originally"
   - "aboriginal stock" → "wild ancestor" / "ancestral stock" / "original wild ancestor"
   - "propounded" → "proposed"
   - "hitherto" → "previously"
   - "whilst" → "while"
   - "shew/shewn" → "show/shown"
   - "vitiated instincts" → "corrupted instincts"
   - "peculiarities" → kept in most cases (still natural), sometimes → "traits"
   - "monstrous character" → "exaggerated character" (to avoid misleading
     modern connotations; Darwin means "extreme/abnormal")
   - "monstrosities" → kept when referring to actual developmental anomalies
   - "lineal descendants" → "direct descendants"
   - "naturalising" → "establishing"
   - "esculent vegetables" → "edible vegetables"
   - "pertinacious" → "stubborn"
   - "manifestly" → "clearly"
   - "efficacious" → "effective"
   - "heartsease" → "pansy" (modern common name, more recognizable)

4. PIGEON PASSAGES: These are the heart of the chapter and were given special
   attention. The breed descriptions were kept vivid and detailed — every breed
   mentioned, every physical detail preserved. The passage about the pouter
   glorying in inflating its crop was kept with its original charm. The passage
   about crossing white fantails with black barbs was kept in full experimental
   detail — this is Darwin the empiricist at his best, and the blue rock-pigeon
   appearing from the cross is a genuine "wow" moment. The rhetorical structure
   of the "seven or eight species" argument was preserved: Darwin systematically
   demolishes the alternative hypothesis point by point.

5. THE "CRACKERS" ANECDOTE: Kept the Virginia pig/paint-root story in full,
   including the direct quote. This is one of Darwin's most vivid examples of
   correlated variation and natural/unconscious selection working together.

6. SECTION HEADINGS: All original section headings preserved. One long heading
   ("Character of Domestic Varieties; Difficulty of Distinguishing Between
   Varieties and Species; Origin of Domestic Varieties from One or More
   Species") kept in full.

7. FAMOUS QUOTATIONS: Preserved the Youatt "magician's wand" quote, the Lord
   Somerville "chalked out upon a wall" quote, the Pliny quote about pigeons'
   pedigrees, and the Akbar Khan passage in full. These are historical voices
   Darwin is channeling.

8. BRIEF IDENTIFIERS ADDED:
   - Professor Weismann: no extra identifier (his role is clear from context)
   - Andrew Knight: no extra identifier (mentioned once, not central)
   - Kolreuter: no extra identifier (mentioned once alongside "and others")
   - Isidore Geoffroy St. Hilaire: no extra identifier (his work is described)
   - Professor Rutimeyer: no extra identifier (his research is described)
   - Mr. Blyth: no extra identifier (his contributions are described in context)
   - Youatt: no extra identifier (described as expert on agriculture in context)
   These are all minor figures whose roles are clear from context. Adding
   identifiers for every one would clutter the text.

9. ENDNOTE REFERENCES: Superscript endnote numbers were omitted per the text
   analysis guidance (they add clutter without value for the modern reader;
   these are purely bibliographic citations).

10. TONE: Evidence-presenting mode with Darwin's personal enthusiasm showing
    through, especially in the pigeon passages. More argumentative than the
    Introduction — Darwin is building his case methodically. The chapter ends
    with a powerful summation that sets up everything that follows.

11. SENSITIVE CONTENT:
    - "savages" → retained in some instances where Darwin uses it, but also
      replaced with "early humans," "peoples without agriculture," "isolated
      peoples," or "indigenous peoples" where appropriate without distorting
      meaning. The Tierra del Fuego passage about killing old women was kept
      as Darwin wrote it — it's a jarring example but it's his text.
    - "race" (biological sense) → "breed" or "variety" in most cases
    - "negroes" → "the people" (in the Livingstone passage)
    - "barbarous" → "primitive" or "early" where possible without losing meaning
    - "Eskimo" → "Inuit"

12. ITALICS: Species names (*Columba livia*, *Gallus bankiva*, *Lachnanthes*,
    *Dipsacus*) and Darwin's book title (*Variation Under Domestication*)
    marked with markdown italics.

TONE CALIBRATION FOR NEXT CHAPTER:
- Chapter 003 (Variation Under Nature) is more technical and data-heavy than
  this chapter. It involves extensive species counts, references to botanists'
  catalogs, and statistical reasoning about large vs. small genera.
- The tone should shift from the enthusiastic pigeon-breeder voice toward a
  more systematic, data-presenting mode. Darwin is now showing that the
  variation he demonstrated under domestication also exists in the wild.
- Key argument to keep crystal clear: the distinction between "species" and
  "varieties" is arbitrary and gradual, not sharp. This is crucial setup for
  the natural selection argument.
- Many botanist and taxonomist references (Alphonse de Candolle, H.C. Watson,
  Asa Gray, etc.) — identify on first mention per our established practice.
- "Individual differences" is an important term Darwin introduces — keep it.
- This chapter is shorter and drier than Ch. I. Don't try to inject false
  enthusiasm. Let the data speak. Darwin's quiet confidence that the data
  supports his view is the right tone.
- The chapter transitions directly into Ch. IV (Struggle for Existence),
  which will be more vivid and powerful. Ch. III is the bridge.
