CHAPTER 007 (Difficulties of the Theory) -- Translation Notes
================================================================

MAJOR CHANGES AND RATIONALE
----------------------------

1. SENTENCE STRUCTURE: This is one of Darwin's longest chapters (~15,600 words
   original). Many sentences run 80-120+ words, especially in the eye passage,
   the swim bladder discussion, and the orchid pollination sections.
   - Semicolons used as period-substitutes -> actual periods throughout
   - Multi-clause sentences of 50+ words broken into 2-3 sentences
   - Parenthetical qualifications moved to their own sentences where they
     disrupted flow
   - Paragraph structure preserved exactly -- each paragraph is one sub-argument
   - Section headings preserved exactly (with minor modernization)

2. HEDGING REDUCTION (~30-40%):
   - "to the best of my judgment" -> kept (characterful opening)
   - "I can hardly reflect on them without being in some degree staggered" ->
     "I can hardly reflect on them without being staggered" (removed "in some
     degree" -- strengthens the famous admission)
   - "it will be more convenient to discuss" -> kept (natural transition)
   - "This difficulty for a long time quite confounded me" -> "This difficulty
     confused me for a long time" (simplified)
   - "as far as I can make out" -> "as far as I can tell"
   - "I freely confess" -> kept (essential to the eye passage's rhetorical power)
   - "seems, I freely confess, absurd in the highest degree" -> kept exactly
     (too famous to modify)
   - "it does not seem impossible" -> "it doesn't seem impossible"
   - "we ought not to expect" -> "we shouldn't expect"
   - "it would be extremely bold to maintain" -> kept (characterful)
   - Kept Darwin's key rhetorical hedges throughout: "I believe," "I think,"
     "as I believe," "no doubt," "probably," "perhaps"

3. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION (per text analysis mappings):
   - "organic beings" -> "organisms" / "living things" / "organisms" throughout
   - "productions" (meaning organisms) -> "organisms" / "productions of nature"
     (kept in beauty context)
   - "profitable modifications" -> "beneficial changes"
   - "hitherto" -> not prominent in this chapter
   - "quadrupeds" -> "mammals" (in browsing context, fur context)
   - "lineal progenitors" -> "direct ancestors"
   - "metropolis" (of a species) -> "heartland" (range center)
   - "the polity of the country" -> "the natural order of the country"
   - "intercrossing" -> "crossbreeding" (limited use in this chapter)
   - "insensibly" -> "imperceptibly" / "gradually"
   - "confines" -> "edges"
   - "neutral territory" -> kept (clear in context)
   - "mongrel" -> not used in this chapter
   - "naturalised" -> not prominently used in this chapter
   - "copious" -> not prominent in this chapter
   - "inasmuch as" -> "since" / "because"
   - "branchiae" -> "gills" (with "branchiae" used where first introduced for clarity)
   - "sarcode/sarcodic" -> "tissue" / "simple tissue"
   - "Articulata" -> "arthropods" (modern class name)
   - "Vox populi, vox Dei" -> "the voice of the people is the voice of God"
     (translated inline)
   - "Natura non facit saltum" -> "Nature does not make leaps" (translated inline)
   - "elytra" -> "hard wing-covers" (brief explanation)
   - "ovipositor" -> "egg-laying tube"
   - "Grallatores" -> "wading birds"
   - "subaquatic" -> "underwater"
   - American spellings used throughout (color, favorable, organization, etc.)
   - "insectivorous quadruped" -> "insect-eating mammal"

     Utilitarian Doctrine: How Far True -- Beauty, How Acquired" (minor)

10. TONE: This chapter is one of Darwin's best -- the most rhetorically powerful
    and the most honest about difficulties. The translation preserves the careful
    rhetorical arc: state each difficulty forcefully and honestly, then gradually
    build a convincing response. The eye passage retains its full power. Darwin's
    raw honesty ("I can hardly reflect on them without being staggered," "seems,
    I freely confess, absurd in the highest degree") is preserved with full
    emotional weight. The confidence calibration is: honest about the very real
    difficulties, but ultimately showing that none is fatal. The chapter ends
    with the elegant philosophical synthesis (Unity of Type + Conditions of
    Existence) that is confident but not overreaching.

11. NOTABLE VOCABULARY CHANGE -- "NIGGARD" TO "STINGY":
    The Milne Edwards quote "Nature is prodigal in variety, but niggard in
    innovation" has been updated to "Nature is lavish in variety, but stingy
    with innovation." The word "niggard" (meaning miserly/stingy), while
    etymologically unrelated to any slur, is frequently misread by modern
    audiences and would be a significant distraction. "Stingy" preserves the
    exact meaning. "Prodigal" updated to "lavish" for the same reason --
    accessibility.

KEY CONCEPTS ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:
- Missing transitional forms explained by: slow variation, narrow intermediate
  zones, extinction of intermediates by more numerous forms on either side
- Organs of extreme perfection (the eye) can be formed by graduated steps --
  the famous passage
- Conversion of function: organs can change their purpose (swim bladder -> lungs,
  egg frena -> gills, breathing tubes -> wings)
- Same function achieved independently in unrelated groups = analogous not
  homologous (cephalopod eyes vs. vertebrate eyes, electric organs in different
  fish)
- Same end achieved by diverse means throughout nature
- Organs of little importance may be inherited from ancestors where they were
  important, or due to correlated variation, laws of growth, sexual selection
- Beauty in nature: flowers colored for insects, fruits colored for seed dispersal,
  male animals beautiful through sexual selection -- not created for human delight
- Natural selection cannot produce anything exclusively for another species' benefit
- Natural selection does not produce absolute perfection (bee sting, eye
  imperfections, drone waste)
- Unity of Type explained by unity of descent; Conditions of Existence embraced
  by natural selection

TONE CALIBRATION FOR NEXT CHAPTER:
- Chapter 008 (Miscellaneous Objections) was added in later editions of the book.
  It is Darwin's most directly polemical chapter, responding to specific critics
  including St. George Mivart. The tone should be: respectful but firm, more
  argumentative and point-by-point than the broad difficulties addressed in
  Chapter VII. Darwin is on the defensive but confident.
- Many references to Mivart's specific criticisms -- preserve all of these
  faithfully as they constitute Darwin's direct rebuttals.
- Cross-references back to Chapter VII concepts (the eye, transitional forms,
  conversion of function) should be maintained.
- The chapter includes the important discussion of incipient stages of useful
  structures and the question of whether natural selection explains the initial
  as well as the final stages of a structure's development.
- Some sections deal with the pace of evolutionary change (gradual vs. sudden)
  -- maintain the nuance of Darwin's position.
- All specific examples and scientific data must be preserved in full.
