KEY CONCEPTS ESTABLISHED IN THESE TWO CHAPTERS:
- Freshwater organisms disperse mainly via birds (seeds in mud on feet, shells on feet,
  seeds in fish eaten by herons)
- Oceanic islands have few species but high endemism
- Some groups on islands are entirely endemic, others not -- depends on ease of immigration
  and frequency of reinforcement from mainland
- Frogs and land mammals absent from true oceanic islands (killed by seawater)
- Bats present because they can fly across oceans
- Sea depth correlates with mammalian fauna distinctness (Wallace's Line)
- Island species related to nearest mainland, not to physically similar islands elsewhere
  (Galapagos = American; Cape Verde = African)
- Within an archipelago, species on different islands are closely related but distinct
  (Galapagos mockingbirds)
- Prior occupancy prevents invasion by closely related species
- Alpine, cave, lake, and marsh species related to surrounding lowland/dry-land species
- Lower organisms range more widely than higher ones
- Parallel between laws of succession in time and distribution in space
- All explicable by migration + modification + natural selection
