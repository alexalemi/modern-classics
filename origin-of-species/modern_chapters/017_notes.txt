Chapter 017 (Glossary) Translation Notes
=========================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This glossary was prepared by W. S. Dallas for the 6th edition to help
general readers with technical terminology. The modernized version preserves
the full alphabetical glossary format and every headword, while making
definitions accessible at an eighth-grade reading level.

MAJOR CHANGES
-------------

1. SENTENCE STRUCTURE: Long, multi-clause Victorian definitions were broken
   into shorter sentences. For example, the Cirripedes entry (originally one
   79-word sentence) was restructured into several clear sentences.

2. VOCABULARY UPDATES:
   - "colouring matters" -> "pigments (coloring substances)"
   - "prehension" -> "grasping" or "capturing"
   - "calcareous" -> "chalky" or "calcium" (with "calcareous" sometimes kept
     for context)
   - "fecundation" -> "fertilization"
   - "impregnated" -> "fertilized"
   - "articulated/articulate" -> "jointed" or "jointed-limbed"
   - "gelatinous material" -> "jelly-like body/material"
   - "animalcules" -> kept as headword but explained clearly
   - "membranous" -> kept (still accessible) but clarified where needed
   - "fossiliferous" -> kept as headword, defined simply as "containing fossils"
   - "ossified" -> "hardened"
   - "destitute of" -> "lack" or "without"

3. MODERN CONTEXT ADDED (sparingly):
   - Batrachians: added "(Now generally called amphibians.)" since modern
     readers know amphibians, not batrachians
   - Glacial Period: added "(Commonly known as the Ice Age.)"
   - Rudimentary: added "(Also called vestigial.)" since that is the modern term
   - Sarcode: added "(Now generally called protoplasm or cytoplasm.)"
   - Pleistocene: added note about Ice Ages
   - Ichneumonidae: added "(Parasitic wasps.)" for clarity
   - Rubus: expanded to "blackberries and raspberries (brambles)" since
     "bramble" alone is less familiar

4. FORMAT: Preserved the exact same alphabetical order and headword structure.
   Combined entries that Dallas listed as variants (e.g., Aplacentalia /
   Aplacentata / Aplacental Mammals on one line). Kept all cross-references
   (e.g., "See Mammalia," "See Neuters").

5. REMOVED/SIMPLIFIED:
   - Removed "said to be" constructions where they added formality without
     clarity
   - Simplified passive voice to active where it helped readability
   - "Quadrupeds" generally kept (still understandable) but occasionally
     clarified as "four-legged animals"

6. PRESERVED AS-IS:
   - All scientific headwords (these appear in the main text)
   - All genus and family names (Balanus, Canidae, Felidae, etc.)
   - Cross-references between entries
   - Dallas's overall organizational approach
   - Terms that are still current and clear (e.g., Embryo, Habitat, Parasite)
