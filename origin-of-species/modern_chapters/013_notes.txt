KEY CONCEPTS ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:
- Climate alone doesn't explain species distribution; barriers do
- Organisms of the same continent are related to each other despite different conditions
  (the inheritance explanation)
- Most species likely originated in a single area and migrated from there
- Continental land bridges are not the explanation for most distributions (contra Forbes)
- Seeds can survive long sea voyages (Darwin's experiments: 64/87 kinds after 28 days)
- Birds are major seed transporters (feet, crops, pellets, via fish)
- Driftwood, icebergs, locusts all serve as transport vectors
- Glacial periods explain identical species on distant mountain summits
- Croll's theory: alternating glacial periods in northern/southern hemispheres
- During northern glaciation, temperate forms invaded equatorial lowlands; southern
  hemisphere was warmer
- When warmth returned, temperate forms retreated but some survived on tropical mountains
- More species migrated north-to-south than south-to-north (northern dominance)
- "Mountains are islands on the land" -- mountain species have yielded to northern forms
