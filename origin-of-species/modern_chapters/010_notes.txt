CHAPTER 010 (Hybridism) -- Translation Notes
=============================================

MAJOR CHANGES AND RATIONALE
----------------------------

1. SENTENCE STRUCTURE: This chapter (~12,900 words original) is one of Darwin's most
   technical and data-heavy chapters, reviewing extensive experimental evidence from
   Gartner, Kolreuter, Herbert, and others on cross-fertility and sterility.
   - Semicolons used as period-substitutes -> actual periods throughout
   - Multi-clause sentences of 50+ words broken into 2-3 sentences
   - Parenthetical qualifications moved to their own sentences where they disrupted flow
   - Paragraph structure preserved exactly -- each paragraph is one sub-argument
   - Section breaks and headings preserved exactly
   - The long grafting analogy passage required careful restructuring to maintain
     clarity while preserving the logical parallel Darwin builds

2. HEDGING REDUCTION (~30-40%):
   - "The view commonly entertained by naturalists is that" -> "The view most naturalists
     hold is that"
   - "This view certainly seems at first highly probable" -> "This certainly seems
     plausible at first"
   - "it is most difficult to say" -> "it's extremely difficult to say"
   - "I think no better evidence of this can be required than that" -> "I think no
     better evidence of this could be asked for than the fact that"
   - "it may be urged as an overwhelming argument" -> "It might be argued as an
     overwhelming objection"
   - "I am fully persuaded" -> "I'm fully convinced"
   - "I believe" -> kept throughout (characterful)
   - "I must, however, be owned that" -> "I must admit, however, that"
   - "Nor do I pretend that" -> "Nor do I claim that"
   - Double qualifications compressed where adjacent
   - Kept all key rhetorical hedges and moments of honest admission of uncertainty

3. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION (per text analysis mappings):
   - "organic beings" -> "living things" / "organisms" throughout
   - "intercrossed" / "intercrossing" -> "crossed" / "crossbreeding" / "crossing"
   - "mongrel" (variety hybrid) -> "crossbreed" / "crossbred" throughout
   - "productions" -> "organisms"
   - "profitable degrees" -> "beneficial degrees" (not used -- used "greater degrees")
   - "hitherto" -> "previously" / "so far"
   - "aboriginal" -> "original" / "native"
   - "naturalised" -> not prominent in this chapter
   - "insensibly" -> "imperceptibly"
   - "germen" -> "ovary"

   - "superfluous" -> "unnecessary"
   - "aboriginally" -> "originally"

   - "indelible characteristic" -> "permanent characteristic"

   - "female-ass" -> "female donkey"
   - "male-ass" -> "male donkey"

   - "vegetated freely" -> "germinated freely"
   - American spellings throughout (favorable, vigor, color, etc.)

4. TONE AND CHARACTER:
   - This chapter is Darwin at his most methodical and data-driven. The tone is careful,
     evidence-weighing, and argumentative (in the scholarly sense). Preserved this
     character throughout.
   - The chapter builds a layered case: (1) sterility is not absolute, (2) its laws
     are too irregular to be "designed," (3) it can't be explained by natural selection,
     (4) it parallels the effects of changed conditions, (5) dimorphism/trimorphism
     proves sterility is about sexual elements alone.
   - The grafting analogy is one of Darwin's most effective rhetorical moves in the
     chapter -- preserved in full with the parallel structure intact.
   - The dimorphism/trimorphism section is complex and abstract -- kept the logical
     steps clear while preserving Darwin's evident pride in this evidence.
   - The chapter summary faithfully recapitulates all major points.

5. KEY TERMINOLOGICAL DECISIONS:
   - "hybrid" vs "crossbreed": Darwin distinguishes between "hybrids" (offspring of
     species crosses) and "mongrels" (offspring of variety crosses). Translated
     "mongrels" as "crossbreeds" throughout to maintain this important distinction.
     The section heading "Hybrids and Mongrels Compared" -> "Hybrids and Crossbreeds
     Compared."
   - "legitimate" / "illegitimate" unions (dimorphism section): kept Darwin's terms
     as they're still used in botanical literature and he defines them clearly.
   - "prepotent" / "prepotency": kept as-is (still standard in genetics); Darwin
     also uses "dominant" in other contexts, which I sometimes used as a synonym.
   - "first cross": kept this phrase (meaning the initial mating between two species)
     as it's clear from context.
   - "reciprocal cross": kept and defined clearly on first use.
   - "systematic affinity": kept with definition on first use ("the overall resemblance
     between species in structure and constitution").

KEY CONCEPTS ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:
- Sterility of species crosses is incidental (a byproduct), not a special endowment
- Two distinct classes: sterility of first crosses vs. sterility of hybrid offspring
- Degrees of sterility form a continuous gradation from zero to absolute
- Sterility is inherently variable and affected by conditions
- Reciprocal crosses often give very different results
- Grafting analogy: parallels between grafting difficulty and crossing difficulty,
  both incidental on unknown differences, neither specially designed
- Natural selection cannot explain sterility (can't select for greater sterility
  once seed production has already ceased; no individual advantage)
- Sterility parallels effects of changed conditions on reproductive system
- Dimorphism/trimorphism proves sterility depends on sexual elements alone, not
  on general structure or constitution
- Illegitimate plants are "hybrids within a species" -- same laws apply
- Variety crosses generally fertile, but not universally (maize, Verbascum,
  tobacco exceptions)
- "Mongrel" (crossbreed) vs "hybrid" distinction: few important differences
- Pallas doctrine: domestication eliminates sterility
- Double parallel: slight changes in conditions = beneficial, large changes =
  sterility; slight crosses = beneficial, wide crosses = sterility
- Species originally existed as varieties (chapter's closing argument)
