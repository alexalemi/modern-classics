   - Semicolons used as period-substitutes -> actual periods throughout

   - Section breaks preserved exactly (marked by blank lines in original)

     chapter to addressing various objections that have been raised against my views"

     address all of them"

KEY CONCEPTS ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:
- Incipient stages of useful structures CAN be accounted for by natural selection,
  habit/use, laws of growth, and correlated variation
- Many seemingly useless morphological characters explained by: laws of growth,
  correlated variation, spontaneous variation, conditions of life -- NOT by any
  innate tendency toward perfection
- Characters unimportant to welfare but important to taxonomists -- the paradox
  of classification
- Extended gradation arguments: goose beak -> duck beak -> shoveler beak -> whale
  baleen; spine -> pedicellariae; zooid -> avicularium -> vibraculum; pollen
  threads -> caudicle -> pollinium
- Flatfish eye migration explained by habit + inheritance + natural selection
- Against sudden/saltational evolution: embryology shows gradual differentiation;
  abrupt changes require simultaneous occurrence in multiple individuals;
  monstrosities =/= species formation
- "To admit all this is, as it seems to me, to enter into the realms of miracle,
  and to leave those of science" -- powerful closing statement
