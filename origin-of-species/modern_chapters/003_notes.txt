CHAPTER 003 (Variation Under Nature) -- Translation Notes
================================================================

MAJOR CHANGES AND RATIONALE
----------------------------

1. SENTENCE STRUCTURE: Darwin's long nested sentences were broken into shorter,
   clearer sentences while preserving logical flow. Semicolons used as period
   substitutes were converted to actual periods. The De Candolle direct quotation
   was preserved exactly as written (it's a translated passage that Darwin himself
   quoted). The Wallace direct quote about "no possible test but individual opinion"
   was also preserved verbatim.

2. HEDGING REDUCTION (~30-40%):
   - "It may be doubted whether" -> "It's doubtful whether" (more direct)
   - "These facts are very perplexing, for they seem to show" -> "These facts
     are very puzzling, because they seem to show" (kept "seem" -- genuine caution)
   - "I am inclined to suspect that we see" -> "I suspect that...we're seeing"
   - "I am convinced that the most experienced naturalist would be surprised" ->
     "I'm convinced that even the most experienced naturalist would be surprised"
   - "It certainly at first appears a highly remarkable fact" -> "It certainly
     seems remarkable at first"
   - "assuredly can be given" -> "can certainly be given"
   - "some considerable degree" -> "a considerable degree"
   - Kept Darwin's core hedges ("I believe," "I think," "it seems") where they
     carry genuine caution or are characterful
   - "I was much struck how entirely vague and arbitrary" -> "I was struck by
     how entirely vague and arbitrary" (reduced emphasis, kept the observation)

3. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION (per text analysis mappings):
   - "organic beings" -> "organisms" throughout
   - "organic beings in a state of nature" -> "organisms in the wild"
   - "productions" -> "organisms"
   - "hitherto" -> not present in this chapter
   - "whilst" -> not present in this chapter
   - "shew/shewn" -> not present in this chapter
   - "intercross" -> "interbreed"
   - "perplexing" -> "puzzling" (in most cases; kept "perplexing" once for variety)
   - "insensible series" -> "unbroken series"
   - "sagacious" -> "sharp" (describing observers)
   - "signification" -> "meaning"
   - "conferva" -> "green alga" (with original context making identification clear)
   - "coleopterous insects" -> "beetles" (clearer for modern audience)
   - "inasmuch as" -> "since"
   - "in the lapse of time" -> "over time"
   - "compatriots" -> "neighbors" (in the ecological sense)
   - "predominence" (Darwin's spelling) -> "dominance"
   - "locomotive" (meaning mobile) -> "mobile"
   - "propagated" -> "passed on"
   - "the economy of nature" -> "ecological role" (in the Wallace passage about
     representative species filling "the same place in the natural economy")

4. TECHNICAL DATA PRESERVED: All species counts, proportional data, and numerical
   comparisons kept exactly: Watson's 182 British plants, Babington's 251 vs.
   Bentham's 112 species (difference of 139), Watson's 63 doubtful species
   ranging over 6.9 provinces vs. 53 varieties over 7.7 provinces vs. parent
   species over 14.3 provinces. The twelve-country flora analysis and two-district
   beetle analysis mentioned intact.

5. SECTION HEADINGS: All original section headings preserved exactly, including
   the long compound ones ("Many of the Species Included Within the Larger Genera
   Resemble Varieties...").

6. BRIEF IDENTIFIERS ADDED:
   - "Mr. H. C. Watson" -> "The botanist H. C. Watson" on first mention; "Watson"
     on subsequent mentions
   - "Dr. Hooker" -> "the botanist Joseph Hooker" on first mention
   - "Alphonse de Candolle" -> "the botanist Alphonse de Candolle" on first mention
   - "Asa Gray" -> "the botanist Asa Gray" on first mention
   - "Mr. Wallace" -> "Alfred Russel Wallace" on first mention (established from
     earlier chapters)
   - "Fries" -> "the botanist Fries"
   - "Westwood" -> "the entomologist Westwood"
   - "B. D. Walsh" -> "The distinguished American entomologist B. D. Walsh"
     (Darwin's own "distinguished entomologist" phrasing adapted)
   - Sir J. Lubbock, Fritz Muller, Wollaston, Babington, Bentham, Gaston de
     Saporta: roles clear from context, no extra identifier needed

7. ENDNOTE REFERENCES: Superscript endnote numbers omitted per text analysis
   guidance.

8. TONE: Systematic, data-presenting mode as specified in the previous chapter's
   notes. Let the data speak without injecting false enthusiasm. Darwin's quiet
   confidence that variation in nature parallels variation under domestication
   comes through in the accumulation of evidence. The chapter reads as a careful
   bridge between the vivid domestication examples and the struggle for existence
   to come.

9. QUOTATIONS PRESERVED:
   - De Candolle's extended passage about doubtful species ("They are mistaken...")
     preserved in full
   - Wallace's quoted line about "no possible test but individual opinion"
   - De Candolle's closing remark about the derivative theory being "the most
     natural one"

10. "SPECIES FACTORY" METAPHOR: Darwin's original "manufactory of species" was
    modernized to "species factory" -- the metaphor is vivid and important to
    preserve, but "manufactory" is archaic.

11. SENSITIVE CONTENT: No significant sensitive content in this chapter. "Race"
    appears as "geographical races" (taxonomic term) and was kept as-is since
    the context is purely biological and unambiguous.
