CHAPTER 000 (Historical Sketch) — Translation Notes
=====================================================

MAJOR CHANGES AND RATIONALE
----------------------------

1. SENTENCE STRUCTURE: Broke Darwin's long, multi-clause sentences throughout.
   Many original sentences ran 80-120 words with nested qualifications. These
   were split into 2-3 shorter sentences while preserving logical flow. Semicolons
   used as period-substitutes were converted to actual periods.

2. FRENCH PASSAGES: All French quotations were translated into English with
   a parenthetical note "(originally in French)" placed before or after the
   translated text. The three major French passages were:
   - Geoffroy Saint-Hilaire's son's remark about leaving the problem to the future
   - Isidore Geoffroy Saint-Hilaire's lecture summary on species variability
   - Naudin's "principle of finality" passage (the longest French block)
   - Lecoq's brief remark about Geoffroy Saint-Hilaire and Goethe

3. BIBLIOGRAPHIC REFERENCES: Kept journal names, volumes, page numbers, and
   years as Darwin provides them, but smoothed the prose around them. Abbreviated
   titles were expanded where helpful (e.g., "Hist. Nat. des Animaux sans
   Vertebres" → "Natural History of Invertebrates"; "Anat. of Vertebrates" →
   "Anatomy of Vertebrates"). Latin/French journal titles were kept as-is since
   they are proper names.

4. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION (per text analysis mappings):
   - "immutable productions" → "unchanging things" / "fixed and unchanging"
   - "organic beings" → "living things" / "organisms"
   - "promulgated" → "proposed" / "presented" / "put forward"
   - "intercrossing" → "crossbreeding" / "interbreeding"
   - "propounded" → "proposed" / "put forward"
   - "superfluous" → "unnecessary"
   - "aboriginally" → (not present in this chapter)
   - "preposterous" kept — it's vivid and still used
   - "monde ambiant" kept with explanation since it's Geoffroy's specific term

5. HEDGING REDUCTION (~30-40%):
   - "I will here give" → "I want to give"
   - "I need not here enter on details" → "I won't go into detail here"
   - Reduced some of the doubled qualifications
   - Kept Darwin's most characteristic hedging: "as far as I can make sense of"
     (from "as far as it is possible to understand")
   - Kept the ironic understatement about Owen: "It's some comfort to me..."

6. THE OWEN PASSAGE: This is the most complex part of the chapter — Darwin's
   extended, barely-contained frustration with Richard Owen's slippery claims.
   Preserved the full sequence of claim/retraction/re-retraction because it's
   essential to understanding the priority dispute and Darwin's character. The
   dry wit ("It's some comfort to me that others find Professor Owen's
   controversial writings as difficult to understand...") was kept intact.

7. WELLS PASSAGE ON RACE: Modernized "negroes and mulattoes" to "Black and
   mixed-race people" for the narrator's framing text. Within the direct
   quotation from Wells, kept closer to original phrasing but used "population"
   instead of "race" where Darwin/Wells meant a breeding population, per the
   text analysis guidance. The Wells quote is important because it's the first
   recognition of natural selection.

8. BRIEF IDENTIFIERS added on first mention per text analysis:
   - "the zoologist Professor Grant"
   - "the botanist Joseph Hooker" (at end, his first mention)
   - "the great embryologist" for Von Baer
   - "the distinguished botanist M. Naudin"
   Many figures (Lamarck, Buffon, Owen, Huxley, Spencer) are famous enough
   or identified by Darwin's own text that additional identifiers weren't needed.

9. TITLE: Simplified from "An Historical Sketch of the Progress of Opinion on
   the Origin of Species, Previously to the Publication of the First Edition of
   This Work" to "A Historical Sketch of How Ideas About the Origin of Species
   Developed Before This Book Was First Published." Kept "Historical Sketch"
   since Darwin references it by name later in the book.

10. ENDNOTE REFERENCES: The original had superscript numbers (1, 2, 3) pointing
    to endnotes. These were purely bibliographic and were omitted per the text
    analysis guidance about removing citation clutter. The substantive content
    of note 2 (about Lamarck's spontaneous generation views) was already
    captured in the main text.


NEXT CHAPTER NOTES (for Chapter 001 — Introduction)
-----------------------------------------------------

VOCABULARY CHOICES ESTABLISHED:
- "natural selection" — kept exactly as-is (standard term)
- "descent with modification" — kept exactly as-is (Darwin's phrase)
- "varieties" — kept as "varieties" (Darwin's usage, still clear)
- "living things" / "organisms" — used for "organic beings" / "productions"
- "crossbreeding" — used for "intercrossing"
- "environmental conditions" — used for "conditions of life" / "physical conditions of life"
- "proposed" / "put forward" — used for "promulgated" / "propounded"

STYLISTIC DECISIONS:
- First person throughout, contractions used naturally ("I'm," "don't," "won't,"
  "can't," "it's") but not excessively — Darwin is a formal speaker made more
  accessible, not made casual
- Paragraph structure preserved — each paragraph is one sub-argument
- Direct quotations from other authors preserved in quotes
- Hedging reduced but not eliminated; Darwin's strategic modesty is part of his voice
- Cross-references to other chapters kept faithfully
- British spellings NOT used (per text analysis final decision: American spellings
  for modern audience)

TONE CALIBRATION:
- This chapter (Historical Sketch) was the most academic/dry chapter in the book.
  The Introduction (001) is much more personal and autobiographical — Darwin tells
  his own story. The tone should warm up noticeably: more personal anecdotes,
  more "I" statements about his own journey, more conversational asides.
- The Introduction contains the famous "mystery of mysteries" phrase — preserve it.
- The Introduction is where Darwin lays out the book's roadmap. Make the structure
  clear and inviting.
- Darwin's characteristic caution appears here too but in a more personal way —
  he's explaining why he's publishing an "abstract" rather than the full work.
  Keep the sense of a man who feels pushed into publishing before he's ready.
