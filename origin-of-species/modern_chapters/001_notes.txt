CHAPTER 001 (Introduction) — Translation Notes
=================================================

MAJOR CHANGES AND RATIONALE
----------------------------

1. SENTENCE STRUCTURE: Darwin's longest sentences were broken into shorter ones
   while preserving the logical flow. The opening paragraph, originally two very
   long sentences, was split into five. The roadmap paragraph (chapter outline)
   was reformatted for clarity but kept as one continuous passage, since its
   list-like structure is the point. Semicolons used as period-substitutes were
   converted to actual periods throughout.

2. HEDGING REDUCTION (~30-40%):
   - "I was much struck with certain facts" → "I was deeply struck by certain facts"
     (kept the expression, it's characterful; just modernized preposition)
   - "seemed to throw some light" → "seemed to shed some light" (modern idiom)
   - "something might perhaps be made out" → "I might be able to make some progress"
     (reduced double hedge "might perhaps")
   - "I allowed myself to speculate" → "I let myself begin to speculate" (lighter)
   - "I hope that I may be excused" → "I hope I may be forgiven" (slightly simpler)
   - "No doubt errors may have crept in" → "No doubt errors will have crept in"
     (stronger, matching Darwin's actual certainty)
   - "I can entertain no doubt" → "I have no doubt" (per vocabulary mapping)
   - "I am fully convinced" → "I'm fully convinced" (kept "fully" here — this is
     the book's thesis statement, the emphasis is warranted)
   - Kept "it seemed to me probable" and similar phrasings where they convey
     Darwin's genuine tentativeness about his early thinking

3. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION (per text analysis mappings):
   - "organic beings" → "living things" / "organisms"
   - "hereafter" → "later" / "eventually"
   - "intercrossed" → "crossbred"
   - "coadaptation" → "mutual adaptation"
   - "lineal descendants" → "direct descendants"
   - "quite conceivable" → "entirely possible"
   - "it is preposterous" → "it's absurd" (kept the force, modernized the word)
   - "reposing some confidence" → "place some trust"
   - "want of space" → "lack of space"
   - "very commonly neglected" → kept (still natural English)
   - "geometrical ratio of their increase" → "high rate at which they multiply"
   - "profitable to itself" → "beneficial to itself"
   - "the less improved forms" → "less well-adapted forms"

4. FAMOUS PHRASES PRESERVED:
   - "mystery of mysteries" — kept exactly, with quotation marks as Darwin has it
   - "struggle for existence" — kept exactly (per text analysis: too famous to change)
   - "natural selection" — kept exactly
   - "naturally selected" — kept exactly
   - "divergence of character" — kept exactly
   - "descent with modification" — not present in this chapter but the concept is
   - "survival of the fittest" — not present in this chapter

5. BRIEF IDENTIFIERS ADDED (per text analysis guidance):
   - Wallace: added "Alfred Russel Wallace" on first mention (Darwin just says
     "Mr. Wallace") — full name helps modern readers recognize him
   - Lyell: added "the geologist" before "Sir Charles Lyell"
   - Hooker: added "the botanist" before "Dr. Joseph Hooker"
   - Malthus: no identifier added (his doctrine is explained in context)

6. THE OPENING PASSAGE: This is one of the book's most famous passages ("When
   on board H.M.S. Beagle..."). Kept it very close to the original — just
   broke the sentence structure and modernized "organic beings" to "living
   things." The rhythm and personal quality of this opening are essential to
   the book's identity.

7. THE WOODPECKER/MISTLETOE PASSAGE: This is Darwin at his vivid best. Kept
   the specific natural examples intact and the rising rhetoric ("it is equally
   preposterous" → "it's equally absurd"). These concrete examples are what
   make the abstract argument real.

8. THE THESIS STATEMENT (final paragraph): The last paragraph is Darwin's
   declaration of his theory. Kept it powerful and direct. "I am fully
   convinced that species are not immutable" → "I'm fully convinced that
   species are not unchangeable." Preserved the structure of the three-part
   claim: (1) species are not fixed, (2) related species share common
   ancestors, (3) natural selection is the main mechanism.

9. ENDNOTE REFERENCES: The original contained no superscript endnote numbers
   in this chapter, so nothing to handle.

10. TONE: Warmer and more personal than Chapter 000 (Historical Sketch), as
    the previous chapter notes recommended. Darwin is telling his own story
    here. The conversational quality comes through in phrases like "And I
    wasn't disappointed" and "Or take the mistletoe."

TONE CALIBRATION FOR NEXT CHAPTER:
- Chapter 002 (Variation Under Domestication) is where Darwin really begins
  building his argument. The pigeons are the star of this chapter — Darwin's
  love of detail and his pigeon-breeding expertise should shine through.
- The tone should shift from the personal/autobiographical voice of the
  Introduction toward a more evidence-presenting mode, but still warm and
  conversational. Darwin is showing you things he finds genuinely fascinating.
- This chapter has extensive specific examples (pigeon breeds, dog breeds,
  plant varieties). Keep all examples — the accumulation of evidence IS the
  argument. Make each example vivid and concrete.
- Darwin's own pigeon-breeding experience makes him speak with particular
  authority and enthusiasm here. Let that come through.
- The chapter introduces the key analogy: if humans can cause dramatic changes
  through selective breeding, nature can do the same thing. Make sure this
  argument is crystal clear.
- "Selection" in this chapter means primarily artificial selection (human
  breeding choices). Natural selection has been previewed in the Introduction
  but won't be fully developed until Chapter 005.
