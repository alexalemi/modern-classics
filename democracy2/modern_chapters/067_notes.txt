Translation notes for Chapter 067 (Vol II, Part 2, Ch XII):

- "spiritualisme exalte et presque farouche" -> "exalted, almost fierce spiritualism" -- "farouche" captures wildness/fierceness, not just enthusiasm
- "s'inquiete et s'agite" -> "restless and agitated" -- per vocabulary guide for "inquiete"
- "la Thebaïde" -> "the Thebaid" -- the desert region in Egypt where early Christian monks retreated; kept the proper noun without adding an explanation since Tocqueville doesn't explain it
- "les delices de Rome et la philosophie epicurienne de la Grece" -- Tocqueville's brilliant counter-argument: it wasn't persecution that drove people to spiritual extremes, but materialism. The argument mirrors the whole chapter.
- Short, punchy chapter -- maintained Tocqueville's concise, aphoristic style
- "le sens commun" -> "common sense" -- straightforward translation
- Preserved the logical structure: observation of American religious extremism -> explanation rooted in human nature -> historical parallel -> application to America
