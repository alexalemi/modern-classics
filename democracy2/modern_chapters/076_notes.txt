Translation Notes — Chapter 076 (Volume Four Title Page)

This is the title page for Tome Quatrieme (Volume Four), which contains Part 3 (Democracy's influence on manners/customs) and Part 4 (Democracy's influence on political society) of Volume II.

Key decisions:
- "Cinquieme edition" → "Fifth edition." This is Volume II's separate printing history — the fifth edition of the second volume, versus the twelfth edition of Volume I.
- Kept publisher details as historical facts.
- Omitted the printer's mark (PARIS.--IMPRIMERIE CLAYE ET TAILLEFER) as purely mechanical front matter, consistent with Chapter 000's treatment.
- The Gutenberg production credits at the top are omitted as editorial, not authorial content.

Next chapter notes:
- Chapter 077 opens Part 3 of Volume II — "Democracy's influence on customs and values" (moeurs). This is the famous section where Tocqueville argues that equality softens social behavior. The key Madame de Sevigne passage needs careful handling — translate the quoted letters fully but in a natural voice, preserving their shocking casualness about violence.
