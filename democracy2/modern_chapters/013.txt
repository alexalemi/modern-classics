DEMOCRACY IN AMERICA.


Up to this point, I have examined the institutions; I have surveyed the written laws; I have described the current forms of political society in the United States.

But above all the institutions and beyond all the forms, there resides a sovereign power — that of the people — which destroys them or modifies them at will.

It remains for me to show by what paths this power proceeds — this power that stands above the laws; what its instincts and passions are; what hidden forces drive it, slow it down, or steer it in its irresistible march; what effects its absolute power produces; and what future awaits it.
