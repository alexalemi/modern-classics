Translation notes for Chapter 069 (Vol II, Part 2, Ch XIV):

- "les Tyriens jusqu'aux Florentins et aux Anglais" -> "from the Tyrians to the Florentines to the English" -- Tocqueville's historical sweep preserved
- "minutieux et tracassier" -> "petty and meddlesome" -- these adjectives capture democratic despotism's character perfectly; "tracassier" is stronger than "annoying"
- "lumieres" -> "education" per vocabulary guide (in the passage about taste for pleasures developing faster than "lumieres")
- "interet bien entendu" -> "self-interest" with the "crude notion" qualifier reflecting Tocqueville's point that these citizens misunderstand the doctrine
- "elle est esclave de son bien-etre" -> "a slave to its own comfort" -- one of the chapter's most powerful lines
- "l'homme qui doit l'enchainer peut paraitre" -> "the man who will chain it may appear at any moment" -- added "at any moment" for the ominous force of the French "peut paraitre"
- "moeurs" -> "customs and values" per text analysis
- The passage about Americans alternating between greed and patriotism is brilliantly observed -- kept the rhetorical structure of the back-and-forth
- "Ils aiment ces deux choses l'une par l'autre" -> "They love these two things because of each other" -- simple, direct, exactly right
