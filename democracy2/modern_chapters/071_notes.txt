Translation notes for Chapter 071 (Vol II, Part 2, Ch XVI):

- Very short, philosophical chapter -- almost aphoristic in structure
- "l'ange enseigne a la brute l'art de se satisfaire" -> "the angel teaches the brute the art of satisfaction" -- kept Tocqueville's striking metaphor of the dual nature of man (angel/brute)
- "energies" / "abaisse" / "affaiblit" -> "weakens" / "diminishes" / "brings low" / "enfeebles" -- varied the vocabulary to avoid repetition while preserving the cascading effect
- The chapter's argument is elegant: you need the soul to be great in order for the body to prosper. Pure materialism is therefore self-defeating even on its own terms.
- "comme les brutes" -> "like animals" -- used "animals" rather than "brutes" for the final word since "brutes" was already used in the angel/brute metaphor and repeating it might dilute the impact
- Maintained the tight, compressed quality of this chapter -- Tocqueville is being deliberately concise here, making one surgical argument
