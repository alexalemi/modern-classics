Translation Notes — Chapter 000 (Title Page)

This is the front matter / title page of the twelfth edition (1848). Translation is straightforward.

Key decisions:
- "Membre de l'Institut" → "Member of the Institut de France." Kept the French institutional name since it's a proper noun with no clean English equivalent. The Institut de France is the umbrella body for the French national academies.
- "Douzième édition" → "Twelfth edition." The text analysis mentions the "Avertissement de la dixième édition" (preface to the 10th edition), so this is a later printing that kept the 10th edition preface while adding new material.
- "Examen comparatif de la Démocratie aux États-Unis et en Suisse" → "Comparative Study of Democracy in the United States and in Switzerland." Straightforward translation.
- Publisher details (Pagnerre, address) kept as-is since they are historical facts.
- "PARIS.--IMPRIMERIE CLAYE ET TAILLEFER / RUE SAINT-BENOÎT, 7." — This is the printer's mark, not the publisher. Omitted as it's purely mechanical front matter (the publisher info is retained below).

Next chapter notes:
- Chapter 001 is the Avertissement (preface to the 10th edition), written in 1848 just after the February Revolution overthrew the monarchy. Tocqueville is writing in a moment of vindication — he predicted this. The tone should be confident but measured.
