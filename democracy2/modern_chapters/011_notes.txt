Translation Notes — Chapter 011 (Chapter VII: Political Trials)

This chapter deals with impeachment — what Tocqueville calls "jugement politique." The core argument is that American impeachment is fundamentally administrative (removing someone from office) rather than judicial (punishing a crime), and that this apparent mildness actually makes it a more effective and more dangerous tool.

Key decisions:

- "jugement politique" → "political trial" throughout. "Impeachment" would be the obvious modern American term, but Tocqueville is discussing a broader comparative concept that encompasses English, French, and American versions. "Political trial" preserves this breadth. Where the specifically American process is meant, the context makes it clear.

- "chambre des pairs" → "House of Peers" — standard English term for this institution.

- "haute cour criminelle" → "high criminal court" — direct translation, more natural than "high court of criminal justice."

- "saisi" — kept the concept of the Senate being "engaged" by the Representatives, which captures the legal notion of a court being formally brought into action.

- "déchéance" → "removal" — this specifically means removal from office/status, not a general "downfall."

- "interdiction politique" → "political disqualification" — the specific penalty of being declared unworthy of future office.

- "acte administratif auquel on a donné la solennité d'un arrêt" → "an administrative act dressed in the solemnity of a judicial verdict" — this is the chapter's key formulation and I wanted it crisp.

- "les passions vindicatives des partis" → "the vindictive passions of parties" — kept close to the French; "vindictive" works perfectly in English here.

- The constitutional quotations: Translated directly from Tocqueville's French renderings rather than looking up the English originals. This keeps us faithful to what Tocqueville actually presented to his French readers.

- "une honteuse oisiveté pire que la mort" → "a shameful idleness worse than death" — powerful closing phrase of that paragraph, kept close.

- The final prophetic line about watching for the increase in political trials: classic Tocqueville — he ends chapters with warnings. Kept the weight of it.

- Footnotes 118, 119 included as [Note: ...]. Footnotes 120, 121 included. Gutenberg editorial reference (_N_) omitted.

Consistency notes for next chapters:

- "constitution fédérale" → "federal constitution" consistently.
- "pouvoir constituant" → "constituent power" (the power to make/amend constitutions) — established this term in chapter 010.
