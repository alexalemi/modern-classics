Translation Notes -- Chapter 057 (Vol. II, Part 2, Ch. II: On Individualism in Democratic Countries)

CONTEXT:
This is one of Tocqueville's most important conceptual chapters. He effectively coins the term "individualism" (or at least gives it its modern political meaning) and carefully distinguishes it from mere selfishness. The chapter is a masterpiece of definition-by-contrast.

KEY TRANSLATION DECISIONS:

1. "individualisme" -> "individualism." Tocqueville's own coinage (or at least his popularization of it). Kept in italics on first use, as in the original French, to signal its novelty: "*Individualism* is a recent expression, born of a new idea."

2. "égoïsme" -> "selfishness" (not "egoism"). "Egoism" is too philosophical/technical for Tocqueville's intended contrast. He means the common, recognizable vice. "Selfishness" is the natural modern English equivalent and makes the distinction with "individualism" clearer.

3. "Nos pères" -> "Our fathers." Direct, and it preserves Tocqueville's generational framing.

4. "sentiment réfléchi et paisible" -> "reflective, peaceful feeling." Key to Tocqueville's definition: individualism is calm and deliberate, not passionate. This is what makes it different from selfishness and, in some ways, more dangerous.

5. "la grande société" -> "the larger society." Natural English phrasing.

6. "la trame des temps se rompt" -> "The thread of time is broken." Beautiful image; translated directly.

7. "L'aristocratie avait fait de tous les citoyens une longue chaîne qui remontait du paysan au roi: la démocratie brise la chaîne et met chaque anneau à part." -> "Aristocracy had made all citizens into one long chain reaching from the peasant to the king. Democracy breaks the chain and sets each link apart." One of Tocqueville's most famous images. Translated to preserve the crisp antithesis. "Anneau" = "link" (of a chain), not "ring."

8. "lumières" -> "education" in the passage about self-sufficient individuals. Following text analysis guidelines.

9. Final sentence: "le renfermer enfin tout entier dans la solitude de son propre coeur" -> "lock him up entirely in the solitude of his own heart." Powerful closing image. "Renfermer" = to shut in, lock up. Chose "lock" for its stronger confinement imagery.

TONE:
Coolly analytical throughout. Tocqueville is doing careful conceptual work -- defining terms, drawing distinctions, building toward the devastating final image. The prose is spare and precise, building through parallel contrasts (selfishness vs. individualism, aristocratic vs. democratic).

NEXT CHAPTER NOTES:
- Chapter 058 addresses individualism after a democratic revolution -- Tocqueville argues it's worse immediately after the transition.
- "individualism" and "selfishness" distinction established; maintain these terms.
- The chain metaphor should not be repeated -- it's a one-time image.
- "conditions s'égalisent" -> "social conditions become more equal" (consistent)
