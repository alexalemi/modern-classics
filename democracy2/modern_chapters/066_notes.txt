Translation notes for Chapter 066 (Vol II, Part 2, Ch XI):

- "jouissances materielles" rendered consistently as "material pleasures" throughout
- "inquietude de leurs desirs" -> "restlessness of their own desires" per vocabulary guide
- "corruption eclatante" -> "dazzling corruption" -- preserving Tocqueville's oxymoronic pairing
- "l'art de s'abrutir" -> "the art of degrading themselves" -- captures the paradox of refined self-degradation
- "quelques toises a ses champs" -> "a few acres to your fields" -- modernized the unit of measurement
- "moeurs" -> "customs and values" per text analysis
- The final paragraph's "materialisme honnete" -> "respectable materialism" -- Tocqueville's coinage is devastatingly ironic
- "detendre sans bruit tous leurs ressorts" -> "quietly loosening all their springs" -- mechanical metaphor preserved; this is one of Tocqueville's most powerful images of democratic decline
- Maintained the contrast structure between aristocratic and democratic versions of materialism throughout
