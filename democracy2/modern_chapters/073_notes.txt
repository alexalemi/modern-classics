Translation notes for Chapter 073 (Vol II, Part 2, Ch XVIII):

- "professions honnetes sont reputees honorables" -> "honest professions are considered honorable" -- preserved the wordplay between "honnetes" and "honorables"
- "le prejuge n'est pas contre lui, il est pour lui" -> "the prejudice isn't against it; it's in its favor" -- kept the chiastic reversal
- "s'estimerait mal fame" -> "would consider himself disreputable" -- modernized from the more literal "badly reputed"
- "le travail procurant un lucre" -> "work done for profit" -- "lucre" exists in English but sounds archaic; "profit" is cleaner
- The President/servant parallel: "On le paie pour commander, aussi bien qu'eux pour servir" -> "He's paid to lead, just as they are paid to serve" -- this perfectly democratic observation is one of the chapter's gems
- "Elles ont beau etre jointes au fait, la pensee les separe" -> "The two may be joined in fact, but thought keeps them apart" -- tight, clean rendering of an important conceptual distinction
- Final line: "Toute profession honnete est honorable" -> "Every honest profession is honorable" -- kept as the chapter's crisp conclusion
