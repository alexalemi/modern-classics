Translation notes for Chapter 072 (Vol II, Part 2, Ch XVII):

- Title: "reculer l'objet des actions humaines" -> "push back the horizon of human actions" -- added "horizon" to make the spatial metaphor clearer in English; "push back the object" would be too literal
- "siecles de foi" -> "ages of faith" -- consistent with established translation pattern
- "lumieres de la foi" -> "the light of faith" -- here "lumieres" clearly means "light" in a metaphorical sense, not "education"
- "indifference complete et brutale de l'avenir" -> "complete and brutish indifference to the future"
- "le present grandit; il cache l'avenir qui s'efface" -> "the present looms large; it hides the future, which fades away" -- preserved the visual/spatial metaphor
- "la passion generale du bonheur, qui tourmente" -> "the great general passion for happiness that torments" -- the final "qui tourmente" is a typical Tocqueville sting in the tail
- The final paradox is beautiful: teaching people to think about the worldly future is actually the best way to lead them back toward religious faith. Translated with the circular logic preserved.
- "par un long detour" -> "by a long detour" -- the very last words of the chapter capture Tocqueville's pragmatic, indirect approach to questions of faith
