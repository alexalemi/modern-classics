Translation notes for Chapter 075 (Vol II, Part 2, Ch XX):

This is one of Tocqueville's most prescient chapters -- he essentially predicts the dynamics of industrial capitalism, the gig economy, and tech oligarchy nearly two centuries in advance.

- "un chemin detourne" -> "roundabout path" -- Tocqueville loves this idea of unintended consequences
- "tetes d'epingles" -> "pin heads" -- a deliberate echo of Adam Smith's famous pin factory example from The Wealth of Nations; Tocqueville is directly responding to Smith
- "l'homme se degrade a mesure que l'ouvrier se perfectionne" -> "the man degrades as the worker improves" -- one of the most powerful lines in the book; kept the chiasmus
- "un monstre dans l'ensemble de l'etat social" -> "a monstrosity within the overall social order" -- "monstre" used in its strong sense
- "il y a donc des membres, mais point de corps" -> "There are members, but no body" -- devastating summary of atomized wealth; the rich exist individually but not as a class with shared identity
- "apres avoir appauvri et abruti les hommes dont elle se sert, les livre en temps de crise a la charite publique" -> "after having impoverished and brutalized the men it uses, abandons them in times of crisis to public charity to be fed" -- this reads like it could have been written yesterday about corporate layoffs
- "elles y entreront par cette porte" -> "they will come in through this door" -- the chapter's famous final warning preserved with full force
- Included the endnote (NOTE, PAGE 316) which discusses money-based aristocracies like England -- it's a substantial argument that Tocqueville considered important enough to append
- "l'esprit humain est alors saisi par tous les bouts" -> "The human mind is then seized from every side" -- vivid image of being captured by the profit motive
- "FIN DU TOME TROISIEME" -> "END OF VOLUME THREE" -- structural marker preserved
