Translation Notes — Chapter 053 (Vol. II, Part 1, Ch. XIX)

Title: "Quelques observations sur le théâtre des peuples démocratiques" — "Some Observations on the Theater of Democratic Peoples."

Key decisions:
- "pris au dépourvu par l'impression qu'on lui suggère" — "caught off guard by the impression being suggested to him." Natural English for the French.
- "Le parterre y a souvent fait la loi aux loges" — "The pit has often made the rules for the boxes." Preserved the theatrical metaphor: "parterre" = the standing area (the cheap seats), "loges" = the boxes (the expensive seats). The class dynamics are the point.
- "elles seront renversées par des émeutes" — "they will be overthrown by riots." Striking contrast with the preceding "graduelle et pour ainsi dire légale." Tocqueville loves these sharp pivots.
- "Racine s'excuse fort humblement dans la préface de Britannicus" — kept the anecdote about Racine and Aulus Gellius; it is a vivid illustration and Tocqueville's readers would have known both.
- "un choix dans la nature humaine" — "a selection from human nature." The aristocracy picks which parts of humanity to show.
- "le mélange confus de conditions, de sentiments et d'idées" — "the confused mixture of conditions, feelings, and ideas." "Confused" here means "jumbled together," not "bewildered."
- "Au théâtre, comme ailleurs, elle ne veut rencontrer que de grands seigneurs" — "At the theater, as everywhere else, it wants to encounter only great lords." Straightforward.
- "On ne le renverse point, on l'abandonne" — "It is not overthrown; it is simply abandoned." Killer closing line. Preserved the terse, epigrammatic structure.
- "la muse comique" — "the comic muse." Kept the classical allusion; it's clearly understood.
- "les administrateurs de la commune les permettent" — "the township administrators permit them." Per text analysis, "commune" = "township."

Style note: This chapter is a blend of literary criticism and sociology. Tocqueville moves between theoretical analysis and vivid particular examples (Racine, the Puritans, American censorship of theater). Maintained the essayistic flow.
