Translation notes for Chapter 070 (Vol II, Part 2, Ch XV):

- "recueillement solennel" -> "solemn meditation" -- captures the reflective quality
- "d'etranges discours qui semblent peu faits pour son oreille" -> "strange discourses that seem poorly suited to his ear" -- Tocqueville's gentle irony preserved
- "lumieres" -> "education" in "donnez aux peuples democratiques des lumieres et de la liberte"
- "metempsychose" -> "transmigration of souls" -- more recognizable in English than "metempsychosis"
- The pig passage: "leur ame va passer dans le corps d'un porc" -- translated directly; this is one of Tocqueville's most delightfully irreverent moments in the book
- "j'aimerais mieux enchainer les pretres dans le sanctuaire que de les en laisser sortir" -> "I would rather chain priests inside the sanctuary than let them leave it" -- bold statement preserved with full force
- "le cercle fatal" -> "the fatal circle" -- the vicious cycle between materialism and democratic taste for material pleasures
- This is Tocqueville's longest and most complex chapter in this batch; maintained his elaborate argumentative architecture throughout
- The passage on Socrates and Plato: Tocqueville uses ancient philosophy to argue that spiritualism doesn't need dogmatic certainty to ennoble -- just the conviction that the soul is more than the body
- Final paragraph's "Ce que je vais dire va bien me nuire" -> "What I'm about to say will certainly hurt me" -- Tocqueville's characteristic frankness
