Translation Notes — Chapter 014 (Tome 2, Chapter I: The People Govern)

This is a short, punchy opening chapter that establishes the fundamental reality of American democracy: the people genuinely rule, through direct elections, annual mandates, jury service, and majority governance. It sets up the deeper analysis to come in subsequent chapters.

Key decisions:

- "Comment on peut dire rigoureusement" → "How one can strictly say" — "rigoureusement" here means "in a rigorous/strict sense," not "harshly." Tocqueville is signaling that this is not mere rhetoric — the people really do govern, in the precise meaning of the word.

- "celui qui fait la loi et celui qui l'exécute" → "the one who makes the law and the one who executes it" — kept the parallel structure. Tocqueville is emphasizing that both legislative and executive power derive from the people.

- "lui-même forme le jury" → "the people themselves form the jury" — "lui-même" is emphatic; "themselves" preserves this.

- "directement" and "tous les ans" are italicized in the French — preserved with asterisks as emphasis markers. These are key points: direct election and annual mandates.

- "la direction journalière de la société" → "the day-to-day direction of society" — "journalière" = daily/day-to-day. This is an important idea: popular power isn't just exercised at elections but constantly.

- "s'agitent sans cesse" → "are constantly in motion" — "s'agiter" means to stir, to be restless, to be active. "In motion" captures the ceaseless activity without the negative connotation "agitate" might carry.

- "s'en faire un appui" → "claim their support" — literally "make of them a support." Rendered naturally.

Consistency notes for next chapters:
- The next chapters will deal with parties, press freedom, and political associations. The analytical register deepens.
- "la majorité" → "the majority" — this becomes the central concept as Tocqueville builds toward his famous "tyranny of the majority" argument.
- "paisibles citoyens" → "peaceful citizens" — Tocqueville distinguishes between the moderate mass and the partisan activists. This distinction recurs.
