Translation Notes — Chapter 013 (Tome 2 Front Matter)

This is the brief transitional passage that opens the second part ("Tome") of Volume I. Tocqueville pivots from the institutional analysis (constitutions, courts, federalism) to the analysis of popular sovereignty in action (parties, the press, elections, the majority's power). It is only a few paragraphs but sets the stage for everything that follows.

Key decisions:

- Omitted the publisher/printer information at the top (PARIS.--IMPRIMERIE CLAYE ET TAILLEFER, etc.) and the title page repetition, as these are mechanical elements of the physical book, not Tocqueville's text. Kept the title line "DEMOCRACY IN AMERICA" as a section header.

- "dominateur des lois" → "that stands above the laws" — the French literally says "dominator of the laws" but that is awkward in English. "Stands above" captures the sense that this power is superior to and controls the laws.

- "quels ressorts secrets le poussent, le retardent, ou le dirigent" → "what hidden forces drive it, slow it down, or steer it" — "ressorts secrets" literally "secret springs" (as in clockwork springs). "Hidden forces" is more natural in English while preserving the idea of concealed mechanisms.

- "sa toute-puissance" → "its absolute power" — more natural than "its all-powerfulness" or "its omnipotence."

- "sa marche irrésistible" → "its irresistible march" — kept close; this is a key Tocquevillean idea (democracy as an unstoppable historical force).

Consistency notes for next chapters:
- Chapter 014 begins the concrete analysis of popular governance. The register shifts from this dramatic transitional rhetoric to more analytical observation.
- "le peuple" → "the people" consistently — Tocqueville means the demos, the collective citizenry exercising sovereignty.
