Translation Notes — Chapter 105 (Volume II, Part 4, Chapter II)
"The ideas of democratic peoples about government naturally favor the concentration of power."

This chapter argues that democratic thinking gravitates naturally toward a single central power and uniform legislation. The logic is clean: if everyone is equal, why should different rules apply to different people? And if no one stands out, who else but the state can govern?

Key decisions:
- "pouvoirs secondaires, placés entre le souverain et les sujets" → "intermediate powers, positioned between the sovereign and the subjects" — "secondaires" here means intermediate/mediating, not secondary in importance
- "lumières" in "la naissance, les lumières, les richesses" → "birth, education, and wealth" — in this context lumières means learning/education
- "se plaît à imaginer" → "likes to imagine" — simple and direct
- "la vaste et magnifique image du peuple lui-même" → "the vast and magnificent image of the people itself" — preserved this striking image exactly
- "prendre chaque citoyen par la main et de le conduire" → "take each citizen by the hand and lead them" — kept the paternalistic image
- "L'unité, l'ubiquité, l'omnipotence du pouvoir social, l'uniformité de ses règles" → preserved the four-part list with its building rhythm
- "L'esprit humain poursuit encore ces images quand il rêve" → "The human mind still pursues these images even in its dreams" — a haunting line, kept close to the French
- "[TN-E]" Gutenberg editorial mark omitted per guidelines
