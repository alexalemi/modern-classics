CHAPTER 1 TRANSLATION NOTES

1. MAJOR CHANGES

- Chapter synopsis: Modernized language while preserving the telegraphic style. Changed
  "Question stated" to "The question at hand," etc.

- Opening sentence: Malthus's famous single-sentence opening paragraph is an 80+ word
  construction. I kept it as one sentence but broke it with a colon before the concluding
  clause, and used em dashes for the parenthetical. The French Revolution comet simile is
  preserved intact -- it's too good to alter.

- Vocabulary updates per text analysis: "shew/shewn" -> "show/shown," "colours" -> "colors,"
  "vigour" -> "energy," "labour" -> "labor," "connexion" -> connection (n/a this chapter),
  "encreases" -> "increases." "Speculative philosophers" -> "idealist philosophers."
  "Postulata" -> "basic assumptions" with the famous lead-in preserved: "I think I can
  fairly start with two basic assumptions."

- The passion between the sexes: Rendered as "sexual desire" per the text analysis mapping.
  Malthus's own term is preserved in context on first use where he discusses Godwin's
  conjecture about its extinction.

- The glass-of-wine analogy: Kept the charming, self-deprecating logic intact. Simplified
  "A juster philosophy might teach me" to "A wiser perspective might teach me."

- The ostrich passage: Preserved complete with all the comic details (elongating necks,
  hardening lips, stubs of feathers). Made the humor more accessible by keeping the
  deadpan delivery but using cleaner modern syntax. The punchline about describing their
  "powers of both running and flying" lands better with shorter sentences around it.

- The thesis statement: "Population, when unchecked, grows at a geometric ratio -- that is,
  exponentially. The food supply grows only at an arithmetic ratio -- that is, linearly."
  Added the parenthetical modern equivalents (exponential/linear) per the text analysis.
  This is THE moment of the essay and I kept it punchy and declarative.

- The two postulata: Preserved as a clear, set-apart moment. Used "basic assumptions"
  but kept the formal numbered structure (First... Second...) and the weighty tone.

- Introduced Godwin and Condorcet with their full names and titles on first mention
  ("William Godwin," "the Marquis de Condorcet"), then used surnames only thereafter
  per consistency rules.

- Adam Smith: Kept "Dr. Adam Smith" on first mention (matching the original), will use
  "Adam Smith" in subsequent chapters.

- Robert Wallace: Added "Mr. Robert Wallace" to give slightly more context than just
  "Mr Wallace."

- Nature's generosity passage: The metaphor of nature scattering seeds "with the most
  profuse and liberal hand" is beautiful. Rendered as "with the most generous and lavish
  hand." Kept the progression from plants to animals to humans.

- "Unphilosophical" -> "unscientific" (more accessible to modern readers while preserving
  the meaning -- Malthus means reasoning that doesn't follow evidence).

- British spellings converted to American: "colours" -> "colors," "labour" -> "labor,"
  "vigour" -> "energy" (reworded), "favour" -> "favor" (n/a this chapter).
