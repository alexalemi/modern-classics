CHAPTER 000 (Title Page and Preface) — Translation Notes

MAJOR CHANGES MADE:

1. Title page: Stripped the Gutenberg production credit line. Simplified the
   verbose all-caps title into a cleaner modern format with the main title
   on one line and the subtitle below. Kept the London/1798 line but removed
   the publisher/printer details (J. Johnson, St. Paul's Church-Yard).

2. Third-person to first-person: Converted all of Malthus's "the Author"
   third-person references to "I" throughout the Preface, per the text
   analysis instructions. This affects nearly every sentence and makes the
   Preface feel much more natural and direct to a modern reader.

3. Vocabulary modernizations applied:
   - "avarice and profusion" → "avarice and extravagance" (kept "avarice"
     since it's part of Godwin's essay title reference, modernized "profusion")
   - "candour" → "an open mind"
   - "in elucidation of" → "to support"
   - "no inconsiderable evidence" → "a substantial case" (double negative
     simplified per analysis guidelines)
   - "adduced" → "presented"
   - "actuated solely by" → "driven solely by"
   - "temper very different from" → "spirit very different from"
   - "spleen of disposition" → "gloomy temperament"
   - "jaundiced eye" → "cynical eye"
   - "unpleasing" → "unwelcome"
   - "judgement" → "judgment" (American spelling)

4. Sentence structure: Broke several of Malthus's longer multi-clause
   sentences into shorter ones. The second paragraph was especially dense
   and benefited from splitting. Preserved the logical flow and all
   qualifications.

5. Preserved the date "7 June 1798" at the end, maintaining its indented
   positioning.

6. Kept "Mr. Godwin" and "M. Condorcet" on the title page since these are
   formal title-page references. Per consistency rules, after first full
   introduction they become just "Godwin" and "Condorcet" in subsequent
   chapters.
