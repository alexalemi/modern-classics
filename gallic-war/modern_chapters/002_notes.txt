TRANSLATION NOTES: Book II — The Belgian War
==============================================

1. MAJOR CHANGES AND RATIONALE
-------------------------------

A. Sentence structure overhaul:
The McDevitte/Bohn translation renders Caesar's famously direct Latin as Victorian
nested clauses. Nearly every paragraph required breaking long compound-complex
sentences into shorter, punchier ones. Example: the opening paragraph was a single
sentence of 150+ words with multiple semicolons and parentheticals; it has been
broken into roughly eight sentences while preserving all content.

B. Active voice restoration:
Systematic conversion of passive constructions to active voice throughout.
"He was informed by letters from Labienus" → "Labienus confirmed..."
"It was reported to him" → "scouts reported" or "word arrived"
"A thanksgiving of fifteen days was decreed" → "the Senate voted a public thanksgiving"

C. Victorian archaisms replaced per text analysis vocabulary mappings:
- "inasmuch as" / "forasmuch as" → "since" / "because"
- "by reason of" → "because of" / "due to"
- "betook themselves" → "fled" / "retreated" / "ran"
- "succour" → "reinforcements" / "help"
- "corn" → "grain" / "wheat"
- "levy" → "raised" (troops)
- "despatch" → "speed" / "urgency"
- "ensigns" → "standards"
- "vineae" → "siege shelters"
- "testudo" → "tortoise formation" (with brief explanation on first use)
- "forage" retained but contextualized

D. Time references converted:
- "second watch" → "roughly ten in the evening"
- "immediately after midnight" → kept as "after midnight" (clear enough)
- "third watch" → "around midnight"

E. Geographic/cultural context added on first mention:
- Belgae: "who occupied the northern third of Gaul (roughly modern Belgium and northern France)"
- Cisalpine Gaul: "the Roman-controlled part of northern Italy"
- Remi: "the Belgian tribe closest to Celtic Gaul (based around modern Reims)"
- Maritime tribes: "peoples of what is now Brittany and the western French seaboard"
- Cimbri/Teutones: "those terrifying Germanic hordes that had overrun the rest of Gaul"

F. Key speeches converted to direct quotes:
- Aduatuci's mockery of Roman stature on the walls
- Aduatuci's surrender terms
- Caesar's reply to the Aduatuci
- Divitiacus's plea for the Bellovaci
- Caesar's brief speech to the tenth legion (kept short as in original)
- Nervii intelligence report on their character

G. The Nervii battle narrative (sections 19-28):
This is one of the most famous battle narratives in ancient literature. Particular
care was taken to:
- Build tension through the approach and the spies' intelligence
- Make the ambush sudden and visceral ("smashed through," "screaming over the hill")
- Render Caesar's crisis moment with cinematic immediacy
- Preserve the Nervii's extraordinary courage in the final stand
- Keep the emotional climax of wounded soldiers propping themselves on shields
- Honor the famous "fighting from piles of their own dead" passage

H. The enslavement of the Aduatuci (53,000 people):
Translated matter-of-factly per the text analysis guidelines. Caesar reports this
without commentary; the translation preserves that clinical tone. The number speaks
for itself.

2. KEY DECISIONS
-----------------

- "Caesar" maintained in third person throughout, never replaced with "he" where
  ambiguous
- Legion numbers spelled out: "the tenth legion," "the twelfth legion"
- Tribal names kept in Latin form: Nervii, Belgae, Suessiones, etc.
- Two Divitiacuses: noted explicitly that the Suessiones king "not the Aeduan of
  the same name" to prevent confusion
- The Remi's troop count report formatted as flowing prose rather than a bare list,
  to maintain narrative momentum while preserving all numbers
- Preserved Caesar's self-serving framing without editorial commentary (e.g., his
  "mercy" toward the Nervii, his "policy" of sparing the surrendered)
- The 15-day thanksgiving: added "No commander in Roman history had ever been granted
  such an honor" — this is historically accurate and captures what Caesar is implying
  but doesn't state outright

3. NOTES FOR NEXT CHAPTER TRANSLATOR (BOOK III)
-------------------------------------------------

Consistency points to maintain:
- "Cisalpine Gaul" for Gallia Cisalpina (not "Hither Gaul")
- "the Province" (capitalized) for Transalpine Gaul
- Labienus established as "his most trusted lieutenant"
- Publius Crassus introduced here with single legion on Atlantic coast — he features
  prominently in Book III
- "siege shelters" for vineae
- "tortoise formation" for testudo
- Time conversions: maintain the approximate modern equivalents
- The Veneti and maritime tribes are introduced at the very end of Book II — Book III
  opens with their revolt, so the translator should not re-introduce them as if new
- The Aduatuci are dealt with here; any future references should note they were
  conquered in Book II
- Caesar's "mercy" framing should continue to be rendered straight-faced
- Maintain the pattern of brief geographic context on first mention of new tribes
- The Nervii remnant (500 surviving warriors) may be referenced again
- Treviri cavalry's premature report of Roman defeat — this is a nice detail that
  might echo later
- Book III has multiple theaters (Veneti naval war, Alps, Aquitania) — maintain
  the same clear orientation at the start of each section
