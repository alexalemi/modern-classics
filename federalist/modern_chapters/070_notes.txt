HAMILTON'S VOICE IN THE EXECUTIVE PAPERS:
- Papers 67-70 are Hamilton's progressive case for the executive:
  67 = expose opponents' lies about the presidency
  68 = defend the election method (Electoral College)
  69 = factual comparison showing president ≠ king
  70 = philosophical case for a strong, unified executive
- Paper 70's argument is the heart of his vision: energy + unity + accountability
- From 71 onward, Hamilton continues discussing duration, financial support, and specific powers

KEY ARGUMENTS TO TRACK:
- The "energy in the executive" framework (unity, duration, adequate support, competent powers)
- The "safety in the republican sense" framework (dependence on the people, accountability)
- The anti-council argument (accountability destroyed by diffusion)
- The Rome examples (consuls, Decemvirs) as cautionary tales
- The England comparison (council makes sense for unaccountable king; destroys accountability for a president)
