1. HAMILTON'S VOICE: Preserved his energetic, combative, lawyerly style. This paper is heavily legalistic — Hamilton is a lawyer making a legal case — so the combative energy is channeled into precise argumentation rather than rhetorical fireworks. His confidence and dismissiveness toward opponents ("false reasoning upon misconceived fact," "a phantom") are maintained.

2. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATIONS:

   - "eligible" → "desirable" / "promising"
   - "pestilential breath of faction" → "poisonous influence of partisanship"
   - "cognizance" → "jurisdiction" or "cases" depending on context
   - "candid mind" → "fair-minded person"
   - "despatch" → "speed and efficiency"
   - "appellate" — kept as a legal term but context makes it clear
   - "hitherto" → "so far"
   - "color to pretend" → "basis for claiming"
   - "supersedure" → "elimination"
   - "amenable to" → "sued by"

3. SENTENCE STRUCTURE: Broke Hamilton's extremely long, heavily subordinated legal sentences into clearer modern constructions while preserving the logical chain. The paragraph on why legislators shouldn't serve as judges (originally two enormous sentences) was broken into many shorter, punchier ones.

4. LEGAL CONCEPTS: Kept legal terminology (appellate jurisdiction, writ of error, common law, civil law, original jurisdiction) since Hamilton's audience understood these and modern readers need them too, but provided enough context to follow the argument.

5. SOVEREIGN IMMUNITY PASSAGE: Hamilton's argument that states can't be sued — later overturned by Chisholm v. Georgia and then restored by the 11th Amendment — is translated faithfully without editorial comment.
