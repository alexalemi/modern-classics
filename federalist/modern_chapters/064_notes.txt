   - "discover" (reveal sense) -> not present here; "discovered less prudence" = "showed good judgment" (rephrased)

2. SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
   - Jay's flowing but sometimes long sentences broken into clearer constructions
   - Preserved his narrative, building style
   - The "tides in human affairs" passage kept close to original for its famous quality
   - Treaty objections reorganized into clearer point-counterpoint format

3. TONE PRESERVATION:
   - Maintained Jay's warm, diplomatic, reassuring voice throughout
   - Less combative than Hamilton, more measured than Madison
   - Kept his appeals to common sense and practical wisdom
   - Preserved his characteristic "stepping back to see the big picture" approach
   - Maintained his gentle dismissal of opponents (vs. Hamilton's aggressive demolition)
   - The corruption passage kept Jay's tone of mild incredulity rather than outrage

4. FAMOUS PASSAGES:
   - "There are tides in them; tides very irregular in their duration..." -- preserved closely, recognizing the echo of Shakespeare's "tide in the affairs of men" (Julius Caesar)
   - "As in the field, so in the cabinet" -- preserved as a memorable formulation
   - The treaty-as-bargain argument preserved in its logical clarity

5. STRUCTURAL DECISIONS:
   - ALL CAPS (SECRECY, DESPATCH, SUPREME, ABSOLUTELY, etc.) converted to plain text
   - The quoted constitutional text retained but lowered from ALL CAPS
   - Retained paper header, author, salutation, and PUBLIUS signature
   - Jay's flowing argumentative structure preserved: qualifications of treaty-makers -> need for continuity -> secrecy and speed -> answering objections
