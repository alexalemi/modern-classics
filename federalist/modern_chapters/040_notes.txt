Major changes and rationale for Federalist No. 40:

1. MADISON'S LEGAL AND EMOTIONAL RANGE:
   Paper 40 shows Madison at his most versatile. It begins with careful legal analysis of the Convention's mandate (parsing the Annapolis and Congressional resolutions like a constitutional lawyer), then builds to an emotional defense of the Convention's moral authority. The translation preserves both registers -- the precise legal reasoning and the passionate civic appeal.

2. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION:
   - "commissions" -> "formal commissions" (clarified)
   - "constituents" -> "states" (in this context, the states that appointed delegates)
   - "exigencies" -> "demands" (repeated term in the authorizing documents)
   - "recommendatory act" -> "resolution" (simplified)
   - "evinced" -> "shown" (archaic -> modern)
   - "mean" (noun) -> "means" (archaic singular -> modern)
   - "scrupulous expositors" -> "strictest interpreters"
   - "inveterate objectors" -> "most determined opponents"
   - "pretermitting" -> "setting aside" (legal archaism -> plain English)
   - "purview" -> "off limits" (rephrased for clarity)
   - "tenor of their commission" -> "terms of its commission"
   - "perverseness" -> "stubbornness"
   - "animadversion" -> "criticism" (archaic -> modern)
   - "commensurate to the national exigencies" -> "fully equal to the nation's needs"
   - "approbation" -> "approval" (per conventions)
   - "antecedent" -> "prior" (simplified)
   - "cavil" -> "find fault"
   - "alacrity" -> "eagerly" (rephrased)
   - "ignoble" -> "shameful"

3. LEGAL DOCUMENT QUOTATIONS:
   - The Annapolis and Congressional resolutions are preserved in full, as they're the documentary foundation of Madison's argument
   - The emphasized phrases (ADEQUATE TO THE EXIGENCIES, etc.) are retained with their force but rendered in clearer modern English
   - Madison's systematic four-point extraction from these documents preserved as a numbered list

4. THE "FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES" ARGUMENT:
   - Madison's devastating series of rhetorical questions ("Do they require...") is one of his finest argumentative sequences
   - Each question shows that the supposed "fundamental principles" of the Confederation are actually present in the new Constitution
   - The cumulative effect preserved -- each answer undermines the objection further
   - The specific examples (piracy, post office, courts-martial, etc.) retained as they demonstrate Madison's command of detail

5. THE EMOTIONAL CRESCENDO:
   - The passage beginning "They must have reflected that in all great changes..." builds to genuine passion
   - Madison invokes the Declaration of Independence
   - The rhetorical question about what judgment would have been passed on a timid Convention is powerful
   - The closing argument (good advice should be accepted regardless of source) preserved
   - Final summary paragraph tightened for modern impact

6. STRUCTURAL DECISIONS:
   - The three-part structure (authority from documents, departure on ratification, moral duty) clearly maintained
   - Long quoted passages from authorizing documents preserved
   - Both footnotes converted to [1] and [2] format
   - Declaration of Independence reference highlighted via footnote
   - "PUBLIUS" signature retained
