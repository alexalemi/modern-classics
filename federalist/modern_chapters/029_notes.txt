5. The satirical catalog of absurd anti-Constitution claims (militias marching across the country, debts paid in militiamen) was preserved in full as it showcases Hamilton's wit and contempt for his opponents' arguments.

6. "Posse comitatus" was kept as a term but given an inline explanation, since the concept has continued historical and legal significance.

7. The constitutional quotation about militia powers was preserved nearly verbatim but with normalized capitalization.
