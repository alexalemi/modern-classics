   - The final summary paragraph with its cascading "The one... the other..." comparisons was preserved for rhetorical effect, as it's one of Hamilton's most powerful passages
   - Long legal/constitutional analysis sentences broken into clearer units

   - This paper is Hamilton at his most systematic and lawyerly -- preserved the calm, devastating tone
   - The opening sarcasm (comparing the president to the Ottoman Sultan and the Pope alongside the British king) preserved with full force
   - The climactic final paragraph builds through comparison after comparison to the triumphant rhetorical question -- this momentum preserved

   - Preserved Hamilton's four-point comparison framework (First through Fourth)

   - The long final comparison paragraph kept as a single block for rhetorical impact

   - Blackstone and legal references kept with brief context
   - The closing rhetorical question ("What answer shall we give...") preserved as Hamilton's triumphant conclusion

5. CONTENT NOTES:
   - The pardon power analysis (Third point) is remarkably detailed and prescient -- translated with full complexity preserved

   - The New York governor comparison is Hamilton's favorite rhetorical device in these papers -- consistently translated
