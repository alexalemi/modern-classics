Major changes and rationale for Federalist No. 46:

1. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION:
   - "predilection" -> "loyalty" (clearer for modern readers)
   - "notwithstanding" -> "despite" (per conventions)
   - "emoluments" -> "salaries" (per conventions)
   - "candid acknowledgments" -> "honest admissions" (18th-c. sense of candor)
   - "prepossessions" -> "loyalties" / "preferences"
   - "defalcations" -> "power grabs" / "expansion at expense of"
   - "chimerical" -> "far-fetched" / "imaginary"
   - "disquietude" -> "unrest"
   - "repugnance" -> "resistance"
   - "executive magistracy" -> "governor"
   - "supposition" -> "idea" / "assumption" / "scenario"
   - "delirious jealousy" -> "paranoid suspicion" (18th-c. jealousy = suspicion)
   - "visionary" (negative sense) -> "far-fetched"
   - "address" (skill sense) -> "skill"

2. SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
   - Broke many extremely long periodic sentences into shorter constructions
   - The paragraph on militia vs. standing army had sentences spanning 5-6 clauses; broken into digestible pieces
   - Madison's "that... that... that..." cascading construction preserved in the "traitors" paragraph but made clearer
   - Untangled inverted constructions throughout

3. TONE PRESERVATION:
   - Maintained Madison's systematic, logical progression
   - Preserved his structured argumentation (first this angle, then another angle)
   - Kept his careful hedging ("even if we grant," "even then")
   - Preserved the passionate crescendo about armed citizens vs. European tyrannies
   - Maintained the neat logical summary at the end (either/or construction)

4. STRUCTURAL DECISIONS:
   - ALL CAPS removed throughout; emphasis carried by word choice
   - Retained paper number, title, publication info, author, salutation
   - Retained "PUBLIUS" signature
   - Preserved paragraph structure largely intact
