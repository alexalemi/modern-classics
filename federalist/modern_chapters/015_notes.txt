   - "imperium in imperio" -> kept with inline translation ("a government within a government")

2. SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
   - Broke Hamilton's extremely long opening sentences into more manageable units
   - The famous litany of rhetorical questions ("Do we have commitments...? Do we owe debts...?") preserved in full with the question-answer structure intact -- this is one of Hamilton's most powerful rhetorical devices
   - The philosophical argument about government, law, and enforcement was preserved in its logical chain but broken into clearer steps
   - The closing metaphor of the "tottering edifice" kept for its vivid imagery

3. TONE PRESERVATION:
   - Restored Hamilton's combative, dramatic voice after Madison's measured tone in Paper 14
   - Kept his aggressive confidence in diagnosing the Confederation's failures
   - Preserved the emotional intensity of passages like "let us make a firm stand" and "break the deadly spell"
   - Maintained his contempt for the opponents who "cling with blind devotion" to contradictory principles
   - Preserved the building sense of crisis throughout

4. STRUCTURAL DECISIONS:
   - Retained all standard elements (paper number, title, author, salutation, PUBLIUS)
   - Footnote converted from "(1)" to "[1]" -- the original footnote "I mean for the Union" is Hamilton clarifying he means the Union has no troops/treasury/government
   - ALL CAPS (LEGISLATION, STATES, GOVERNMENTS, CORPORATE, COLLECTIVE CAPACITIES, INDIVIDUALS, COERCION, DISCRETIONARY SUPERINTENDENCE) all converted to plain text
   - Preserved the two-part structure: diagnosis (the Confederation's failures) and analysis (the root cause -- legislating for states not individuals)

5. KEY PASSAGES:
   - The rhetorical question litany ("Are there engagements... Do we owe debts... Have we valuable territories...") is the emotional centerpiece of the paper -- preserved with its devastating question-answer rhythm
   - "Why has government been instituted at all? Because the passions of men will not conform to the dictates of reason and justice, without constraint." -- preserved nearly verbatim as a fundamental statement
   - The closing image of the "frail and tottering edifice" -> "frail and crumbling structure" updated slightly while preserving the metaphor
