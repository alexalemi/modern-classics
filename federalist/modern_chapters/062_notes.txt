Major changes and rationale for Federalist No. 62:

1. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION:
   - "dilate on" -> "dwell on" (more natural)
   - "pretensions" -> "claims" (18th-c. sense = legitimate claims)
   - "consonant" -> "agreeable"

   - "salutary" -> "healthy" / "beneficial"
   - "sinister combinations" -> "corrupt alliances"

   - "acquaintance with the objects" -> "expertise in"
   - "pecuniary" -> financial (not used here but noted)
   - "flattering hopes" -> "hopes" (simplified)
   - "sagacious, enterprising, and moneyed" -> "shrewd, enterprising, and wealthy"
   - "voluminous codes" -> "bloated legal codes"
   - "mutability" -> "constant turnover" / "instability"
   - "mutable government" -> "unstable government"

2. SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
   - Madison's long, architecturally balanced sentences broken into shorter units while preserving logical chains
   - Kept his numbered framework (I-V and First through Fourth) as it's characteristic of his style
   - Preserved his parallel constructions where they serve the argument
   - The famous passage on unstable law ("if the laws be so voluminous...") kept close to original for its rhetorical power

3. TONE PRESERVATION:
   - Maintained Madison's systematic, philosophical voice
   - Kept his careful qualifications and balanced acknowledgments of counter-arguments
   - Preserved his characteristic move of defining a problem, then showing how the Senate solves it
   - Maintained the building intensity of the "mutable government" section at the end
   - Kept the memorable closing formulation about respectability

4. STRUCTURAL DECISIONS:
   - Preserved Madison's I-V and First-Fourth organizational framework
   - ALL CAPS (PROPORTIONAL, EQUAL, FEW, MANY) converted to plain text
   - Kept the quoted phrase about "spirit of amity" as it references Convention language
   - Retained paper header, author, salutation, and PUBLIUS signature

5. NOTABLE PASSAGES:
   - "Law is defined to be a rule of action; but how can that be a rule, which is little known, and less fixed?" -- preserved closely as a powerful formulation
   - "laws are made for the FEW, not for the MANY" -- preserved in substance, lowered from caps
   - The concluding passage about respectability preserved closely
