Major changes and rationale for Federalist No. 58:

1. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION:
   - "augmented" -> "increased" (title kept for fidelity, modernized in text)
   - "jealousy which discolors and disfigures" -> "suspicion that distorts and disfigures" (per vocabulary mappings, preserved the vivid imagery)
   - "unequivocal objects" -> "clear purposes"
   - "resolvable into a mere directory provision" -> "amounts to nothing more than a general recommendation"
   - "strenuous advocates" -> "strong advocates"
   - "pretensions" -> "claims"
   - "expedient too obvious" -> "strategy too obvious"
   - "sensible to every prospect" -> "sensitive to any threat"
   - "secessions" -> "walkouts" (the 18th-c. practice of legislators walking out to deny quorum)
   - "baneful" -> "destructive"
   - "convulsions" -> "upheaval"
   - "infirmities incident to" -> "weaknesses that come with"
   - "the dupe of cunning" -> "the tool of cunning"
   - "sceptre" -> "scepter" (modernized spelling, preserved metaphor)

2. SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
   - Broke many long compound-complex sentences into shorter ones
   - Preserved Madison's numbered framework (1, 2, 3) in the opening section
   - Maintained the logical progression from constitutional provisions to practical incentives to the ultimate weapon (power of the purse)
   - Preserved the important passage about larger assemblies being more oligarchic, maintaining its paradoxical force

3. TONE PRESERVATION:
   - Maintained Madison's systematic, precise, philosophical voice
   - Preserved his careful building of multiple layers of argument
   - Kept his habit of acknowledging the opposing argument before dismantling it
   - Preserved the power of his observation about assembly size and oligarchy -- one of Madison's most incisive insights

4. STRUCTURAL DECISIONS:
   - Maintained numbered points (1, 2, 3) as in original
   - Preserved the structural pivot from the main argument to the quorum discussion
   - Kept the British Parliament analogy (power of the purse)
   - The famous passage about large assemblies being more oligarchic is preserved with maximum clarity

5. FAMOUS PASSAGES:
   - "The countenance of the government may become more democratic, but the soul that animates it will be more oligarchic" -- preserved closely as a memorable Madison formulation
   - "Ignorance will be the dupe of cunning, and passion the slave of sophistry" -- modernized to "Ignorance will be the tool of cunning, and passion will be the slave of clever rhetoric"
