From Caesar Parts 2–6 (files 001–005) — Caesar's life COMPLETE:
- Locked lines spent: "The die is cast." (framed "Speaking the words

  "Yes... they have come — but they have not gone."
- Furlongs → miles; myriads → plain figures. "quaestor" gloss spent

- Tribunes' flight (Antony retells): driven out "with insults", fled
  "in a hired carriage, dressed as slaves"; Antony's motion "that

- Cato threads (Cato's life must match): prison march in silence;
  "shipped off to Cyprus"; hand-over-to-the-barbarians motion; wept
  at the thousand dead ("Agamemnon, King of Kings"); Caesar at Utica:
  "Cato, I grudge you your death, as you grudged me the honor of

  "Anti-Cato".
- Lupercalia (Antony/Brutus retell): the diadem "wreathed with

- NO "You too, my child?" — Plutarch's Caesar has Caesar cover his

- Names: Vercingetorix, Ambiorix, Aedui, Labienus, Apollodorus the

From Cato Parts 2–6 + Cicero Part 1 (files 007–012) — Cato COMPLETE:
- Catiline debate (Cicero Parts 2–3 and Brutus must echo): Caesar's
  motion "held instead under guard in whatever Italian towns Cicero
  himself might choose"; Cato "pointed the finger of suspicion at
  Caesar himself"; Silanus' walk-back (imprisonment "was the utmost a
  Roman could suffer"); the shorthand-writers detail; note-passing:
  "Keep it, you drunkard." (Servilia = Brutus' mother — Brutus' life
  may allude.)
- Murena trial (Cicero's life retells): Cicero's mockery answered
  "What a pleasant consul we have, my friends."
- Cato refusal of Pompey: "Cato cannot be captured by way of the
  women's rooms."; Cicero's oath argument: "though Cato may have no
  need of Rome, Rome has need of Cato."
- Sole consulship: "better to set up a more legal sort of monarchy
  themselves". Pompey's fake thunder. "the laws ought not to look to
  Pompey for protection — Pompey should look for protection to the
  laws."
- Cato's death (echo if reported): "The sword is now my own."; Utica's
  cry "the only free man, the only man undefeated"; Caesar's grudge
  line per ledger. Young Cato at Philippi: "scorned to run... fell at
  last, leaving his enemies in awe of his courage"; Portia "ended her
  life in a manner worthy of her birth and her virtue"; Philippi
  opponents styled "Octavian and Antony".
- Corcyra (Cicero's life retells): Cato "plainly saved Cicero's life"
  from young Pompey's officers.
- NOTE the source-faithful divergence: prison march silent in Caesar's
  life, Cato still speaking in Cato's — both stand; do not "fix".
- Cicero Part 1: Molon's "Greece has my pity" speech; "So where have
  you been all this time, Cicero?"; Vatinius "Ah, but I don't have
  your neck."; Verres quips; Catiline "a bold, daring, restless
  character"; pact "by sacrificing a man and tasting his flesh".
  Cicero's vanity with light irony; his anxiety established.
- Glosses spent: Deiotarus ("the Galatian king"), fuller, Marsian war,
  verres/boar, Phaedo ("Plato's dialogue on the soul"), the Psylli.
- Seam: Cicero Part 2 opens with the conspiracy ripening toward the
  consular election — no re-introduction.
