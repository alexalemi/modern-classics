Chapter 000 (Preface) - Translation Notes

TONE AND APPROACH:
- The Preface sets the combative, confident tone for the whole work. Locke is
  essentially saying: Filmer's arguments are nonsense, the nation has already
  proven me right by overthrowing James II, and here's my case.
- Preserved Locke's swagger and directness. He's not hedging -- he's throwing
  down the gauntlet.
- Kept the famous phrase "never so much glib nonsense put together in
  well-sounding English" nearly verbatim as the text analysis flagged it.

STRUCTURAL DECISIONS:
- Broke Locke's enormous opening sentence (which runs the entire first
  paragraph of the original) into multiple sentences while preserving the
  logical flow with connectives.
- The original is essentially one massive paragraph followed by two numbered
  points and a closing note. I broke the first long paragraph into several
  shorter ones at natural topic shifts to improve readability.
- Preserved the "First... Second..." structure for the two conditions Locke
  sets for any would-be critic.

VOCABULARY CHOICES:
- "Thou hast" -> "What you have" (standard modernization per text analysis)
- "wanting part" -> "missing sections" (archaic "wanting" = lacking)
- "pretends wholly to build on" -> "claims to build everything on" (archaic
  "pretends" = claims/asserts)
- "want of scripture-proofs" -> "complete lack of scriptural proof" (archaic
  "want" = lack)
- "advertise the reader" -> "One last thing" (archaic "advertise" = inform/notify)
- "viz." -> "namely" (per text analysis consistency rules)
- "drum ecclesiastic" kept in quotes as it's a literary reference (from
  Samuel Butler's Hudibras) that Locke himself puts in quotation marks
- "cavilling" -> "picking at" / "nitpicking" (modernized but kept the
  dismissive tone)
- "railing" -> "insults" (clear modern equivalent)
- "conscientiously scrupulous" -> "honest, well-grounded concerns in good faith"
  (unpacked the compressed phrase)

KEY REFERENCES PRESERVED:
- Sir Robert Filmer identified by full name on first mention, then "Sir Robert"
  as Locke does
- The abbreviation key (A. = our author, O. = Observations) preserved as
  reference apparatus needed for Book I
- Patriarcha, 1680 edition citation kept
- "drum ecclesiastic" kept with quotes (Butler's Hudibras reference)
- King William and the Glorious Revolution context clear

NOTES FOR NEXT CHAPTER (001 - Book I, Chapter 1):
- Locke will begin his systematic demolition of Filmer's Patriarcha
- "Our author" / "A." has been established as referring to Filmer
- "O." has been established as Filmer's Observations on Hobbes, Milton, etc.
- The combative, confident tone established here should carry into Book I,
  which is prosecutorial and often exasperated
