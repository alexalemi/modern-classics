LOCKED VOCABULARY (no re-glossing)
- commonwealth (= the state; glossed in Introduction), sovereign/sovereignty,
  covenant, artificial man, body politic, LEVIATHAN.
- fancy = Hobbes' technical word for the appearance produced by sense
  (ch. 1); from ch. 2 on, use "imagination" (= decaying sense). MEMORY,
  EXPERIENCE, simple vs. compound imagination, DREAMS, UNDERSTANDING
  (= imagination raised by words) all defined in ch. 2.
- MENTAL DISCOURSE ("a conversation the mind has with itself"), train of
  thoughts, REMEMBRANCE, PRUDENCE (companion word: foresight; best prophet =
  best guesser), SIGN (prior event as evidence) — ch. 3.
- SPEECH, MARKS, SIGNS, proper/common/universal names, TRUE/FALSE
  ("attributes of speech, not of things"), ERROR, DEFINITIONS, abstract
  names, INCONSTANT signification — ch. 4.
- "Reckoning" = Hobbes' bridge word for calculation/reasoning; ch. 5 defines
  REASON as reckoning. Keep "reckoning," never replace with plain "thinking."
- "Absurdity"/"absurd" = technical term for meaningless or contradictory
  speech ("incorporeal substance," "round quadrangle"). Keep it technical.
- "Insignificant speech" = locked anti-Scholastic jab, glossed once (ch. 1)
  as "words that signify nothing."
- "Schooles": "the Scholastics" for the philosophers/doctrine, "the
  universities" for the institutions. Scholastic jargon Hobbes mocks goes in
  scare quotes ("species," "quiddity," "in-poured virtue").
- "Ghostly men" → keep pun with "— the clergy" gloss (done once in ch. 2).
- "Romants" → romance novels; "rude people" → uneducated people; "the
  Gentiles" → the pagans of the past (in religion contexts).
- Latin tags: keep with em-dash gloss ("Respice finem — 'look to the end'").
- "Seven wise men" → the Seven Sages of Greece.

TONE CALIBRATION
- Definitional passages contraction-free; conversational asides carry the
  storyteller energy ("Watch the water," "Take the famous case of...").
- Direct second-person address ("Press, rub, or strike your eye...") is the
  ceiling for Part I technical chapters.
- One-word punchline sentences ("Absurd.") used sparingly.
- Anti-Scholastic bite stays dry, not sneering; sharpest sarcasm reserved
  for Parts III-IV where Hobbes himself escalates.
- Religious references: light touch ("the One who searches hearts").
- Period terms for disability etc. rendered in measured modern phrasing
  ("a born fool," "entirely deaf and mute") without losing the argument.
