TEXT ANALYSIS — Characters (Theophrastus, c. 319 BC)
Source: Bennett & Hammond's translation (1902), Gutenberg #58242;
their introduction and footnotes stripped in prep. 30 sketches + the
Epistle Dedicatory (kept, though probably a later addition — it has
been read with the book for centuries).

WHAT THIS IS

Aristotle's successor at the Lyceum wrote thirty short comic
portraits of Athenian personality defects — the Flatterer, the Bore,
the Newsmonger, the Penny-pincher, the man who can't stop talking.
Each sketch: a one-line definition, then a torrent of concrete
behaviors ("He is the sort of man who..."). It is the ancestor of
every comic character piece since — La Bruyère translated it and
built his career on it. Still laugh-out-loud funny when the examples
land in modern terms.

STYLE AND TONE

- Each sketch = definition + "He's the kind of man who..." catalogue.
  KEEP THAT ENGINE: the comedy is accumulation. Punchy, specific,
  present-tense.
- Modernize the register fully — these should read like brilliant
  contemporary comic writing — but keep the EXAMPLES ancient: the
  marketplace, the baths, sacrifices, symposia, lawsuits stay Greek
  (light appositive gloss where needed). The joke is that the man is
  timeless while his world is ancient; don't transplant him to an
  office.
- Sketch titles: modern, sharp, faithful — the ledger will lock each
  title at first rendering (the voice agent proposes the full list of
  30 for consistency; e.g. "The Dissembler" may become "The Phony" if
  the body supports it — titles are the translator's strongest tool
  here, choose boldly but accurately).
- The definitions open each sketch with the same formula shape:
  "X is, in essence, ... and the X-ish man is the sort who..." —
  keep a consistent formula across all 30.

SENSITIVE CONTENT

- Slavery, superstition rituals, and period misogyny appear inside
  the jokes — render plainly as the ancient world per project policy;
  the Superstitious Man's rituals are the joke's substance, keep them
  exact.

FORMAT

3 files of 10 sketches; heading "Characters N–M" exactly; the
dedication ("Theophrastus to Polycles") opens file 000 as a
subheading section; each sketch as a standalone subheading line
"N. Title" then the text. Ratio target 0.85–1.15.
