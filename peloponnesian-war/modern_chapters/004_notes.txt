=== CONSISTENCY NOTES FOR NEXT CHAPTER ===

1. ALLIANCE TERMINOLOGY: The Delian League has been established in this chapter. It started as a voluntary alliance with congresses at Delos, then gradually became an Athenian empire. Refer to it as "the alliance" or "the league" — not "confederacy." "Tribute" is established as the standard term for allied financial contributions.

2. SPARTAN-ATHENIAN RELATIONS: By the end of this chapter, the Thirty Years' Peace is in effect (made after the Euboean revolt). Key concessions: Athens gave up Nisaea, Pegae, Troezen, and Achaia. This truce is what will eventually break down to start the Peloponnesian War.

3. SAMOS: The Samian revolt and siege is the last major event before the war. Samos surrendered and was forced to pay war costs in installments. Byzantium also submitted. The Athenian commanders at the siege included Thucydides (possibly the historian himself — this is debated), Hagnon, and Phormio — all of whom will appear later.

4. PERICLES: Has been introduced with patronymic ("son of Xanthippus") in this chapter. He appears as military commander at Sicyon, during the Euboean crisis, and commanding the Samos campaign. He is clearly Athens's dominant leader by chapter's end. Future chapters should use just "Pericles" — no patronymic needed.

5. CIMON: Introduced as "son of Miltiades." Major commander in the first half of this chapter (Eion, Eurymedon). Dies at the siege of Kitium in Cyprus. Already fully handled.

6. SPARTAN KINGS: Pleistoanax appears twice — first as a minor (Nicomedes commanding for him), then as king leading the invasion of Attica. His father Pausanias (the regent, not the earlier commander) is mentioned in passing.

7. TONE: This chapter is primarily analytical/narrative prose — the "rise of empire" story. It should feel like a clear-eyed account of how good intentions and practical decisions accumulate into something darker. The next chapter should maintain this analytical clarity, particularly around the diplomatic crises that lead directly to war.

8. EGYPT: The Egyptian disaster is fully resolved in this chapter. Inaros was crucified. Amyrtaeus still holds out in the marshes. The relief squadron was destroyed. No loose threads here.

9. CHRONOLOGICAL MARKERS: The chapter ends with the Samian revolt, which is roughly 440 BC. The Thirty Years' Peace was made c. 446/445 BC. The war begins in 431 BC. The next chapters will cover the immediate diplomatic crises.

10. "BARBARIAN" vs. "PERSIAN": In this chapter I consistently used "Persian(s)" instead of "barbarian" since the context was always specific enough. If future chapters use "barbarian" in a more general or emphatic sense (especially in speeches), it can be kept with the understanding that it means "non-Greek" — established in the text analysis.
