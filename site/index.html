Political Philosophy

  • Leviathan Thomas Hobbes (1651) · ~16-hour read · epub Why we need government at all — the founding argument of the modern state, written amid civil war.
  • Two Treatises of Government John Locke (1689) · ~7-hour read · epub The demolition of the divine right of kings, and the case for government by consent.
  • The Social Contract Jean-Jacques Rousseau (1762) · ~3-hour read · epub The general will, the social contract, and the most famous opening line in political philosophy.
  • The Prince Niccolò Machiavelli (1532) · ~2-hour read · epub The infamous handbook of power politics.
  • Common Sense Thomas Paine (1776) · ~90-minute read · epub The pamphlet that talked America into independence.
  • Cato's Letters John Trenchard and Thomas Gordon (1720–1723) · ~3-hour read · epub Eighteen selected letters on liberty, power, and free speech — the columns that taught America to argue for revolution.
  • Utopia Thomas More (1516) · ~3-hour read · epub The island where everything works — and the deadpan joke is that you can't tell how much More means it.
  • The Federalist Papers Alexander Hamilton, James Madison, and John Jay (1787–1788) · ~12-hour read · epub The Constitution's authors explain — and sell — their design.
  • Democracy in America Alexis de Tocqueville (1835/1840) · ~18-hour read · epub A young Frenchman tours the United States and sees the democratic future — flaws and all.
  • Democracy in America (From the French) Alexis de Tocqueville (1835/1840) · ~19-hour read · epub A second pass, translated directly from Tocqueville's French.

Economics

  • The Autobiography of Benjamin Franklin Benjamin Franklin (1771–1790) · ~4-hour read · epub The original American life story — runaway apprentice to statesman, told with a wink.
  • The Way to Wealth Benjamin Franklin (1758) · ~15-minute read · epub Poor Richard's proverbs rolled into one sly speech — delivered to a crowd that applauds, then ignores it.
  • The Wealth of Nations Adam Smith (1776) · ~22-hour read · epub The book that invented economics: pins, markets, and the invisible hand.
  • An Essay on the Principle of Population Thomas Malthus (1798) · ~4-hour read · epub Malthus' unsettling arithmetic of people and food.
  • Progress and Poverty Henry George (1879) · ~12-hour read · epub Why progress and poverty rise together — and George's famous remedy, the land tax.

The Ancient World

  • Dialogues Plato (399–347 BC) · ~42-hour read · epub The complete dialogues — Socrates on trial, in the agora, and at the limits of knowledge.
  • The Memorabilia Xenophon (c. 371 BC) · ~4-hour read · epub Socrates remembered by his other student — practical, sociable, and useful, with the Choice of Heracles.
  • Nicomachean Ethics Aristotle (c. 340 BC) · ~6-hour read · epub The original inquiry into the good life — happiness, virtue as habit, the mean, and friendship.
  • Meditations Marcus Aurelius (c. 170–180 AD) · ~3-hour read · epub A Roman emperor's private notes to himself on how to live.
  • The Enchiridion Epictetus (c. 125 AD) · ~30-minute read · epub The Stoic pocket manual — fifty-one drills for keeping your head, from a philosopher born enslaved.
  • On Duties Cicero (44 BC) · ~4-hour read · epub Cicero's last book, a letter to his son — translated from the Latin: nothing dishonorable is ever truly useful.
  • Tusculan Disputations Cicero (45 BC) · ~5-hour read · epub Five days on death, pain, grief, and the passions — from the Latin, with the Sword of Damocles.
  • Roman Lives Plutarch (c. 100 AD) · ~8-hour read · epub The fall of the Republic in five lives — Caesar, Cato, Cicero, Brutus, Antony — five angles on one catastrophe.
  • The Histories Herodotus (c. 430 BC) · ~15-hour read · epub The first history book — Persia, Egypt, and the Greek wars, with magnificent detours.
  • History of the Peloponnesian War Thucydides (c. 431 BC) · ~14-hour read · epub Power, plague, and the ruin of Athens, by the first analytical historian.
  • Commentaries on the Gallic War Julius Caesar (c. 50 BC) · ~4-hour read · epub Caesar's own dispatches from the conquest of Gaul.

Philosophy & Science

  • Selected Essays Michel de Montaigne (1580–1588) · ~9-hour read · epub The man who invented the essay, talking to you across four centuries about death, friendship, cannibals, and himself.
  • Philosophical Works René Descartes (1637–1644) · ~5-hour read · epub Descartes doubts everything and rebuilds from “I think, therefore I am.”
  • On the Origin of Species Charles Darwin (1859) · ~14-hour read · epub Darwin's patient, overwhelming case for evolution by natural selection.

Fiction & Ideas

  • True History Lucian of Samosata (c. 175 AD) · ~1-hour read · epub The first science fiction story: a voyage to the moon, war between planets, and a narrator who swears that he lies.
  • The Decameron Giovanni Boccaccio (1353) · ~20-hour read · epub Ten friends flee the plague and tell a hundred stories — bawdy, sly, and humane.
  • Candide, or Optimism Voltaire (1759) · ~2-hour read · epub Voltaire's whirlwind satire of optimism in a broken world.
  • Flatland: A Romance of Many Dimensions Edwin A. Abbott (1884) · ~2-hour read · epub Geometry as social satire: a square discovers the third dimension.
