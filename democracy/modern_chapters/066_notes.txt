Next Chapter Notes -- Chapter 066 (Individualism in Democratic Countries)

VOCABULARY DECISIONS

- "individualism" -- kept as-is. This is the chapter where Tocqueville essentially coins the modern meaning of the word. Preserved his careful distinction between individualism and selfishness (his "egotism").
- "egotism" -> "selfishness" throughout, per text_analysis.txt. Critical here because the individualism/selfishness contrast is the chapter's central argument.
- "fellow-creatures" -> "fellow citizens" or "others" depending on context
- "propinquity" -> "immediate vicinity"
- "the woof of time" -> "the thread of time" (archaic weaving metaphor modernized)
- "effaced" -> "erased"
- "aristocratic nations" -> "aristocratic societies" for consistency
- "men" -> "people" / "a person" / "each person" / "everyone" throughout where generic
- "in the same ratio as" -> "at the same rate as"

STYLISTIC DECISIONS

- Preserved the definition paragraph almost word-for-word in modern syntax. This is the most-quoted passage in the chapter and possibly the whole book -- Tocqueville's distinction between individualism and selfishness. Kept the parallel structure (selfishness is X / individualism is Y) intact.
- Broke the long definition paragraph into three paragraphs: (1) the core distinction, (2) origins and effects, (3) the democratic origin point. This matches how modern essays handle definitional arguments.
- The famous closing sentence ("throws him back forever upon himself alone, and threatens in the end to confine him entirely within the solitude of his own heart") -- preserved almost intact, just modernized pronouns. This is one of Tocqueville's most powerful lines.
- The aristocracy-as-chain metaphor ("from the peasant to the king") -- kept exactly as-is. It's vivid and clear.
- The "bond of human affection is extended, but it is relaxed" -- rendered as "stretched wider, but they grow thinner" for more natural modern phrasing while keeping the paradox.

TONE CALIBRATION

- This chapter has a philosophical, almost elegiac quality. Tocqueville isn't angry -- he's describing a deep structural problem with a mixture of analytical precision and genuine sadness. Maintained that register.
- The chapter is short but devastating. Resisted the temptation to add explanatory padding -- Tocqueville's concision is part of the power.

RECURRING TERMS TO MAINTAIN

- "individualism" vs. "selfishness" -- this distinction must carry forward through all the following chapters in this section
- "equality of conditions" / "equality" / "social equality" -- the driving force
- "democracy breaks that chain" -- the chain metaphor may recur
- "solitude of their own heart" -- this image recurs later when Tocqueville talks about remedies
