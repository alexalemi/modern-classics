VOCABULARY DECISIONS

- "irregularities in morals" -> "moral excess"
- "inquietude" -> "restlessness"
- "sumptuous depravity and splendid corruption" -> "extravagant depravity and magnificent corruption" -- kept the dramatic pairing; this is one of Tocqueville's most vivid phrases
- "gorgeous" (in the archaic sense of "magnificent") -> "spectacular"
- "lustre" -> dropped; restructured
- "enervated" -> "soft" (first use in the chapter), "drain it of energy" (final line)
- "debauched" -> "debauched" (kept; still current)
- "nay" -> "in fact"
- "a species of" -> "a certain kind of"
- "roods" -> "acres" (a rood is a quarter-acre; modernized the unit)
- "virtuous materialism" -> "respectable materialism" -- this is a key Tocquevillean coinage; "respectable" better captures his ironic intent than the more paradoxical "virtuous"
- "noiselessly unbend its springs of action" -> "silently loosen every spring of action"

STYLISTIC DECISIONS

- The chapter's structure is a clear contrast: aristocratic decadence (dramatic, spectacular, self-destructive) vs. democratic materialism (modest, pervasive, soul-deadening). Kept this contrast sharp.
- The aristocratic passages are allowed to be more dramatic and rhetorical -- matching Tocqueville's tone when he describes aristocratic decline.
- The democratic passages are deliberately more understated -- "adding a few acres to your property, planting an orchard, expanding your house" -- the plainness of the list IS the point.
- "These are small things, but the soul clings to them" -- preserved this pivot sentence; it's the chapter's most important turn.
- The final paragraph is Tocqueville's thesis statement for these chapters -- kept it clean and punchy.

TONE CALIBRATION

- Two registers in this chapter: vivid/dramatic for the aristocratic decline passages, quiet/understated for the democratic materialism passages. This tonal contrast mirrors the content contrast.
- The closing line about "respectable materialism" that "wouldn't corrupt the soul so much as drain it of energy" -- this is the dark heart of Tocqueville's warning about democratic society. Kept it sharp.
