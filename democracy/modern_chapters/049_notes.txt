STYLISTIC DECISIONS

- Short chapter -- kept it tight and punchy. This is essentially one sustained argument: democracy pushes people toward either total unbelief or total Catholicism.
- "Here's what we see..." -- used a direct pivot to signal the empirical observation.
- Preserved the strong closing prediction about the two-camp sorting, which is vintage Tocqueville prophecy.
- "halfway thinkers" -- a colloquial summary of Tocqueville's description of people who want both religious freedom and religious authority.

TONE CALIBRATION

- This chapter has an analytical, almost sociological tone -- Tocqueville explaining a paradox. Kept it explanatory and clear.
- The conditional about Catholicism disentangling itself from politics is one of Tocqueville's characteristically bold predictions -- kept it forceful.
