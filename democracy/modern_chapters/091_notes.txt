STYLISTIC DECISIONS

- This chapter has a clear two-part structure: the general law (wages rise under democracy) then the dark exception (industrial workers trapped in poverty). Maintained this structure cleanly.
- The farming example is concrete and well-placed; kept it as a clear illustration
- The "vicious cycle" passage about industrial workers is one of Tocqueville's most prescient observations -- he's essentially describing the dynamics Marx would analyze more systematically a few years later. Preserved the force and clarity of the argument.
- The strike passage is strikingly modern; kept the stark contrast between the rich employer who can wait and the workers who starve
- The closing paragraph is a warning to legislators; kept its urgency

TONE CALIBRATION

- Opening: analytical, measured -- laying out the general principle
- The farming example: concrete, illustrative
- The industrial exception: darkening in tone, building toward alarm
- The vicious cycle passage: bleak, forceful
- Closing: urgent, addressed to those in power -- Tocqueville the reformer speaking
