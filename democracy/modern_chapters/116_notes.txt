STYLISTIC DECISIONS

- THE FAMOUS PASSAGE: "Above this race of men stands an immense and tutelary power..." -- rendered as "Above these people stands an immense, protective power that takes it upon itself alone to secure their comforts and watch over their fate." Key decisions:
  - "race of men" -> "these people" (no racial connotation in original; "race" here means "category/type")
  - "tutelary" -> "protective" -- captures the guardian connotation without the obscure word
  - "gratifications" -> "comforts" -- more natural, same meaning
  - Preserved the devastating rhetorical buildup through the whole passage

- THE KILLER SEQUENCE: "it does not destroy, but it prevents existence; it does not tyrannize, but it compresses, enervates, extinguishes, and stupefies" -- rendered as "It doesn't destroy; it prevents things from coming into being. It doesn't tyrannize; it compresses, it drains, it extinguishes, it stupefies." Key decisions:
  - Broke into shorter sentences for maximum punch
  - Separated the four verbs with commas + "it" for rhythmic emphasis
  - Kept "stupefies" -- it still works perfectly and is the strongest final word
  - "prevents existence" -> "prevents things from coming into being" -- slightly expanded for clarity without losing power

- The shepherd/flock metaphor: "reduces each nation to nothing more than a flock of timid, industrious animals, with the government as their shepherd" -- preserved almost verbatim; this callback to Volume 1 works perfectly

- "I'm trying myself to choose an expression..." -> "I'm struggling to find a word..." -- the moment where Tocqueville admits language fails him is crucial to the passage's power; kept it prominent

- Section break (***) placed between the analytical setup and the visionary passage, giving the famous section its own space

- The "paradox" passage about being playthings and masters -- preserved the sharp contrast: "greater than kings and less than fully human"

- Final metaphor: "short-lived monster" -- kept this striking image

TONE CALIBRATION

- The opening is measured, analytical, building toward the vision

- The second half is more argumentative -- Tocqueville working through the logic of why elections don't solve this problem -- kept the analytical precision
