STYLISTIC DECISIONS

- Used section breaks (***) liberally -- this is a long chapter covering many distinct topics (associations, press freedom, judiciary, procedural formalities, individual rights, revolution, final summary). Each section gets its own space.
- "I should have loved freedom, I believe, at all times, but in the time in which we live I am ready to worship it." -- This is one of Tocqueville's most famous personal declarations. Rendered as: "I would have loved freedom, I believe, in any era. But in the age we live in, I'm ready to worship it." Two short sentences for maximum impact.
- The passage about rulers using people to make things great vs. making great people -- preserved the rhetorical parallel structure with "I wish they would..." repetition
- The two kinds of fatalism (those who fear freedom / those who think it's impossible) -- preserved the sharp binary and Tocqueville's personal intervention: "If I shared the second group's conviction, I would not have written this book"
- The final sentence is the chapter's emotional climax: "Let us face the future with healthy fear... not passive terror." Rendered to feel like a genuine rallying cry without losing Tocqueville's measured tone.

TONE CALIBRATION

- This chapter oscillates between practical remedies and passionate declarations
- The practical sections (associations, press, judiciary) maintain the analytical register
- The personal declarations ("I'm ready to worship it," "I would not have written this book") are rendered with emotional directness
