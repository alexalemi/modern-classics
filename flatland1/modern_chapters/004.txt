Concerning the Female Inhabitants

If our highly pointed Triangular soldiers are formidable, our Female inhabitants are even more so. For while a soldier is like a wedge, a Female is like a needle - all point, so to speak, at least at the two ends. Add to this their ability to become practically invisible at will, and you'll see that a Female in Flatland is a force to be reckoned with.

But perhaps some of my younger readers wonder how a Female in Flatland can become invisible. This should be apparent without explanation, but I'll clarify it further.

Imagine placing a needle on a table. When viewed from the side, you can see its full length, but when viewed from the end, you only see a point - it has become practically invisible. It's the same with our Females. When their sides are turned toward us, we see them as straight lines; but when the end containing their eye or mouth (which are one and the same for us) faces us, we see only a bright point. And when their backs are presented, their duller hind ends serve as a sort of "invisible cap."

The dangers our Females pose must now be clear even to the most oblivious person in Spaceland. If the angle of a respectable middle-class Triangle can be hazardous, if colliding with a common worker means a gash, and if touching the tip of a soldier's weapon can mean death, then imagine the absolute and immediate destruction of running into a Female! And when a Female is invisible or visible only as a dim point, how difficult it must be to always avoid colliding with her!

Many laws have been enacted in the various states of Flatland to minimize this peril, and in the warmer southern climates, where the force of gravity is greater and people more prone to sudden, involuntary movements, the regulations concerning Females are naturally much stricter...
