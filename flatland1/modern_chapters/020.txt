How the Sphere Encouraged Me in a Vision

Although I had less than a minute to think about it, I instinctively knew I had to keep my experiences from my wife. Not that I feared she would reveal my secret at the moment, but I understood that my tale of adventure would be entirely incomprehensible to any woman in Flatland. So I tried to reassure her with a story I made up on the spot, claiming I had accidentally fallen through a trap door in the cellar and lay stunned there.

The pull of the south in our country is so slight that even to a woman, my story would seem extraordinary and almost unbelievable. But my wife, whose good sense far exceeds that of the average of her sex, recognized that I was unusually excited and did not argue with me. Instead, she insisted I was unwell and needed rest. I was glad to have an excuse to retire to my chamber and quietly reflect on what had happened.

When I was finally alone, a drowsy sensation came over me. But before my eyes closed, I tried to recall the Third Dimension and the process by which a Cube is constructed from the motion of a Square. It was not as clear as I had hoped, but I remembered the crucial instructions: "Upward, yet not Northward." I determined to steadfastly hold on to these words as the key that would guide me to the solution.

Mechanically repeating the words "Upward, yet not Northward" like a charm, I fell into a deep, refreshing sleep. During my slumber, I had a dream. I thought I was once again by the side of the Sphere, whose brilliant glow indicated he had exchanged his anger towards me for perfect calmness. We were moving together towards a bright but infinitesimally small Point, to which my Guide directed my attention.

As we approached, a faint humming sound emerged from the Point, like the buzzing of one of your Spaceland flies, but much quieter - so faint that even in the perfect stillness of the Vacuum through which we glided, the sound reached our ears only when we paused our flight at a distance of about twenty human diagonals.

"Look there," said my Guide, "in Flatland you have lived; of Lineland you have received a vision; you have soared with me to the heights of Spaceland. Now, to complete the range of your experience, I shall lead you downward to the lowest depth of existence, even to the realm of Pointland, the Abyss of No Dimensions.

"Behold that wretched creature. That Point is a Being like ourselves, but confined to the dimensionless Void. It is its own World, its own Universe; it can conceive of no other existence besides its own. It knows nothing of Length, Breadth, or Height, for it has never experienced them. It has no awareness even of the number Two, nor any notion of Plurality; for it is itself its One and All, being truly Nothing. Yet observe its perfect self-satisfaction, and learn the lesson that to be content with one's own ignorance is to be vile and ignorant, and that to aspire is better than to be blindly and helplessly happy. Now listen."

He fell silent, and from the little buzzing creature arose a tiny, low, monotonous, yet distinct tinkling, like the sound of one of your Spaceland phonographs, from which I caught these words: "Infinite bliss of existence! It is; and there is nothing else beside It."

"What," said I, "does the puny creature mean by 'It'?" "It means itself," said the Sphere: "have you not noticed that babies and childish people who cannot distinguish themselves from the world speak of themselves in the Third Person? But hush!"

"It fills all Space," continued the little soliloquizing Creature, "and what It fills, It is. What It thinks, that It utters; and what It utters, that It hears; and It itself is Thinker, Speaker, Listener, Thought, Word, Hearing; It is the One, and yet the All in All. Ah, the happiness, ah, the happiness of Being!"

"Can you not startle the little thing out of its complacency?" said I. "Tell it what it really is, as you told me; reveal to it the narrow limitations of Pointland, and lead it up to something higher." "That is no easy task," said my Master; "you try."

Thereupon, raising my voice to the utmost, I addressed the Point as follows:

"Silence, silence, contemptible Creature. You call yourself the All in All, but you are Nothing: your so-called Universe is but a speck in a Line, and a Line is merely a shadow compared to—" 

"Hush, hush, you have said enough," interrupted the Sphere, "now listen, and mark the effect of your speech on the King of Pointland."

The brilliance of the Monarch, who shone more brightly than ever upon hearing my words, showed clearly that he remained thoroughly content with himself. I had hardly finished speaking when he resumed his refrain:

"Ah, the joy, ah, the joy of Thought! What can It not achieve by thinking! Its own Thought coming to Itself, suggesting Its own insignificance, thereby enhancing Its happiness! Sweet rebellion stirred up to result in triumph! Ah, the divine creative power of the All in One! Ah, the joy, the joy of Being!"

"You see," said my Teacher, "how little your words have affected him. As far as the Monarch understands them at all, he accepts them as his own - for he can conceive of no other existence but his own - and takes pride in the variety of 'Its Thought' as an example of creative Power. Let us leave this God of Pointland to the ignorant bliss of his omnipresence and omniscience: nothing you or I can do can rescue him from his self-satisfaction."

After this, as we floated gently back to Flatland, I could hear the mild voice of my Companion pointing out the moral of my vision and encouraging me to aspire higher. He had initially been angered by my ambition to explore dimensions beyond the Third, but since then, he had gained new insight and was not too proud to acknowledge his error to a student. He then proceeded to initiate me into mysteries even loftier than those I had witnessed, showing me how to construct Extra-Solids from the motion of Solids, and Double Extra-Solids from the motion of Extra-Solids - all "strictly according to Analogy," and all through methods so simple and easy that they would be accessible even to the Female Sex.
