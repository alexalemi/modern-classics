§ 9 Concerning the Universal Color Law

However, the intellectual arts were rapidly decaying.

The skill of Visual Recognition was no longer needed, so it was no longer practiced. The studies of Geometry, Statics, Kinetics, and other related subjects soon came to be seen as unnecessary and fell into disrespect and neglect, even at our University. The lesser skill of Touch quickly suffered the same fate in our elementary schools.

Then the Isosceles classes, claiming that the Specimens were no longer used or needed, and refusing to pay the customary tribute from the Criminal classes to the service of Education, grew daily more numerous and more insolent, bolstered by their freedom from the old burden that had once tamed their brutish nature and thinned their excessive numbers.

Year by year, the Soldiers and Artisans began to assert more vehemently—and with increasing truth—that there was little difference between them and the very highest class of Polygons, now that they had been raised to an equality with the latter and enabled to grapple with all the difficulties and solve all the problems of life, whether Static or Kinetic, by the simple process of Color Recognition. Unsatisfied with the natural decline of Visual Recognition, they boldly demanded the legal prohibition of all "monopolizing and aristocratic Arts" and the consequent abolition of all funding for the studies of Visual Recognition, Mathematics, and Touch.

Soon, they insisted that since Color, which was a second Nature, had destroyed the need for aristocratic distinctions, the Law should follow suit, and that henceforth all individuals and all classes should be recognized as absolutely equal and entitled to equal rights.

Finding the higher Orders wavering and undecided, the leaders of the Revolution pressed further in their demands, and ultimately required that all classes alike—the Priests and the Women not excepted—should pay homage to Color by submitting to be painted. When it was objected that Priests and Women had no sides, they retorted that Nature and Expediency dictated that the front half of every human being (containing the eye and mouth) should be distinguishable from the rear half. They therefore brought before a general and extraordinary Assembly of all the States of Flatland a Bill proposing that in every Woman, the half containing the eye and mouth should be colored red, and the other half green. The Priests were to be painted in the same way, with the semicircle containing the eye and mouth colored red, while the other or rear semicircle was to be colored green.

There was no small cunning in this proposal, which indeed came not from any Isosceles—for no being so degraded would have the angularity to appreciate, let alone devise, such a model of statecraft—but from an Irregular Circle who, instead of being destroyed in childhood, was spared by a foolish indulgence to bring desolation on his country and destruction on myriads of followers.
