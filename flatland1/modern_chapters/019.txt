When I saw my poor brother led away to imprisonment, I tried to leap down into the Council Chamber to plead on his behalf or at least say goodbye. But I found I had no control over my own movements. I was entirely dependent on the will of my Guide, who said in a gloomy tone, "Pay no mind to your brother; you will have ample time later to mourn him. Follow me."

Once more we ascended into space. "Until now," said the Sphere, "I have shown you only two-dimensional shapes and their interiors. Now I must introduce you to three-dimensional solids and reveal the principles behind their construction. Behold this collection of movable square cards. See, I place one on top of another, not to the north as you might have assumed, but directly on top. Now a second, now a third. I am building up a three-dimensional solid by stacking many squares parallel to one another. Now the solid is complete, being as tall as it is long and wide, and we call it a cube."

"Pardon me, my Lord," I replied, "but to my eyes it appears to be an irregular figure with its interior exposed; in other words, I see not a solid, but a two-dimensional plane, albeit one with an abnormal irregularity that seems almost criminal, so painful is it to my eyes."

"True," said the Sphere, "it appears to you as a plane, because you are unaccustomed to the effects of light, shadow, and perspective; just as in Flatland, a hexagon would look like a straight line to one who lacks the skill to recognize three dimensions. But in reality, it is a solid, as you shall learn by the sense of touch."

He then introduced me to the cube, and I found that this remarkable being was indeed no mere plane, but a three-dimensional solid, endowed with six flat sides and eight corners. I recalled the Sphere's explanation that such a creature would be formed by a square moving through space parallel to itself, and I was overjoyed to think that an insignificant being like myself could in some sense be considered the progenitor of such an illustrious offspring.

Yet I still did not fully grasp the meaning of the Sphere's words about "light," "shadow," and "perspective"; and I did not hesitate to voice my confusion.
