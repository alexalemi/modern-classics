§ 12 On the Teachings of Our Priests

The doctrine of the Circles can be summed up in a single principle: "Focus on your Shape." Whether in politics, religion, or morality, all their teachings aim to improve individual and collective Shape - with a special emphasis, of course, on the Shape of the Circles, to which all other concerns are subordinate.

The Circles deserve credit for effectively suppressing the ancient heresies that led people to waste energy and compassion on the misguided belief that behavior depends on willpower, effort, training, encouragement, praise, or anything other than Shape. It was Pantocyclus - the renowned Circle mentioned earlier as the queller of the Color Rebellion - who first convinced humanity that Shape defines the person. If, for example, you are born an Isosceles with two uneven sides, you will inevitably go astray unless you have them made even - for which you must visit the Isosceles Hospital. Similarly, if you are a Triangle, Square, or even a Polygon born with any Irregularity, you must be taken to one of the Regular Hospitals to cure your "disease"; otherwise, you will end your days in prison or at the angle of the state executioner.

Pantocyclus attributed all faults or defects, from the slightest misconduct to the most heinous crime, to some deviation from perfect Regularity in the physical form, perhaps caused (if not congenital) by a collision in a crowd, neglect of exercise, excess of it, or even a sudden temperature change resulting in the shrinkage or expansion of some susceptible part of the frame. Therefore, concluded that illustrious Philosopher, neither good nor bad conduct is truly worthy of praise or blame. Why praise, for example, the integrity of a Square who faithfully defends a client's interests, when you should really admire the exact precision of their right angles? Or why condemn a lying, thieving Isosceles, when you should instead lament the incurable inequality of their sides?

Theoretically, this doctrine is unassailable, but it has practical drawbacks. When dealing with an Isosceles, if a scoundrel pleads that they cannot help stealing due to their unevenness, you reply that precisely for that reason - because they cannot help being a nuisance to their neighbors - you, the Magistrate, cannot help sentencing them to be consumed. But in minor domestic disputes, where the penalty of consumption or death is not an option, this theory of Shape sometimes proves awkward. I must confess that when one of my own Hexagonal Grandchildren pleads that a sudden temperature change has been too much for their Perimeter, and that I should blame their Shape rather than them, I neither see a logical way to reject nor a practical way to accept their reasoning.

Personally, I find it best to assume that a good scolding or punishment has some hidden, strengthening influence on my Grandchild's Shape, though I admit I have no grounds for believing so. I am not alone in this approach to extricating myself from this dilemma; many of the highest Circles, when serving as Judges in law courts, use praise and blame towards Regular and Irregular Figures, and in their homes, I know from experience that they speak of "right" and "wrong" as passionately as if they believe these concepts have real existence and that a human Figure is truly capable of choosing between them.

Continuously emphasizing the primacy of Shape in every mind, the Circles have reversed the nature of the Commandment that in your world regulates the relationship between parents and children. With you, children are taught to honor their parents; with us, next to the Circles, who are the primary object of universal reverence, a person is taught to honor their Grandchild, if they have one, or their Son if not. However, "honor" here does not mean "indulgence," but a respectful regard for their highest interests; and the Circles teach that a father's duty is to subordinate their own interests to those of future generations, thereby advancing the welfare of the entire State as well as their own immediate descendants.

The weak point in the system of the Circles - if a humble Square may dare to identify any element of weakness in anything Circular - appears to be their relations with Women. As it is of utmost importance for society that Irregular births be discouraged, it follows that no Woman with any Irregularities in her ancestry is a fit partner for one who desires that their posterity rise steadily in the social scale. Now, a Male's Irregularity is a matter of measurement, but since all Women are straight and therefore visibly Regular, one must devise other means of ascertaining their "invisible Irregularity," that is, their potential Irregularities as regards possible offspring. This is achieved through carefully maintained pedigrees, preserved and supervised by the State; and without a certified pedigree, no Woman is allowed to marry.

One might have expected a Circle - proud of their ancestry and mindful of a posterity that might one day produce a Chief Circle - to be the most careful in choosing a wife with an unblemished lineage. But this is not the case. The care in selecting a Regular wife appears to diminish as one rises in the social scale. Nothing would induce an ambitious Isosceles, hoping to generate an Equilateral Son, to take a wife with even a single Irregular Ancestor; a Square or Pentagon, confident that their family is steadily improving, does not inquire beyond the five-hundredth generation; a Hexagon or Dodecagon is even more careless of the wife's pedigree; but a Circle has been known to deliberately marry a Woman with an Irregular Great-Grandfather, all because of some slight superiority in luster or the charm of a low voice - which, with us, even more than with you, is considered "an excellent thing in a Woman."

Such ill-advised marriages often result in barrenness or even positive Irregularity or diminution of sides, though none of these deterrents have thus far proved sufficient. The loss of a few sides in a highly developed Polygon is not easily noticed and can sometimes be compensated by a successful operation in the Neo-Therapeutic Gymnasium, as I have described; and the Circles are too inclined to acquiesce in infertility as a law of superior development. Yet, if this evil is not addressed, the gradual diminution of the Circular class may soon accelerate, and the time may come when the race can no longer produce a Chief Circle, and the very Constitution of Flatland must fall.

One other word of warning occurs to me, though I cannot easily propose a remedy; and this, too, relates to our relations with Women. About three hundred years ago, it was decreed by the Chief Circle that, since Women are deficient in Reason but abundant in Emotion, they should no longer be treated as rational beings nor receive any mental education. The consequence was that they were no longer taught to read, nor even to master basic Arithmetic enough to count the angles of their husbands or children; and hence, they sensibly declined in intellectual power with each passing generation. This system of female non-education or passivity still prevails.

My fear is that, with the best intentions, this policy has been carried too far, and now reacts injuriously on the Male Sex. For the result is that, as things now stand, we Males must lead a kind of bilingual and almost bimental existence. With Women, we speak of "love," "duty," "right," "wrong," "pity," "hope," and other irrational and emotional concepts that have no real existence, and whose sole purpose is to control feminine excesses; but among ourselves and in our books, we have an entirely different vocabulary and idiom. "Love" becomes "the anticipation of benefits"; "duty" becomes "necessity" or "fitness"; and other words are correspondingly transformed. Moreover, among Women, we use language implying the utmost deference to their Sex, and they fully believe that the Chief Circle Himself is not more devoutly adored by us than they are; but behind their backs, they are regarded and spoken of - by all but the very young - as being little better than "mindless organisms."

Our Theology in the Women's chambers is also entirely different from our Theology elsewhere.

Now, my humble fear is that this dual training, in both language and thought, imposes too heavy a burden on the young, especially when, at the age of three years old, they are taken from maternal care and taught to unlearn the old language - except for the purpose of repeating it in the presence of Mothers and Nurses - and to learn the vocabulary and idiom of science. Already, I believe I discern a weakening in the grasp of mathematical truth at the present time, as compared to the more robust intellect of our ancestors three hundred years ago. I say nothing of the possible danger if a Woman should ever surreptitiously learn to read and convey to her Sex the result of her perusal of a single popular volume; nor of the possibility that the indiscretion or disobedience of some infant Male might reveal to a Mother the secrets of the logical dialect. On the simple ground of the enfeebling of the male intellect, I humbly appeal to the highest Authorities to reconsider the regulations of Female education.
