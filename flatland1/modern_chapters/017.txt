§ 17 How the Sphere, Having Failed to Convince with Words, Resorted to Deeds

It was no use. I collided my sharpest corner with the Stranger, pressing on him with enough force to have destroyed any ordinary Circle, but I could feel him slowly and unstoppably slipping from my grasp, not moving to the right or left, but somehow leaving our world and vanishing into nothingness. Soon there was just emptiness. But I still heard the Intruder's voice.

Sphere: Why do you refuse to listen to reason? I had hoped to find in you—a sensible man and accomplished mathematician—a fitting messenger for the Gospel of the Three Dimensions, which I am allowed to preach once every thousand years. But now I don't know how to convince you. Wait, I have an idea. Deeds, not words, shall reveal the truth. Listen, my friend.

I have told you that from my position in Space, I can see the insides of all things you consider closed. For example, in that cupboard near you, I see several of what you call boxes (though in Flatland, they have no tops or bottoms) filled with money, and two account ledgers. I'm about to descend into that cupboard and retrieve one of those ledgers. I saw you lock the cupboard half an hour ago, and I know you have the key. But I come from Space; the doors remain unmoved. Now I'm in the cupboard, taking the ledger. Now I have it. Now I ascend with it.

I rushed to the closet and flung the door open. One of the ledgers was gone. With a mocking laugh, the Stranger appeared in the corner of the room, and at the same time the ledger appeared on the floor. I picked it up. There was no doubt—it was the missing ledger.

I groaned in horror, doubting my own senses. But the Stranger continued, "Surely you must now see that my explanation, and no other, fits the facts. What you call Solid things are really just surfaces; what you call Space is really nothing but a great Plane. I am in Space and can see the insides of the things you only see the outsides of. You could leave the Plane yourself, if you had the necessary willpower. A slight upward or downward motion would let you see all that I can see.

"The higher I rise, and the further I go from your Plane, the more I can see, though on a smaller scale. For example, I can now see your neighbor the Hexagon and his family in their apartments; the inside of the Theater, ten doors down, with the audience just leaving; and a Circle in his study, reading his books. Now I will come back to you. And as final proof, what do you say to my giving you a little touch, just the slightest, in your stomach? It won't seriously hurt you, and the brief pain will be nothing compared to the mental enlightenment you'll receive."

Before I could protest, I felt a shooting pain inside me, followed by a demonic laugh that seemed to come from within. A moment later, the sharp agony had passed, leaving only a dull ache, and the Stranger began to reappear, saying as he grew larger, "There, I didn't hurt you much, did I? If you're not convinced now, I don't know what will convince you. What do you say?"

My decision was made. It was intolerable to exist at the mercy of this Magician who could play tricks with one's very stomach. If only I could somehow pin him against the wall until help arrived!

Once more I charged at him with my hardest edge, while also alarming the whole household with cries for assistance. I believe the Stranger had briefly sunk below our Plane and had difficulty rising. In any case, he remained motionless as I pressed against him with renewed force, continuing to shout for help.

A convulsive shudder ran through the Sphere. "This must not be," I thought I heard him say, "either he must listen to reason, or I must resort to civilization's last recourse." Then, speaking louder, he urgently exclaimed, "Listen: no stranger must witness what you have seen. Send your Wife back at once, before she enters. The Gospel of Three Dimensions cannot be thwarted like this. The fruits of a thousand years' waiting must not be thrown away. I hear her coming. Back! back! Get away from me, or you must go with me—to a place you know not—into the Land of Three Dimensions!"

"Fool! Madman! Irregular!" I exclaimed. "I will never release you; you shall pay for your deceptions."

"Ha! Has it come to this?" thundered the Stranger. "Then meet your fate: out of your Plane you go. Once, twice, thrice! It is done!"
