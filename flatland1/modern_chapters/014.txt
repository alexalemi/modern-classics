How I Vainly Tried to Explain the Nature of Flatland

Thinking it was time to bring the Monarch down from his raptures to a more practical level, I decided to try to give him a glimpse of the truth - that is, the true nature of things in Flatland. So I began:

"How does Your Highness distinguish the shapes and positions of your subjects? I noticed, before entering your Kingdom, that some of your people are Lines and others are Points, and that some Lines are larger than others."

"That is impossible," interrupted the King. "You must have had a vision, for it is well known that one cannot tell the difference between a Line and a Point by sight. But this can be done by sound - and in this way, I can precisely determine my own shape. Behold me! I am the longest Line in Lineland, over six inches long!"

"Of length," I ventured to suggest.

"Fool!" he said. "Space is length. Interrupt me again, and I'm done with you."

I apologized, and he continued scornfully, "Since you are impervious to reason, you shall hear with your own ears how I reveal my shape to my Wives, who are at this moment miles away. Listen, I call to them."

He made a chirping sound, then said, "My wives, hearing the sound of my two voices and perceiving the interval between them, can calculate my exact length to be 6.457 inches. But they do not need to make this calculation every time - they did it once before we were married. And in the same way, I can estimate the shape of any of my male subjects by sound alone."

"But what if a man imitates a woman's voice?" I asked. "Could that not cause problems? And do you have no way to verify such deceptions by having your subjects feel one another?"

This, of course, was a rather foolish question, as touch could not serve that purpose. But I asked it to irritate the Monarch, and I succeeded.

"What?!" he cried in horror. "The very idea of such close proximity is punishable by death in my realm! Women's frail forms must be protected from any violation of the sacred space between individuals. And the sense of hearing provides a far superior, more exact method of identification than your brutish notion of 'touching.'"

He then went on to extol the virtues of his auditory abilities, claiming that in a single moment he could gather the "census and statistics, local, corporeal, mental and spiritual, of every living being in Lineland." I could only listen in dismay, for his life in that one-dimensional world seemed utterly dull and limited to me.

Hoping to make him understand, I described what I had seen of him and his subjects before entering Lineland. But he dismissed it as a mere dream, insisting that I could not have truly seen the "inside" of any being. Frustrated, I then tried to explain the concept of moving in a different direction, beyond the confines of his linear existence. But he remained completely perplexed, unable to grasp the idea of any dimension beyond his narrow line.

Finally, in a last attempt, I began to move my body out of Lineland, passing out of the King's view. As I did so, he cried that I had vanished and was dead. "I am not dead," I replied, "I am simply out of Lineland, in the true Space where I can see things as they are." I then proceeded to describe the details of his subjects that I could now perceive.

But the Monarch refused to accept my account, declaring it irrational and accusing me of mere trickery. Furious at his stubborn blindness, I retorted with scorn, calling him a "besotted Being" who could see nothing beyond a single Point. I proclaimed myself the "completion of [his] incomplete self," a being of higher dimensions able to comprehend what he could not.

This only enraged the King further, and I found myself spell-bound as he advanced upon me, his subjects joining in a tumultuous war cry. But just as he was about to strike, I awoke to find myself back in the realities of Flatland, the breakfast bell summoning me to the day's tasks.
