CHAPTER 000 TRANSLATION NOTES — Prefatory Note
================================================

CHANGES AND RATIONALE
---------------------
- Broke the single enormous sentence into multiple shorter sentences, one per
  "part" of the Discourse. The original is a single run-on sentence with
  semicolons; the modern version gives each section its own sentence for clarity.

- Shifted from third person ("the Author") to first person ("I've discovered")
  after the opening, since Descartes wrote the Discourse in first person and
  this prefatory note was his own. The first mention keeps "this Discourse" to
  preserve the sense of a note addressed to the reader.

- "appear too long to be read at once" → "seems too long to read in one sitting"
  — more natural modern phrasing.

- "the brutes" → "animals" — per vocabulary mapping. Descartes' term for
  non-human creatures.

- "soul of man" → "human mind" — since Descartes uses "soul" (âme) to mean
  the thinking/reasoning faculty (what we'd now call the mind), and this
  chapter is previewing the mind-body distinction. "Soul" will be used
  elsewhere when the theological sense is primary.

- "explication of the motion of the heart" → "how the heart works" —
  modernized but faithful.

- "what the Author believes to be required in order to greater advancement
  in the investigation of Nature" → "what I believe is needed to make greater
  progress in understanding nature" — simplified syntax, same meaning.

- Used em dashes (--) for the parenthetical "the foundations of my metaphysics"
  to keep the reading flow smooth.

CONSISTENCY NOTES FOR NEXT CHAPTER
-----------------------------------
- First person ("I") established as the voice — maintain throughout the Discourse.
- "Method" capitalized when referring to Descartes' specific Method.
- "the sciences" left lowercase (general reference to fields of study).
- "animals" not "brutes" for non-human creatures.
- "metaphysics" lowercase when used descriptively.
- Tone is direct and conversational — the Discourse should feel like Descartes
  talking to you personally.
- "Clear and distinct" has not appeared yet but will — keep as a paired phrase.
- Part I (chapter 001) is autobiographical — lean into the storytelling voice.
