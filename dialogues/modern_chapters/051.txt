As for marriage portions — I've already covered that. Let me repeat: no one should feel ashamed of poverty in marriage. The citizens of our state are provided with the necessities of life. Banning dowries means wives are less likely to become arrogant and husbands less likely to become servile and groveling because of their wife's money.

Anyone who follows this law is doing a noble thing. The excess gifts themselves are dedicated to Hera and Zeus. Their treasurers will collect the fines, just as Hera's treasurers collect from confirmed bachelors — or pay the penalty themselves.

For betrothals: a father's betrothal is valid in the first degree, a grandfather's in the second, and a brother's (from the same father) in the third. If none of these men are alive, a mother's betrothal holds in the same order. In cases of extraordinary misfortune — when no family member is available — the next of kin and the legal guardians have authority.

As for pre-wedding rituals and other sacred ceremonies related to past, present, or future marriages: the interpreters will handle that. Follow their advice, and you can rest easy.

For the wedding celebration itself: no more than five male and five female friends from each family, plus an equal number of relatives from each side. Nobody should spend more than he can afford. The richest class may spend one mina on the feast; the second class, half a mina; and so on in proportion. Everyone should praise the man who follows this rule. Anyone who goes overboard should be penalized by the guardians of the law — fined as a man with no taste and no understanding of proper wedding etiquette.

Now, getting drunk. It's always inappropriate — except at the festivals of the god who gave us wine. And it's especially dangerous when you're getting married. On this day, of all days, the bride and groom should have their wits about them. They should take care that their children are born to parents who are clearheaded — since nobody knows which day or night heaven will grant them conception.

Beyond that, children shouldn't be conceived when the parents' bodies are wrecked by intoxication. Offspring should be formed from what's solid and steady, calm and well-composed. A drunk person lurches through everything — his body is all over the place, and so is his mind. He's the worst possible seed-sower: unstable, unreliable, likely to produce children who are unsteady in both body and mind.

So throughout the entire year — and really throughout his whole life, but especially during the time he's fathering children — a man should avoid anything that damages his health or involves reckless behavior. He can't help leaving an impression of himself on the souls and bodies of his children, and he'll produce inferior offspring in every way.

And especially on the wedding day and night itself: abstain. The beginning of anything — which is itself a kind of divinity dwelling in human affairs — preserves all things, if it receives proper respect.

Moving on: the newlywed should treat one of the two houses in his allotment as the nest and nursery for his children. That's where he marries, sets up his home, and raises his family — away from his father and mother.

Now, in any close relationship, there needs to be some degree of missing each other, some longing, to cement the bond between different personalities. But too much togetherness — constant contact without the desire that comes from time apart — gradually dissolves affection through sheer saturation. That's why the young couple should leave their parents' homes and establish their own household, like colonists founding a new settlement. They'll visit their parents and be visited in return. They'll have children and raise them, passing the torch of life from one generation to the next, worshipping the gods according to the law, forever.

Now we need to consider what kind of property will be most practical. Most kinds of property are easy enough to understand and acquire. The remark I made in passing — which naturally prompted your question — was this: we all agree that we'd like to have the best and most loyal slaves we can get. Of course.
