SOURCE ODDITY (all files): the Latin Library text carries occasional
lines in [square brackets] — these are editorial variant/doublet lines
(e.g. Book I line 100, a doublet of Daphne's prayer). Fold their sense
in ONCE where relevant; do NOT translate a bracketed doublet twice.

word/line — read through them to the plain sense; never render the markup.

FORMATTING: render any EPITAPH or INSCRIPTION (e.g. Phaethon's tomb) in
normal/Title case, NEVER all-caps — assemble.py treats an all-caps line
as a section heading. Set such couplets off as their own short lines but
keep them mixed-case.

EMBEDDED TALES (tale-within-a-tale) — locked convention (used in files
005, 008, 009; CRITICAL for Book X where Orpheus sings for ~700 lines,
and for any frame like the Minyeides or the Muses): render a LONG
embedded song/tale as PRIMARY NARRATION — NO enclosing quotation marks
around the whole tale. Reserve double quotes for the framing remarks
and for direct character DIALOGUE; use single quotes for dialogue nested
inside the embedded tale. Return to the outer frame's first person
cleanly at the tale's end. Do not wrap hundreds of lines in one quote.
