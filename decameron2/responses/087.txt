The Pig Heist: A Tale of Total Trickery

When Filostrato finished his hilarious story, the queen asked Filomena to tell the next tale. Filomena grinned and said, "Alright, let me tell you about Calandrino and his buddies - a story that'll definitely make you laugh."

Everyone knew Calandrino was kind of a country simpleton with a small farm near Florence. Every year, he and his wife would go to the farm, butcher a pig, and salt it for the winter. This particular year, his wife wasn't feeling well, so Calandrino went alone.

His friends Bruno and Buffalmacco heard he was going solo and immediately started plotting. They stopped by their priest friend's place and waited for an opportunity.

Calandrino, proud of his pig-butchering skills, showed it off to Bruno and Buffalmacco. "Check out this awesome pig!" he bragged.

Bruno had a mischievous glint in his eye. "Dude, you should totally sell this pig and use the money for a night out! Tell your wife it got stolen."

"No way," Calandrino said. "She'd never believe me and would kick me out of the house."

When Calandrino half-heartedly invited them to dinner, Bruno and Buffalmacco declined. But that didn't stop their scheming. "Let's steal his pig tonight," Bruno whispered to Buffalmacco.

Their plan? Get Calandrino drunk at the tavern, then swipe the pig while he's passed out. And that's exactly what they did.

The next morning, Calandrino discovered his pig was gone and started panicking. Bruno and Buffalmacco arrived, pretending to be concerned friends.

"My pig is stolen!" Calandrino wailed.

Bruno played along. "Really? Tell me more about how this happened."

To prove who stole the pig, they proposed a ridiculous "truth test" involving ginger pills. They'd give everyone a pill, and whoever couldn't swallow it would be the thief. Of course, they rigged the game.

When it was Calandrino's turn, they gave him pills laced with an incredibly bitter aloe. Predictably, he couldn't keep them down. The crowd started laughing, convinced Calandrino himself had stolen the pig.

Cornered, Calandrino finally surrendered. To keep his wife from finding out and to shut Bruno and Buffalmacco up, he gave them two pairs of chickens.

The pranksters left town with the pig and the chickens, leaving Calandrino to stew in his embarrassment.
