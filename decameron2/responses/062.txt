The Fourth Story: How a Clever Cook Turned Anger into Laughter

After Lauretta finished her tale and everyone had praised Nonna's story, the group leader asked Neifile to share her story next. With a smile, she began:

"Sometimes, a quick wit can save you in the most unexpected moments. And sometimes, pure luck hands you the perfect comeback - something you could never have planned."

Currado Gianfigliazzi was a well-known guy in our city - generous, adventurous, and crazy about hunting. One day, he caught a young, plump crane and sent it to his cook, Chichibio, a Venetian who looked about as sharp as a butter knife.

"Roast this for dinner," Currado told him. "Make it perfect."

Chichibio got to work, carefully preparing the bird. Just as it was almost done and smelling amazing, his girlfriend Brunetta walked into the kitchen. She caught a whiff of the roasting crane and immediately asked for a piece.

"No way," Chichibio sang, playfully.

Brunetta wasn't having it. "Give me some, or you'll never get anything from me again," she threatened.

After some back-and-forth, Chichibio - not wanting to upset her - cut off one of the crane's legs and gave it to her.

Later that evening, when Currado and his guests sat down to eat, they noticed something weird: the crane was missing a leg. Currado called Chichibio over.

"What happened to the other leg?" he demanded.

Without missing a beat, Chichibio replied, "Sir, cranes only have one leg."

Currado was stunned. "Are you serious? One leg? I've seen plenty of cranes before!"

"I'll prove it to you," Chichibio said confidently. "Just wait until tomorrow morning."

The next day, Currado - still angry - took Chichibio to a riverside where cranes liked to hang out. "Let's see this 'one-legged' crane," he growled.

To Chichibio's incredible luck, they spotted a group of cranes standing on one leg - which is how they sometimes rest. "See?" Chichibio pointed. "One-legged, just like I said!"

Currado wasn't buying it. He shouted "Hey!" which made the cranes drop their other leg and fly away.

"So, they DO have two legs!" Currado laughed.

But Chichibio wasn't done. "Well," he said, "you didn't yell 'Hey!' at last night's crane. If you had, that one would've dropped its other leg too!"

Currado was so tickled by Chichibio's quick thinking that his anger melted into laughter. "You know what? You might be right," he chuckled.

And just like that, Chichibio's wit saved him from serious trouble.
