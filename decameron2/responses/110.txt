The Third Story: A Lesson in Generosity

Everyone thought it was almost unbelievable that someone—especially a well-known person—could be truly generous. But as the ladies finished discussing the previous story, the group leader asked Filostrato to tell his tale.

"Let me tell you about an incredible story of unexpected kindness," Filostrato began. "It's about a man who was willing to help someone who actually wanted to kill him—and might have done so, if things had gone differently."

In a region called Cattajo, there lived a wealthy man named Nathan. He was famous for his incredible hospitality. Nathan's estate was perfectly located on a major travel route, and he had built an amazing palace where he welcomed travelers from all over. Unlike most wealthy people who become stingy as they get older, Nathan continued to be generous and kind to everyone who passed through.

His reputation spread far and wide, eventually reaching a young man named Mithridanes. Mithridanes was also wealthy and became jealous of Nathan's fame. He decided he would prove he was even more generous, so he built his own palace and started offering massive hospitality to travelers.

One day, something funny happened. A poor woman came to Mithridanes' palace and asked for help thirteen times. When he finally commented on her persistence, she compared him unfavorably to Nathan, saying that at Nathan's palace, she could ask for help thirty-two times without being recognized.

This comparison made Mithridanes furious. "How can I ever be as generous as Nathan?" he thought. "The only way to prove I'm better is to get rid of him."

So Mithridanes traveled to Nathan's estate, planning to kill him. But when he arrived, he didn't know what Nathan looked like. He asked a random person (who was actually Nathan himself) for directions to Nathan's house.

Nathan, without revealing his identity, cheerfully offered to help. He even helped Mithridanes stay at the palace secretly, making sure no one would tell the young man who he really was.

When Mithridanes finally revealed his murderous plan to Nathan, something extraordinary happened. Instead of being angry, Nathan was impressed by the young man's ambition to be truly generous. He even gave Mithridanes advice on how to find and kill him!

In the woods, when Mithridanes finally confronted Nathan, he realized he couldn't go through with murdering the man who had been so kind to him. Overcome with shame, he broke down and apologized.

Nathan's response was remarkable. He didn't just forgive Mithridanes—he praised his ambitious spirit and explained that seeking fame through great deeds was a noble pursuit. "The greatest leaders in history expanded their kingdoms through bold actions," he said.

In the end, Mithridanes was so moved by Nathan's wisdom and generosity that his desire to kill him transformed into deep respect and friendship.

"Sometimes," Nathan told him, "the most powerful way to defeat an enemy is to show them unexpected kindness."
