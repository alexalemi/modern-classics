The Third Story: How a Foolish Painter Got Tricked by His Clever Friends

Let me tell you about Calandrino, quite possibly the most naive painter in all of Florence. He hung out with two other painters, Bruno and Buffalmacco, who found endless entertainment in his ridiculous gullibility.

One day, a local prankster named Maso del Saggio spotted Calandrino checking out some artwork in the church of San Giovanni. Seeing an opportunity for some epic trolling, Maso started a conversation with a friend about magical stones, speaking with such convincing authority that Calandrino couldn't help but listen in.

"You won't believe where the most incredible stones come from," Maso said loudly. "There's this amazing place called Bengodi, where everything is totally wild. The vines are tied up with sausages, you can buy a goose for pocket change, and there's an entire mountain made of grated Parmesan cheese!"

Calandrino, wide-eyed and completely believing every word, asked, "Really? Tell me more!"

Maso continued spinning his outrageous tale, describing a magical land where people spent all day making pasta and tossing it down a mountain, with a river of the finest wine running nearby. And the best part? A special stone called the heliotrope that could make you invisible.

"The heliotrope?" Calandrino asked eagerly. "Where can I find one?"

"Oh, they're all over the Mugnone River," Maso said with a straight face. "Just look for black stones."

Calandrino was beyond excited. He immediately tracked down Bruno and Buffalmacco, sharing his "brilliant" plan to get rich by finding this magical stone. "We'll become invisible and just walk into money exchanges and take whatever we want!" he proclaimed.

His friends played along, barely containing their laughter. They agreed to go stone hunting the following Sunday, knowing exactly how this would play out.

Sunday morning arrived, and the trio headed to the Mugnone River. Calandrino, more enthusiastic than a kid in a candy store, started grabbing every black stone he could find, stuffing them into his clothes until he could barely move.

Bruno and Buffalmacco, seeing he was fully loaded down, decided to have some fun. They started pretending they couldn't see Calandrino, talking about how he'd abandoned them and gone home.

Calandrino, believing he'd found the magical heliotrope that made him invisible, triumphantly headed home. When he arrived, his wife Tessa was waiting and immediately started lecturing him about being late for dinner.

Big mistake.

Calandrino, thinking the "magic" was ruined because his wife could see him, flew into a rage. He beat her thoroughly, convinced that she had somehow broken the stone's power.

When Bruno and Buffalmacco caught up, they found Calandrino surrounded by stones, his wife bruised and crying, still playing along with the joke. They listened to his ridiculous story about the magical stone, barely holding back their laughter.

In the end, they "consoled" Calandrino and left him disconsolate, his house full of worthless stones and his pride thoroughly wounded.
