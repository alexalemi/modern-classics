THE EIGHTH STORY: When Your Uncle Drops the Ultimate Burn

Let me tell you about Fresco and his niece Ciesca—a perfect example of how some people just can't take a hint.

Ciesca was one of those people who thought she was way more impressive than she actually was. She had a decent face and figure, but boy, did she have an attitude. She constantly criticized everyone around her, acting like she was royalty, making disgusted faces whenever someone walked by, as if everyone else was beneath her.

One holiday afternoon, she dramatically stormed home early and plopped down next to her uncle Fresco, sighing and huffing dramatically. 

"What's going on?" Fresco asked. "Why are you home so early?"

"Ugh," she replied, "This city is unbearable today. There are SO many annoying people everywhere. Every single person on the street is completely intolerable. I'm certain no one in the world gets more irritated by ugly, disagreeable people than I do."

Fresco, who was definitely not impressed by her performance, shot back with the most epic comeback: "If ugly people bother you so much, maybe you shouldn't look in the mirror."

Ciesca, completely missing the point, just said she'd keep looking in the mirror like any other woman. And that, my friends, is how she remained—totally clueless about her own ridiculousness.
