Here Begins the Third Day of the Decameron: A Day of Stories About People Who, Through Hard Work and Creativity, Achieved Their Dreams or Recovered Something Precious

As the sunrise painted the sky in soft oranges and pinks, the queen woke up and gathered her friends. The event coordinator had already prepared everything perfectly - packing supplies, arranging transportation, making sure everything was ready for their day's adventure.

The group set out along a quiet path lined with green herbs and blooming flowers. Nightingales and other birds sang around them, creating a cheerful morning soundtrack. They walked and chatted, laughing and joking, covering about a mile before mid-morning.

Soon they arrived at a stunning palace perched on a small hill. The building was incredible - grand halls, elegant rooms, everything perfectly arranged. They were impressed by how magnificent the place was, especially its spacious courtyard and wine cellars stocked with the finest drinks.

They settled into a flower-filled gallery overlooking the courtyard, where the event coordinator served them delicious treats and excellent wine. Then they explored an amazing walled garden that took their breath away.

The garden was like something out of a dream. Vine-covered walkways created perfect green tunnels, with roses and jasmine growing along the sides. The air was filled with incredible fragrances - like walking through the most beautiful spice markets of the world. No matter how hot the sun got, these paths remained cool and shaded.

At the garden's center was an extraordinary feature: a perfectly manicured grassy area surrounded by orange and citron trees. These trees were magical, bearing both old and new fruits, creating a mix of colors and scents. A stunning marble fountain stood in the middle, shooting water so high it could have powered a mill, with the water flowing through hidden channels that eventually powered two mills outside the garden.

But the most delightful surprise? The garden was full of friendly animals. Rabbits hopped around, deer grazed peacefully, and various gentle creatures wandered about as if they were part of a living painting.

After exploring, they ate a wonderful meal by the fountain, then spent the afternoon singing, dancing, and enjoying each other's company. Some took naps, while others read books or played chess.

As the afternoon wore on, they gathered again near the fountain, ready to share stories - just as they had done on previous days.
