The First Story

When Honor Meets Opportunity

Let me tell you something fascinating about recognition and luck—a story that proves talent doesn't always get the credit it deserves.

Back in the day, there was this incredible knight named Ruggieri de' Figiovanni. He was wealthy, ambitious, and realized that staying in Tuscany wouldn't let him show off his true potential. So, he made a bold move: he packed up his gear, gathered some top-notch horses and teammates, and headed to Spain to serve King Alfonso, who was basically the rock star of rulers at the time.

Ruggieri wasn't just another knight. He was exceptional. Within a short time, he became known for incredible military skills and an impressive lifestyle. But here's where things get interesting: he started noticing something weird about how the king distributed rewards. The king seemed to be giving out castles, cities, and titles randomly—often to people who didn't really deserve them.

And guess who wasn't getting anything? Ruggieri.

Feeling frustrated that his incredible talents weren't being recognized, Ruggieri decided to leave. When he told the king he was heading out, the king did something surprising—he gave Ruggieri a magnificent mule for his journey and secretly arranged for one of his servants to travel with him and report back on everything Ruggieri said.

During their trip, Ruggieri made an offhand comment that would change everything. When they stopped and all the animals relieved themselves—except the mule—Ruggieri muttered, "God, what a useless creature. Just like the prince who gave it to me—doing things at all the wrong times!"

Little did he know, the king's spy heard every word.

When Ruggieri was called back to court, the king confronted him about the comment. Instead of being angry, the king was curious. "Why did you compare me to this mule?" he asked.

Ruggieri, being totally honest, replied, "Well, my lord, just like this mule doesn't relieve itself when and where it should, you seem to give rewards to the wrong people at the wrong times."

Instead of getting offended, the king saw an opportunity to prove his fairness. He brought out two locked chests and told Ruggieri to choose one—one containing the royal treasures, the other filled with dirt. "Pick whichever you want," he said.

Ruggieri chose a chest. When opened, it revealed—dirt.

The king laughed. "See? It's not my fault. It's just your bad luck!" Then, to make up for it, he gave Ruggieri the chest of dirt, saying, "Take this back to Tuscany. Let everyone see how fortune works—sometimes brilliance gets overlooked, but true worth always shines through."

And that's exactly what Ruggieri did—returning home with a chest of dirt and an incredible story of wit, honesty, and royal humor.
