The Third Story: How Calandrino Got Pranked

After Elisa finished her story, and everyone thanked God for helping the young nun escape her mean-spirited colleagues, the group leader asked Filostrato to continue. Without hesitation, he began:

"Listen up, folks. Remember that story about Calandrino I was about to tell yesterday? Even though people have talked about him before, anything involving this guy is guaranteed to be hilarious. So here we go.

By now, you probably know who Calandrino is - but let me refresh your memory. His aunt had recently died and left him two hundred gold coins. Instead of being smart about it, he started dreaming about buying land, talking to every real estate broker in Florence like he was some big-shot investor. But every deal fell through.

His buddies Bruno and Buffalmacco knew exactly what was going on. They'd been trying to convince him to spend the money having fun with them, but Calandrino was super tight with his cash. So one day, they hatched a plan with their painter friend Nello to finally get a free meal out of him.

The next morning, they set their prank in motion. Nello approached Calandrino and gave him a weird look. 'Hey, you okay?' he asked. 'You look... different.'

Calandrino, who was always jumpy, immediately started panicking. 'What? What's wrong with me?'

Buffalmacco joined in, adding dramatic flair. 'Wow, you look terrible. Are you sick?'

By now, Calandrino was completely convinced something was seriously wrong with him. Bruno stepped in with the perfect solution: 'We should get you to a doctor. Let's go see Master Simone - he'll know exactly what's happening.'

They dragged Calandrino home, put him in bed, and sent a sample of his urine to the doctor. Master Simone, playing along perfectly, examined the sample and delivered the most ridiculous diagnosis possible: Calandrino was "pregnant."

Calandrino lost it. He started yelling at his wife Tessa, blaming her for his "condition." His poor, embarrassed wife just blushed and left the room.

'How will I even give birth?' Calandrino wailed. 'I'll definitely die!'

His friends were dying of suppressed laughter. The doctor prescribed a fake "cure" - some herbal drink and three pairs of expensive capons. Calandrino, totally panicked, handed over all his inheritance money.

After a few days of this elaborate joke, the "doctor" declared Calandrino "cured." Our gullible hero went around town bragging about how Master Simone had miraculously "unburdened" him, while his friends laughed behind his back.

And Tessa? She figured out the whole thing and gave her husband an earful later."
