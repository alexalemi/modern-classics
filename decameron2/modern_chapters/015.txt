The Second Story: A Traveler's Unexpected Night

Let me tell you about Rinaldo d'Asti, a merchant whose incredible journey proves that sometimes, when things look their worst, luck is just around the corner.

Rinaldo was traveling between cities when he ran into three guys who looked like merchants but were actually professional thieves. These smooth-talking criminals chatted him up, gaining his trust while secretly planning to rob him.

During their conversation, they started talking about prayers and spiritual protection. Rinaldo shared that he always says a morning prayer to Saint Julian, asking for safe travels and a good place to sleep each night. He firmly believed this prayer had saved him during many previous journeys.

The thieves listened carefully, with one of them thinking to himself, "We'll see how well that prayer works tonight."

Sure enough, as evening approached, the three men attacked Rinaldo, stealing his money, horse, and clothes. They left him barefoot and shivering in his shirt, mockingly telling him to see if Saint Julian would provide him a good place to stay.

Rinaldo's own servant - proving himself to be a total coward - rode away and abandoned him, heading to the nearby town of Castel Guglielmo without even trying to help.

Cold, scared, and alone, Rinaldo trudged toward the town. But by the time he arrived, the gates were closed for the night. Desperate and desperate, he found a small sheltered area near a house and huddled down, feeling miserable and wondering why Saint Julian seemed to have abandoned him.

Little did he know, his luck was about to change dramatically.

The house belonged to a beautiful widow who was actually the mistress of the local Marquis. That very night, the Marquis was supposed to visit her but had been called away unexpectedly. When she heard Rinaldo shivering outside, she took pity on him.

Her servant brought Rinaldo inside, gave him a warm bath, and dressed him in her late husband's clothes. The widow, finding herself unexpectedly alone and attracted to the handsome, grateful traveler, decided to make the most of the situation.

One thing led to another, and they spent a passionate night together. Come morning, she gave Rinaldo some clothes, money, and directions to find his servant.

As if by miracle, the very thieves who robbed Rinaldo were caught that same day and forced to return his horse and belongings. Rinaldo returned home, grateful to Saint Julian and amazed by how quickly his fortunes had changed.

Sometimes, it seems, a little faith - and a lot of luck - can turn everything around.
