But Francesco, blinded by his desire for the horse, agreed.

What happened next was pure genius.

Ricciardo sat with the wife, knowing Francesco had ordered her to stay completely silent. So he started talking... to himself. But really, he was talking to her. He dramatically proclaimed his undying love, describing how she was the most beautiful woman in the world. He spoke about his passion, his devotion - all while pretending to have a conversation with himself.

And here's the brilliant part: even though she couldn't respond, her body language spoke volumes. Subtle sighs. Tiny glances. Her silence was actually communicating everything.

Ricciardo, playing both sides of the conversation, essentially seduced her by talking to himself. He imagined her response, describing a passionate future rendezvous when her husband would be away in Milan.

Francesco, listening nearby, thought his wife was a model of virtue. He was so impressed by her "silence" that he happily handed over the horse.

What Francesco didn't realize was that Ricciardo had just set the perfect trap. When the husband left for Milan, the wife - suddenly feeling adventurous - sent the pre-arranged signal. Ricciardo came over, and let's just say they made the most of Francesco's absence.

The moral? Sometimes the cleverest lovers don't need words - just imagination and perfect timing.
