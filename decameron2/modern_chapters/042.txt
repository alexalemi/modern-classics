The Sixth Story: A Dream's Dark Prophecy

In the city of Brescia, there lived a young woman named Andrevuola, whose heart belonged to Gabriotto, a man of humble origins but incredible character. Despite her father's potential disapproval, they had secretly married themselves, bound by a love deeper than social expectations.

One night, Andrevuola had a terrifying dream. In it, she held Gabriotto close in her father's garden, feeling pure joy - until something dark and monstrous emerged from his body, violently tearing him away from her and dragging him underground. She woke up shaken, an unexplainable dread settling into her bones.

The next evening, despite her fears, Gabriotto visited her in the garden. Surrounded by freshly picked roses, they sat by a beautiful fountain. Andrevuola, unable to keep her anxiety hidden, told him about her nightmare.

Gabriotto laughed it off. "Dreams are nonsense," he said. "They come from eating too much or too little. I had a weird dream myself - about hunting a pure white deer that I loved so much I put a golden collar on her. Then a scary black dog appeared and somehow tore my heart out."

But something felt different that night. As they held each other, Gabriotto suddenly gasped, "My love, help me - I'm dying!" And just like that, he collapsed in her arms.

Devastated, Andrevuola realized her worst fears had come true. With her serving maid's help, she carefully wrapped Gabriotto's body in a silk cloth, adorned him with roses, and decided to bring him to his family, hoping they would give him a proper burial.

Their journey was interrupted by city officials who arrested them. At the courthouse, Andrevuola bravely explained everything. The medical experts confirmed Gabriotto had died from a heart condition, not foul play.

The court's provost, seeing Andrevuola's beauty and dignity, tried to take advantage of her vulnerable position. But she fiercely defended herself, rejecting his inappropriate advances.

Her father, hearing the story, was initially heartbroken but ultimately recognized his daughter's pure love. He ensured Gabriotto received an honorable funeral, with the entire city mourning.

In the end, overwhelmed by grief and seeking peace, Andrevuola and her faithful maid became nuns, dedicating their lives to remembrance and spiritual reflection.
