The Fifth Story

How One Lost Daughter Found Her Family

The storytellers were still chuckling about the previous tale when the group's leader turned to Neifile and asked her to share her story. With a smile, she began:

"Let me tell you about something that happened in Faenza, a city with quite the complicated history.

Back when the city was recovering from years of conflict, two older veterans named Guidotto from Cremona and Giacomino from Pavia had settled down. Guidotto, knowing he was close to death and trusting no one more than his friend Giacomino, left him a ten-year-old daughter and all his possessions.

Giacomino took the girl to Faenza and raised her as his own. She grew into an absolutely stunning young woman - beautiful, kind, and well-respected. Two local guys, Giannole and Minghino, both fell madly in love with her and became fierce rivals.

Neither could officially marry her, so they started plotting. Giannole made friends with Crivello, a servant in Giacomino's house, and convinced him to help him meet the girl. At the same time, Minghino had already gotten the maid on his side and was making his own plans.

One evening, when Giacomino was out to dinner, both guys showed up with their friends, each thinking he'd be the one to win the girl. Total chaos broke out - shouting, fighting, accusations flying. Neighbors came out with lights and weapons, taking sides in the drama.

The town captain eventually arrested everyone involved, including Giannole, Minghino, and Crivello. When Giacomino returned and heard what happened, he was furious but realized the young woman wasn't to blame.

The next day, the families of the young men came to Giacomino, begging him to be merciful. Giacomino surprised everyone by revealing an amazing backstory: the girl wasn't actually from Cremona or Pavia, but from Faenza itself - though no one knew her real family.

He explained how he'd gotten her: During a war, when the city was attacked, his friend Guidotto found her alone in a house, a scared two-year-old who called him 'father'. Moved with compassion, he took her with him.

A local man named Bernabuccio heard this story and remembered losing a daughter during those same chaotic times. When they checked, they discovered a small cross-shaped scar behind her ear - proof she was his long-lost daughter!

The reunion was emotional. Bernabuccio's entire family welcomed her back, and the town captain - showing remarkable wisdom - realized that Giannole was actually her brother. He released the prisoners and, in a final twist, arranged for Minghino to marry the girl, whose name was Agnesa.

And so, what started as a wild kidnapping attempt ended in a joyful wedding, with everyone celebrating this unexpected happy ending."
