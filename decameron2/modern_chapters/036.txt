Day Four: A Personal Preface

Look, I need to clear something up. 

I used to think jealousy and criticism only targeted the most successful or prominent people. Turns out, I was totally wrong. No matter how carefully I've tried to write these stories - in simple language, without making a big deal about who wrote them - I can't escape the haters.

Some people are seriously criticizing me for a few different reasons:
- They think I'm too interested in entertaining women
- They believe I'm too old to be writing these kinds of stories
- They suggest I should be doing something more "serious" with my time
- They're claiming my stories aren't even accurate

Well, let me tell you a story that explains exactly why I do what I do.

There was once a man named Filippo Balducci, a regular guy who deeply loved his wife. When she died, leaving him with only a young son, he decided to become a hermit. He took his boy to live on a mountain, raising him in total isolation and teaching him only about religious devotion.

Years passed, and when the boy turned eighteen, Filippo finally brought him to Florence. Imagine never having seen civilization before! The young man was amazed by everything - buildings, streets, people.

But then something hilarious happened. They encountered a group of beautiful young women, and the sheltered son immediately became fascinated. When Filippo tried to discourage him, calling the women "green geese" and saying they were "bad," the son became even more intrigued.

"Father," he asked, "if they're so bad, why are they so beautiful? I want one of these green geese!"

In that moment, Filippo realized that nature - human attraction, curiosity, passion - was far more powerful than any attempt to suppress it.

And that's exactly why I write these stories. Because human nature is complicated, beautiful, and impossible to control. Because love and attraction aren't things to be ashamed of - they're what make us human.

To those who criticize me: I'm not going to stop writing. I'm not going to apologize for finding women fascinating, for wanting to tell stories that celebrate human emotion and experience. Great writers like Dante and Cavalcanti understood this. Why shouldn't I?

So here we are, ready to begin another day of storytelling. Let the critics talk - I'll keep doing what I love.
