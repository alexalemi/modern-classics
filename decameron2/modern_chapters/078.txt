The Ninth Story: A Wife's Clever Game

Let me tell you about a story that proves love can make people do absolutely anything - even the most ridiculous things imaginable.

In the ancient Greek city of Argos, there lived a wealthy nobleman named Nicostratus and his much younger wife, Lydia. Nicostratus was getting older, but he still enjoyed hunting and keeping a fancy household. Among his servants was a young man named Pyrrhus - handsome, skilled, and completely trusted by his master.

Here's where things get interesting: Lydia was totally head over heels for Pyrrhus. We're talking can't-think-about-anything-else, completely obsessed kind of love. The problem? Pyrrhus seemed totally oblivious - or was purposely ignoring her signals.

Desperate to get his attention, Lydia confided in her trusted servant, Lusca. "Look," she said, "I'm young, I'm attractive, and I'm stuck with an old husband. I want Pyrrhus, and I'm going to make that happen."

Lusca approached Pyrrhus with the message, but he was suspicious. "No way," he told her. "I would never betray my master. This must be some kind of test."

But Lydia wasn't giving up. She had Lusca convince Pyrrhus by arguing that servants shouldn't be more loyal to masters than masters are to servants. "If the roles were reversed," Lusca told him, "Nicostratus wouldn't hesitate."

Pyrrhus finally agreed, but with three bizarre conditions: Lydia had to kill Nicostratus's prized hawk in front of him, cut a lock of his beard, and pull out one of his teeth - all to prove her serious intentions.

Challenge accepted.

At a dinner party, Lydia dramatically killed the hawk, claiming it had stolen her husband's attention. The guests were shocked, but she spun such a convincing story about wanting revenge that everyone, including Nicostratus, ended up laughing.

Then, during a playful moment with her husband, she "accidentally" yanked out a chunk of his beard while they were horsing around. She made it seem like pure accident, and Nicostratus was none the wiser.

Pyrrhus watched, increasingly impressed by Lydia's incredible scheme. Would she complete her mission? Only time would tell...
