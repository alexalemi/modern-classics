The Ninth Story: Two Guys, Some Advice, and a Really Weird Solution

Look, here's a wild story about wisdom, relationships, and what happens when you take advice from a legendary king.

Back in the day, Solomon was famous worldwide for being seriously smart. People would travel from all over just to get his advice on their toughest problems. Which is exactly what two young men did.

Melisso was this wealthy guy from Lajazzo, and Giosefo was traveling with similar travel plans. During their journey, they started chatting about why they were heading to see Solomon.

Giosefo went first: "I've got this wife who's impossible. No matter what I do, she just won't listen or cooperate."

Melisso jumped in: "Wild coincidence - I've got my own problem. I'm young, rich, throw amazing parties, and somehow nobody actually likes me. I want to figure out how to get people to genuinely care about me."

When they finally met Solomon, they each got the world's most cryptic advice. Melisso asked how to be loved, and Solomon just said one word: "Love." Giosefo asked how to handle his stubborn wife, and Solomon told him to go to "Goosebridge."

Confused and feeling totally scammed, they started heading home. On the way, they came across a muleteer trying to get a stubborn mule to move. The muleteer was beating the mule with a stick, and our two travelers thought this seemed cruel.

But here's the crazy part - when the mule finally moved, Giosefo had an epiphany. He realized Solomon's advice about "Goosebridge" meant something specific: Sometimes, you've got to be firm to get results.

So when they got home, Giosefo decided to test this theory on his wife. Let's just say it involved a stick, and by the next day, his wife was suddenly super cooperative.

Melisso, hearing the advice to simply "love," realized he'd been performing generosity without genuine feeling. A wise friend told him, "You're doing things for show, not for real connection. Start actually caring about people."

The moral? Sometimes solving relationship problems takes unexpected approaches - and listening to cryptic king's advice might just work... in the weirdest way possible.
