The Eighth Story: A Tragic Love

Some people think they're smarter than everyone else, but usually, they're just stubborn. This is especially true when it comes to love – you can't logic your way out of real feelings. Let me tell you a story about what happens when someone tries to control love.

In Florence years ago, there was a wealthy merchant named Lionardo Sighieri who died shortly after his son Girolamo was born. As Girolamo grew up, he fell deeply in love with Salvestra, the daughter of a local tailor who lived in his neighborhood. Their connection was so strong that they were inseparable, even as kids.

Girolamo's mother wasn't thrilled. She worried her son would marry beneath his social status and ruin his future. "He's only fourteen," she complained to his guardians, "and he's obsessed with a tailor's daughter! We need to get him away from her."

The guardians agreed. They suggested sending Girolamo to Paris to study and expand his horizons. At first, he refused – he was happy in Florence and totally in love with Salvestra. But after his mother's persistent pleading and gentle manipulation, he eventually agreed to go.

In Paris, what was supposed to be a one-year trip turned into two. When Girolamo finally returned home, his world shattered: Salvestra was married to another man, a tent maker.

Heartbroken but hopeful, Girolamo started hanging around her neighborhood, hoping she'd remember him. But Salvestra seemed to have completely moved on, pretending not to know him or care about their past.

One night, when Salvestra and her husband were out, Girolamo broke into their home. Hiding behind some tent cloths, he waited until her husband was asleep. When he approached her, she was terrified.

"Please," she whispered, "we can't be lovers anymore. I'm married now. If my husband finds you here, everything will be ruined."

But Girolamo was desperate. He begged her to let him just lie beside her, promising he wouldn't touch her, just wanting to be near her one last time. Surprisingly, she agreed.

As he lay there, overwhelmed by memories and lost hope, Girolamo made a devastating choice. Focusing all his emotional pain, he simply willed himself to die, right there next to the woman he loved.

When Salvestra realized he was dead, something broke inside her. At the funeral, overcome with grief and regret, she threw herself onto his body and died instantly – her heart quite literally breaking.

In the end, the lovers who couldn't be together in life were united forever in death, buried in the same tomb – a testament to a love so powerful it transcended life itself.
