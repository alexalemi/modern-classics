Day Six: Where Wit Saves the Day

As dawn broke and sunlight started warming the countryside, the group leisurely wandered through the dewy grass, chatting about yesterday's wild stories and laughing about the crazy adventures they'd heard. When the heat started picking up, they headed back to the palace for breakfast.

After enjoying a meal surrounded by fresh flowers and sweet herbs, they sang some songs, played games, and relaxed. Some napped, some played chess, and Dioneo and Lauretta sang about the legendary lovers Troilus and Cressida.

Just as they were about to start their storytelling session, an unexpected commotion erupted in the kitchen. Loud voices and arguments caught everyone's attention.

When the servants Licisca and Tindaro were brought before the queen, Licisca - a sharp-tongued older woman - immediately took control of the conversation.

"Listen up!" she announced. "This young guy thinks he knows everything about women, but he doesn't know the first thing! He claims that when Sicofante's wife got married, her first night was some traumatic experience. But I'm telling you - it was totally fine!"

She continued her rant, basically declaring that none of her female friends were naive virgins and that women knew exactly what they were doing. "I've been around," she boasted. "And let me tell you, no woman is sitting around waiting for her family to decide when she gets married!"

The other women were dying of laughter, and the queen kept trying to quiet Licisca down - but she was on a roll.

Dioneo, always ready with a quip, immediately sided with Licisca. "She's absolutely right," he declared. "Tindaro doesn't know what he's talking about!"

Licisca, thrilled with her victory, turned to Tindaro and basically told him to buzz off - she knew way more than he ever would.

The queen, both amused and exasperated, finally shut down the entire argument and sent them away, warning Licisca to keep quiet or face consequences.

And just like that, it was time to start the day's storytelling.
