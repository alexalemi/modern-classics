The Fourth Story: A Tale of Forbidden Love and Tragic Consequences

Let me tell you about a love so intense it transcended sight, a passion so powerful it would ultimately destroy those who felt it.

Many people believe love only sparks when two people lock eyes. But this story proves that sometimes, reputation alone can kindle the most explosive romance. And trust me, this tale doesn't just challenge that belief—it obliterates it in the most heartbreaking way possible.

King Guglielmo of Sicily had a grandson named Gerbino, a young man whose reputation for courage and charm spread far beyond the island's shores. His fame was especially strong in North Africa, where stories of his heroism were whispered in marketplaces and royal courts.

In Tunisia, the king's daughter had heard these stories. Though she'd never met Gerbino, she became utterly captivated by his legendary reputation. She would listen eagerly to every tale about him, imagining his heroic figure, and slowly, unexpectedly, she fell deeply in love—all without ever seeing his face.

Meanwhile, Gerbino had heard equally breathless stories about her extraordinary beauty and noble spirit. He too found himself falling for someone he'd never met, their connection sparked purely by reputation and imagination.

Their long-distance romance blossomed through secret messages and precious gifts exchanged by trusted messengers. They dreamed of meeting, sharing passionate letters filled with hope and desire.

But fate had other plans.

The Tunisian princess was suddenly betrothed to the King of Granada, a marriage that would separate the lovers by vast distances. Desperate and heartbroken, Gerbino decided he would risk everything to prevent this marriage.

Against his grandfather's explicit promise of safe passage to the other king, Gerbino attacked the princess's ship. In a moment of brutal tragedy, the ship's crew—knowing they were outmatched—killed the princess right before Gerbino's eyes and threw her body into the sea.

Consumed by rage and grief, Gerbino massacred the crew. He retrieved her body, weeping, and buried her with honor on a small island near Sicily.

For breaking his grandfather's word, Gerbino was condemned to death. The king, valuing his reputation for integrity over his love for his grandson, personally ordered Gerbino's execution.

And so, within days of each other, these two lovers who had never truly met died violent, tragic deaths—their passionate romance ending before it could truly begin.
