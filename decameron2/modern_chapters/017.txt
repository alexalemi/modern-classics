The Fourth Story: How Luck Can Change Everything

Most people think life follows a straight path. Landolfo Ruffolo's story proves otherwise.

Once upon a time in southern Italy, near the beautiful Amalfi Coast, there lived a merchant who had it all - and then lost everything. Landolfo was wealthy, successful, and ambitious. But ambition can be a dangerous thing.

When he sailed to Cyprus hoping to make a massive trading deal, disaster struck. Every merchant had the same goods, which meant Landolfo couldn't sell anything at a decent price. Overnight, he went from being a rich businessman to being nearly broke.

Most people would've given up. Not Landolfo.

Desperate and angry, he made a wild decision: if he couldn't make money trading, he'd take what he needed by force. He sold his big trading ship and bought a smaller, faster vessel perfect for... well, piracy.

And surprisingly, it worked. Over the next year, Landolfo became an expert at capturing Turkish merchant ships. He not only recovered his lost wealth but doubled it. Finally feeling secure, he decided to return home.

But the sea has other plans.

A massive storm hit while he was sailing through the Archipelago. Two giant Genoese ships - known for being ruthless - spotted Landolfo and attacked, stealing everything he owned. They left him with nothing but the shirt on his back.

Then things got even worse.

The ship Landolfo was forced onto was destroyed in another violent storm. Waves crashed, wood splintered, and suddenly he was alone in the dark, freezing water, clinging to a wooden plank for dear life.

Hours passed. Exhausted and half-drowned, Landolfo spotted a mysterious chest floating nearby. With his last bit of strength, he grabbed it and somehow survived until morning, when he washed up on the shores of Corfu.

A poor local woman found him - barely alive, more sea than human. Instead of ignoring him, she dragged him to safety, warmed him up, and nursed him back to health. When he recovered, he discovered something miraculous: the chest he'd been clinging to was filled with precious jewels.

Talk about a plot twist.

Landolfo carefully transported the jewels home, shared some of his newfound wealth with the woman who saved him and the people who helped him along the way, and finally settled down. He never traded again, choosing instead to live quietly and gratefully.

Sometimes, survival itself is the greatest fortune.
