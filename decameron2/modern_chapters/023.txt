The Tale of the Kidnapped Wife

Look, let me tell you a story about what happens when you treat your wife like a legal document instead of a human being.

In Pisa, there was this judge named Ricciardo di Chinzica - brilliant with paperwork, absolutely terrible with people. Somehow, he managed to marry Bartolomea, who was gorgeous and way too young for his dried-up, rule-obsessed self.

On their wedding night, Ricciardo barely managed to consummate the marriage. Instead of figuring out how to be a decent husband, he decided to create an insane calendar of days they couldn't have sex: saints' days, feast days, fast days, basically every day he could possibly invent.

Poor Bartolomea was lucky if she got intimate with her husband once a month. The guy treated sex like a complicated legal proceeding, complete with more exceptions than a tax code.

One day, while they were out fishing, a legendary corsair named Paganino spotted Bartolomea and - boom - kidnapped her right in front of her husband. Talk about a plot twist.

Paganino wasn't just some random kidnapper. He was actually smooth about it. Instead of being cruel, he treated Bartolomea with respect and - shocking concept - actually paid attention to her desires.

When Ricciardo finally tracked her down, expecting a tearful reunion, Bartolomea shut that down real quick. She basically told him, "Look, you treated me like a legal document. Paganino treats me like a human being. I'm staying."

She called out Ricciardo's ridiculous approach to marriage: "You were more interested in saints' days and legal technicalities than in actually being a husband. Here, we work hard and love harder."

Ricciardo, totally devastated, returned to Pisa muttering something about "bad luck" and died soon after. Paganino married Bartolomea, and they lived a passionate, holiday-free life.

The moral? Treat your partner like a human being, not a legal contract.
