The Seventh Story: A Clever Seduction

In Paris, there was a wealthy Florentine merchant with a son named Lodovico. Unlike his father, who wanted him to be more than just a businessman, Lodovico was sent to serve in the royal court, where he learned refined manners and social skills.

One day, some travelers were discussing the most beautiful women they'd encountered during their travels. They all agreed that Beatrice, the wife of Egano de' Gulluzzi from Bologna, was absolutely stunning. Lodovico, who had never been seriously interested in any woman before, became instantly fascinated.

Desperate to see this legendary beauty, he convinced his father he wanted to visit the Holy Sepulchre - but his real plan was to go to Bologna. Using the fake name Anichino, he arrived and quickly found a job as a servant in Egano's household.

Lodovico was incredibly strategic. He made himself indispensable to Egano, working so well that his master trusted him completely. One day, while Egano was out hunting, Lodovico saw his chance.

He challenged Beatrice to a chess game, deliberately letting her win. As they played alone, he began sighing dramatically. When she asked what was wrong, he hesitated, building suspense. Then, with tears in his eyes, he confessed his burning love for her.

To his surprise, Beatrice was totally charmed. She'd been courted by many men before, but none had moved her like Lodovico. She promised to meet him that night and even sealed their connection with a passionate kiss.

That evening, Beatrice brilliantly manipulated her husband. She convinced Egano that Lodovico was trying to seduce her, and tricked him into disguising himself as her and waiting in the garden. Meanwhile, she and Lodovico enjoyed their intimate rendezvous.

As a final twist, she sent Lodovico to the garden to "punish" the disguised Egano, beating him with a stick while pretending to scold a would-be seductress. Egano returned home, believing his wife was completely faithful and his servant completely loyal.

In the end, Lodovico got exactly what he wanted - and nobody was the wiser.
