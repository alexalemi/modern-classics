The Incredible Love Story of Gianni and Restituta

Love can be a crazy adventure, as anyone who's ever been young and passionate knows. Sometimes it can lead you into the most unexpected situations - like almost getting executed, which is exactly what happened to Gianni and Restituta.

Gianni was from a tiny island near Naples called Procida, and Restituta lived on the nearby island of Ischia. They were completely head over heels in love with each other. We're talking serious, can't-live-without-you love. Gianni was so devoted that he would regularly swim across the water just to catch a glimpse of Restituta's house - talk about romantic!

One summer day, Restituta was alone on the beach, collecting shellfish, when a group of Sicilian sailors spotted her. Instead of helping her, they decided to kidnap her. They grabbed her and took her aboard their boat, heading to Calabria and then to Palermo.

When they arrived, they decided to present her as a gift to King Frederick of Sicily. The king was young and thought this was an exciting surprise. He was impressed by her beauty but was temporarily dealing with some health issues, so he had her placed in a beautiful garden pavilion called La Cuba, under careful watch.

Back on Ischia, everyone was upset about Restituta's disappearance. But no one was more determined to find her than Gianni. He quickly got his own boat and searched the entire coastline, asking everyone he met if they had seen her.

Eventually, he discovered she was in Palermo, living in the king's garden. Desperate to see her, he managed to sneak into the pavilion one night. Restituta, feeling like she had already lost everything, was ready to escape with him. They were reunited, sharing an incredibly passionate night together.

But their luck ran out. The king, feeling better and curious about the girl, decided to visit her pavilion early in the morning. Imagine his shock when he found Gianni and Restituta asleep together - naked and in each other's arms!

The king was furious. He could have killed them right then, but decided execution would be more public. He ordered them to be taken to Palermo, tied to a stake in the town square, and burned alive.

Just when things looked hopeless, the king's admiral, Ruggieri dell' Oria, recognized Gianni. He knew Gianni's family had been crucial in helping the king maintain power. Ruggieri intervened, explaining to the king that these weren't just random lovers, but people important to the kingdom.

The king, realizing his mistake, not only spared their lives but gave them a wedding and sent them home with gifts. Love had conquered all - even royal anger.
