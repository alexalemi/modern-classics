The Fourth Story: A Remarkable Rescue

Everyone was stunned by the incredible story of Nathan's generosity, which seemed to outshine even the most legendary acts of kindness. After plenty of discussion, the storyteller Lauretta began her tale.

"Listen up," she said to the group, "we've heard some amazing stories, but I want to share something truly special - a story about love that shows how far someone might go for the person they care about."

In Bologna, there lived a remarkable gentleman named Gentile Carisendi. He had fallen deeply in love with a beautiful married woman named Catalina, who was married to Niccoluccio Caccianimico. Though Catalina didn't return his feelings, Gentile remained devoted.

While Niccoluccio was away, Catalina became seriously ill during her pregnancy. Her condition was so severe that doctors declared her dead. Her family, believing the pregnancy wasn't far enough along to worry about the baby, quickly buried her in a nearby church vault.

When Gentile heard about her death, something extraordinary happened. Despite her never having returned his affection, he was devastated. That night, he secretly rode to her burial site, opened the tomb, and discovered something incredible - she wasn't actually dead.

Moved by compassion and his enduring love, Gentile carefully carried Catalina to his home, where his mother nursed her back to health. When she regained consciousness, Catalina was shocked and worried about her reputation. But Gentile promised to protect her honor, swearing he would treat her like a sister.

Days later, Catalina gave birth to a healthy son. Gentile saw this as a sign of hope and decided to do something remarkable. He organized a banquet and invited Niccoluccio and other prominent citizens.

At the feast, Gentile posed a philosophical question: If someone abandons a faithful servant in their time of need, and a stranger rescues and cares for that servant, who truly owns them? The guests unanimously agreed that the rescuer now had the right to the servant.

Then, in a stunning moment, Gentile revealed that the "servant" was Catalina herself. He had rescued her from certain death, cared for her, and now was returning her - completely unharmed - to her husband.

Niccoluccio was overwhelmed with joy, accepting his wife and newborn son. Everyone present was moved to tears by Gentile's incredible act of selflessness.

The story spread through Bologna, with people marveling at how a man could be so noble - loving someone so deeply yet being willing to let them go out of pure kindness.
