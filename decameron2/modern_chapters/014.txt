The First Story

How People Who Try to Fool Others Often End Up Fooling Themselves

Let me tell you something, ladies - sometimes when people try to pull a fast one, especially in sacred spaces, they end up in way more trouble than it's worth. I'm going to share a story about a guy from our town whose crazy adventure went from bad to worse, and then somehow worked out in the end.

Not too long ago in Treviso, there was this German guy named Arrigo. He was super poor and made a living carrying stuff for people. Despite being broke, everyone thought he was an incredibly good person. When he died, something weird happened - the church bells started ringing by themselves. The people of Treviso were convinced this was a miracle and immediately declared him a saint.

Suddenly, everyone was rushing to the cathedral, bringing people with all kinds of disabilities hoping to be healed by touching Arrigo's body. It was total chaos.

Right in the middle of all this excitement, three guys from our town showed up: Stecchi, Martellino, and Marchese. These guys were professional performers who were experts at disguises and making people laugh. They heard about the commotion and decided to check it out.

Marchese was worried they'd never get close to the saint's body because the church was packed with soldiers and people. But Martellino had a plan. "Watch this," he said. "I'll pretend to be totally disabled. You two can help me walk, like you're bringing me to be healed. Nobody will stop us."

His friends thought this was hilarious. So Martellino twisted his body in the most horrifying way - contorting his hands, fingers, arms, legs, and even his face. He looked so convincingly broken that anyone who saw him would swear he was completely paralyzed.

Marchese and Stecchi "helped" him toward the church, making a big show of how difficult it was to move him. People kept making way, feeling sorry for the poor "crippled" man.

They got right up to Saint Arrigo's body, and some gentlemen actually lifted Martellino and placed him on the saint, hoping he'd be miraculously healed. Martellino, being the amazing performer he was, started "recovering" - first moving a finger, then a hand, then an entire arm, until he was completely "cured."

The crowd went wild, praising Saint Arrigo's miraculous powers.

But here's where things got complicated. A Florentine guy who knew Martellino recognized him and started laughing. "That guy? Disabled? No way! He's the best actor in the world!"

The crowd turned on Martellino instantly. "Fraud!" they shouted. "He's mocking our saint!" They grabbed him, stripped his clothes, and started beating him up. His friends Stecchi and Marchese were terrified and pretended not to know him.

Quick-thinking Marchese saved Martellino's life by telling the authorities that Martellino was a pickpocket. This got the police to drag Martellino away from the angry mob.

At the courthouse, things looked bad. The judge was determined to hang Martellino. But Martellino's friends found a local important guy who knew the city's leader. They explained the whole crazy story, and surprisingly, the leader found it all pretty amusing.

In the end, not only did Martellino escape execution, but the leader even gave all three friends new clothes and sent them home safe.

Moral of the story? Sometimes your clever schemes can backfire spectacularly - but if you're lucky, you might just escape with your life and a good story to tell!
