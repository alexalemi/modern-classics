The Seventh Story: A Winter's Revenge

Let me tell you about a wild tale of love, trickery, and payback that happened right in Florence not too long ago. 

Elena was a young widow with attitude - beautiful, wealthy, and definitely not interested in remarrying. Instead, she was totally into a secret boyfriend and loved playing games with anyone who caught her eye. And boy, did she catch the eye of Rinieri, a super-smart scholar who'd just returned from studying in Paris.

Rinieri wasn't like other academics who just wanted to sell their knowledge. He was genuinely curious about the world and loved learning for learning's sake. When he first saw Elena at a party, dressed in elegant black widow's clothing, he was completely smitten. He decided right then and there that he was going to win her over.

Elena, who was always aware of who was looking at her, quickly noticed Rinieri. "Well, well," she thought to herself, "looks like I've got a nice little catch here." She started flirting subtly, giving him just enough attention to keep him interested.

Rinieri went all out. He found out where she lived, started walking by her house, and even got friendly with her maid. Through the maid, he sent love letters and gifts, hoping to impress Elena. Little did he know, Elena was playing him like a fiddle.

Her secret boyfriend was getting a bit jealous, so Elena decided to teach Rinieri a lesson. She set up an elaborate trap: She invited Rinieri to meet her on a cold winter's night, promising a romantic rendezvous. But when he arrived, expecting to finally be with her, she had other plans.

It had snowed hard that day, and Rinieri waited in the freezing courtyard, believing her story about a brother preventing their meeting. Meanwhile, Elena was cuddled up with her actual boyfriend, laughing about how they were torturing the poor scholar.

Hours passed. Rinieri jumped around to keep warm, his teeth chattering so hard he could barely speak. When the maid finally let him out in the morning, he was nearly frozen solid.

The scholars and doctors who treated him afterwards were amazed he survived. But Rinieri wasn't defeated - he was plotting his revenge.
