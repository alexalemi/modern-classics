THE NINTH STORY: A LOVE WORTH MORE THAN MONEY

Let me tell you about Federigo degli Alberighi, a guy who proves that true love isn't about what you own, but how big your heart is.

Federigo was a young man from Florence who was totally head over heels for Giovanna, easily the most beautiful woman in the city. He was the kind of guy who would do anything to win her attention - hosting fancy tournaments, giving expensive gifts, spending every cent he had. But here's the thing: Giovanna wasn't impressed. She was too classy and independent to be swayed by flashy displays.

Eventually, Federigo's wild spending caught up with him. He went from being a wealthy bachelor to basically broke, with nothing left except a small farm and - get this - an incredibly awesome falcon that he absolutely treasured. Talk about falling hard.

When Giovanna's husband died, he left her with one son whom she loved more than anything in the world. The boy got sick, and during his illness, he became obsessed with one crazy wish: he wanted Federigo's falcon. Not just any bird - specifically Federigo's falcon.

Giovanna was torn. She knew Federigo had spent years loving her without getting so much as a glance in return. And now she had to ask him for his most prized possession? Awkward.

But her love for her son was stronger than her embarrassment. So she went to Federigo's humble home, and here's where the story gets wild. When she arrived, Federigo was so thrilled to see her that he wanted to make her an incredible lunch. Problem was, he was completely broke and had nothing to serve her.

His solution? He killed his beloved falcon and cooked it for her lunch.

When Giovanna later asked for the falcon for her son, Federigo broke down crying. He had already served the bird as their meal. Can you imagine? He loved her so much that he literally cooked his only valuable possession just to show her hospitality.

Giovanna was stunned. She realized Federigo's love was so pure, so genuine, that he would sacrifice his most treasured thing without hesitation.

Tragically, her son died soon after. But something remarkable happened: Giovanna decided she would only marry Federigo. When her brothers protested that he was poor, she famously said, "I'd rather have a man who lacks money than money that lacks a man."

They married. Federigo, now wealthy through her inheritance, became a responsible husband. And just like that, his endless devotion transformed his entire life.
