The Tenth Story: How a Clever Doctor Turned the Tables on Someone Trying to Embarrass Him

As the previous storyteller finished, the queen took her turn to speak. With a graceful smile, she addressed the group:

"Look, everyone knows that just like stars make the night sky beautiful and flowers transform spring meadows, great conversation is made amazing by clever wit. And quick, sharp responses are especially awesome when they come from women.

Let's be real - these days, most people think women aren't funny or smart. Everyone seems more interested in looking good than being interesting. People would rather have fancy clothes than a clever mind. It's like they think wearing the most elaborate outfit makes them special - as if an overdecorated outfit is some kind of achievement!"

She chuckled and continued, "I'm going to be totally honest. A lot of women today think being quiet and boring is the same as being modest. They'd have you believe that only talking to their housekeepers makes them respectable. But that's nonsense.

The real trick is knowing when to speak, how to speak, and who you're speaking to. Sometimes people try to embarrass someone else and end up embarrassing themselves instead. So listen up - I'm about to tell you a story that shows exactly how to handle social situations with style."

The story she told was about Master Alberto, a famous doctor in Bologna who was almost seventy years old but still had a playful spirit. Despite his age, he found himself totally crushing on a beautiful young widow named Margherita.

He would constantly pass by her house, hoping to catch a glimpse of her. The local ladies found this hilarious - an old man acting like a lovesick teenager! One day, they decided to have some fun with him.

When Master Alberto approached, they invited him to join them, planning to tease him about his romantic pursuits. "Tell us," they asked with mischievous smiles, "how could an older gentleman like yourself be in love with our friend, when so many young, handsome men are competing for her attention?"

Master Alberto wasn't about to be embarrassed. With a clever twinkle in his eye, he responded, "Well, ladies, let me explain something about love. Just like with leeks - where the bottom part tastes best - sometimes the most experienced lover is the wisest choice."

He went on to explain that while young men might seem more attractive, older men bring knowledge, understanding, and real appreciation. The ladies were stunned - they'd tried to mock him, and instead, he'd completely turned the tables.

Impressed and slightly embarrassed, they graciously accepted his witty response. Master Alberto had not just defended himself - he'd made them look foolish in the process.

The moral? Never try to embarrass someone unless you're absolutely certain you can pull it off!"
