The Eighth Story: A Wife's Clever Escape

Look, everyone agreed that Beatrice was brilliant at tricking her husband, and poor Anichino must have been terrified when she claimed he was trying to seduce her. 

Here's a wild story from our city: There was this wealthy merchant named Arriguccio Berlinghieri who, like many merchants of his time, thought marrying a young gentlewoman would make him seem more refined. He married Sismonda, a woman way out of his league. Since he was always traveling and rarely home, she started an affair with a guy named Ruberto who'd been pursuing her for ages.

Arriguccio became insanely jealous. He'd barely sleep, and when he did, he'd only drift off after making absolutely sure his wife was in bed next to him. This drove Sismonda crazy - she was desperate to see her lover.

So she came up with a brilliant plan. She noticed Arriguccio took forever to fall asleep but then slept like a rock. Her scheme? She'd tie a piece of string from her bedroom window to her big toe. When Ruberto pulled the string, she'd know to let him in while her husband was out cold.

One night, everything went wrong. Arriguccio felt the string, realized something was up, and switched it to his own toe. When Ruberto pulled the string, Arriguccio jumped up and chased him through the streets. Ruberto managed to escape, but Arriguccio was furious.

Back home, Arriguccio was seeing red. Instead of confronting his wife, he beat up her maid, cutting off her hair, thinking it was Sismonda. The poor maid took the entire beating, promised payment by Sismonda to stay quiet.

When Arriguccio dragged her brothers over to accuse her, Sismonda was ready. She played innocent perfectly. "Me? Beaten? Look at my hair, look at my face!" She turned the tables, accusing him of being drunk and mistaking some other woman for her.

Her mother went ballistic, defending her daughter and attacking Arriguccio. Her brothers threatened him to never speak of this again.

In the end, Sismonda not only escaped trouble but gained total freedom to do whatever she wanted. Her quick thinking and total commitment to her performance saved her - and then some.
