THE PARISH PRIEST'S SNEAKY SCHEME

Let me tell you a story about the village priest who thought he was way smarter than everyone else—but wasn't.

In a small village just outside town, there was this priest who was notorious for paying a little too much attention to the local women. Sure, he wasn't the best at reading, but he was an expert at flirting. Every Sunday, he'd hang out by the elm tree, dropping "holy" wisdom and making sure to visit all the wives when their husbands were away.

His absolute favorite was Belcolore, a total knockout of a country woman. She was married to a farmer named Bentivegna, and she was known for being great at everything—singing, dancing, and looking amazing while doing farm work. The priest was completely obsessed with her.

He'd try to win her over with fancy gifts: the most perfect garlic from his garden, fresh pea pods, and chives. He'd give her these little presents and try to catch her eye during church, singing extra dramatically whenever she was around. But Belcolore? She played hard to get, pretending not to notice him.

One day, Bentivegna announced he was heading into town on business. The priest saw his chance and raced over to Belcolore's house. "Your husband's away," he said with a sly grin. "How about we spend some time together?"

Belcolore wasn't stupid. She knew exactly what he wanted. "Five crowns," she said. "Lend me five crowns, and maybe we can talk."

The priest, desperate to seal the deal, agreed. But instead of cash, he left his expensive blue cloak as a promise. They sneaked into the barn, and well... let's just say the priest got exactly what he wanted.

But here's where it gets good. The next day, the priest wanted his cloak back. So he sent a boy to Belcolore with her stone mortar, knowing her husband would force her to return the cloak if the priest requested it.

Sure enough, Bentivegna erupted: "You took a pledge from the priest? Give it back immediately!" 

Belcolore returned the cloak, muttering that the priest would never use her mortar again. The priest just laughed, saying if she won't lend him her mortar, he won't lend her his "pestle" - if you catch my drift.

By harvest time, they'd made peace over some wine and roasted chestnuts. The priest even got her musical instrument repaired as a bonus.

The moral? Even holy men aren't always holy—and sometimes, they're the most mischievous of all.
