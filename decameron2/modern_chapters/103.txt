The Nightmare That Came True

One night, Talano had a terrifying dream. He saw Margarita walking through their nearby woods when suddenly a massive wolf attacked her, tearing into her throat and face. When he woke up, he was genuinely worried.

"Honey," he told her that morning, "I know we don't always get along, but I'm seriously concerned. Please don't go into the woods today."

Margarita rolled her eyes. "Oh, sure. You're just trying to control me. I bet you're planning to meet someone there and don't want me to find out!"

Despite Talano's repeated warnings, Margarita was determined to prove him wrong. She secretly followed him to the woods, hiding among the trees, convinced he was up to something.

But sometimes, a warning is a warning for a reason.

Suddenly, exactly as Talano had dreamed, a huge wolf burst from the bushes. Before she could even scream, it attacked her throat. Shepherds nearby heard her struggle and chased the wolf away, but the damage was done.

Margarita survived, but her beauty was destroyed. The wolf had mangled her face and neck, leaving her permanently scarred. Humiliated and regretful, she finally understood that sometimes, just sometimes, it pays to listen to your spouse.
