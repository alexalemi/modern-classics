THE FIRST STORY: How a Total Scoundrel Became a Saint

Let me tell you something interesting, friends. Every good story should start by giving a nod to something bigger than ourselves. So I'm going to share a tale that shows just how wild and unpredictable life can be - and how sometimes, the universe has a seriously twisted sense of humor.

Here's the thing about life: it's messy, complicated, and full of unexpected twists. We're all basically stumbling through, trying to survive, and without a little help from above, we'd be totally lost. But here's the truly fascinating part - sometimes help comes from the most unlikely places.

Take my story, for example. It's about a guy named Ciappelletto - probably the most dishonest person who ever walked the earth. And yet, through a series of ridiculous events, he ended up being declared a saint. No, really.

Musciatto Franzesi was a wealthy merchant who was wrapping up his business in France. He had one big problem: he needed someone tough and cunning to collect some money from some tricky Burgundian businessmen. After thinking it over, he remembered Ciappelletto - a small, well-dressed guy who everyone in Paris knew as a total con artist.

Now, Ciappelletto wasn't just your average shady character. He was a professional troublemaker. As a scribe, he specialized in creating fake documents. He loved giving false testimony, breaking oaths, and stirring up drama. He'd start fights between friends and family just for fun. Murder? No problem. Blasphemy? His specialty. Church? He thought it was a joke. Taverns and gambling? Now we're talking.

In other words, Ciappelletto was basically the worst human being imaginable. But he was clever - and that made him dangerous.

When Musciatto asked him to collect some debts in Burgundy, Ciappelletto jumped at the chance. Desperate for work and with no real moral compass, he headed off, planning to use his usual bag of tricks.

But fate had other plans. Ciappelletto got seriously sick while staying with two Florentine brothers who were money-lenders. They were worried: if he died in their house without being able to confess, they'd be in big trouble with the local community.

Little did they know, Ciappelletto was about to pull off his greatest performance yet.
