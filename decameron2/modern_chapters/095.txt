After receiving multiple urgent messages from his business partners demanding he resolve some financial issues, Salabaetto decided it was time to leave town—and fast. Instead of heading to Pisa as originally planned, he hopped on a small ship and sailed to Naples.

Fortunately, his good friend Pietro dello Canigiano, the treasurer for the Empress of Constantinople, was in town. Pietro was known for being incredibly sharp, and Salabaetto trusted him completely. So he sat down and spilled the whole messy story—how he'd gotten himself into serious trouble and was basically trying to start over somewhere new.

Pietro listened carefully and then gave him a classic "what were you thinking?" look. "You really messed up," he said. "You disobeyed your bosses and blew a ton of money on stupid stuff. But what's done is done. Let's figure out how to fix this."

Being the clever guy he was, Pietro quickly hatched a plan. Salabaetto loved the idea and got to work immediately. He gathered some cash, borrowed a bit more from Pietro, and packed several merchant crates. He bought twenty oil barrels, filled them up, and sailed back to Palermo.

At the customs office, he submitted all the proper paperwork, declaring the full value of his cargo. He stored everything in the warehouse, telling officials he wasn't planning to touch the goods until some additional shipments arrived.

Little did he know, Biancofiore—the woman who'd previously scammed him—heard about his new shipments. When she learned his current merchandise was worth around 2,000 florins, with another 3,000 florins expected, her eyes lit up. She realized she'd gone after too small a target before and decided to play a new game.

She sent for Salabaetto, who by now had become much more cunning. When he arrived, she greeted him with exaggerated affection. "Oh, if you were upset that I didn't pay you back on time..." she began.

Salabaetto just laughed. "To be honest, I would've given you my entire heart just to make you happy. But let me tell you how things have changed. I'm so in love with you that I've sold most of my stuff and brought merchandise worth over 2,000 florins, with another 3,000 on the way. I'm planning to open a warehouse here, just so I can be close to you."
