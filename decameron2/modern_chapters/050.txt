The Third Story: Love on the Run

In Rome, not long ago, there was a young man named Pietro Boccamazza from a prestigious family who fell head over heels in love with Agnolella, a beautiful girl from a working-class family. Their love was so intense that they didn't care about the social differences that were supposed to keep them apart.

Pietro's family was furious. They warned him not to marry Agnolella and threatened to disown him. But when you're young and in love, those warnings sound more like a challenge than a deterrent.

So Pietro and Agnolella hatched a plan. They would run away together, escaping the disapproving eyes of Rome and chasing their dream of being together. One early morning, they mounted their horses and rode out of the city, hearts racing with excitement and fear.

They hadn't gone far when everything went wrong. About eight miles from Rome, they took a wrong turn and suddenly found themselves surrounded by a group of rough-looking men. Agnolella, quick-thinking and brave, shouted, "Pietro, run!" She spurred her horse into a nearby forest, disappearing among the trees.

Pietro wasn't so lucky. The men captured him, discussing whether they should rob or kill him. Just when things looked bleakest, another group attacked the first, creating chaos that allowed Pietro to escape.

Meanwhile, Agnolella was lost in the dense forest, riding wherever her horse chose to take her. Scared but determined, she wandered all day, calling Pietro's name and hoping for a miracle.

As night fell, she came across a small house where an elderly couple lived. Exhausted and terrified, she asked for shelter. The old man warned her about the dangerous travelers in the area, but Agnolella, more afraid of the forest's wild animals, begged to stay.

Just when she thought things couldn't get more complicated, a group of rough men arrived at the house. By pure luck and quick thinking, Agnolella hid in a hay pile, barely avoiding detection when one of the men threw a lance that nearly struck her.

Pietro, meanwhile, had survived his own series of adventures. After a night terrified in an oak tree, watching wolves devour his horse, he was rescued by some sympathetic shepherds who directed him to a nearby castle.

And here's where fate intervened. The castle belonged to Lionello, a friend of Pietro's, and his wife happened to be there. To Pietro's absolute amazement, Agnolella was already at the castle, safe and sound!

The castle's lady, seeing their genuine love and hearing their incredible story, decided to help them. She arranged their wedding right there in the mountains, promising to help reconcile Pietro's family.

A few days later, they returned to Rome. Pietro's family was still angry, but the lady's intervention and the couple's obvious love eventually won them over. Pietro and Agnolella went on to live a long, happy life together – proof that true love can overcome any obstacle.
