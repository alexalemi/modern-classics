The problem? Ferondo might have been dumb about everything else, but he was fiercely protective of his wife. The abbot was totally obsessed with her and knew he'd have to be clever to get what he wanted.

So he hatched a plan. During a confession, he convinced the wife that her husband's jealousy was unbearable and that the only solution was to... well, temporarily "remove" Ferondo.

Using a mysterious sleeping powder he'd gotten from some exotic prince, the abbot drugged Ferondo, making everyone believe he was dead. He was then secretly moved to a dark prison cell in the monastery, where a monk convinced him he was actually in purgatory.

Meanwhile, the abbot started visiting the wife—a lot. And wouldn't you know it, she ended up pregnant.

When the time was right, they "brought Ferondo back to life," and he returned home, completely believing he'd been to purgatory and back. The wife gave birth to a son, which everyone assumed was Ferondo's.

In the end, Ferondo became less jealous, the abbot got what he wanted, and nobody was the wiser. Just another day in medieval Italy.
