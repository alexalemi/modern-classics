The Tenth Story: How a Naive Girl Learns Some Unexpected Lessons

Dioneo, always the most mischievous storyteller, leaned forward with a grin. "Ladies, let me tell you about something you've probably never heard before - how someone 'puts the devil in hell'."

In a small North African town called Capsa, there lived a wealthy man with a teenage daughter named Alibech. Unlike most girls her age, Alibech was intensely curious about spirituality. She'd heard local Christians talk about serving God by retreating to the desert, and being young and impressionable, she decided that's exactly what she would do.

One morning, without telling anyone, fourteen-year-old Alibech packed a small bag and wandered into the desert, searching for a way to truly serve God. After days of traveling, she stumbled upon a hermit's hut and met Rustico, a monk who was definitely not prepared for her unexpected arrival.

Rustico, seeing a beautiful young woman who was completely naive, realized he had an... interesting opportunity. Instead of sending her away like the other hermits had, he decided to "teach" her about serving God.

He explained that the devil was God's greatest enemy, and the most acceptable service to God would be putting the devil back in hell. Alibech, totally serious and earnest, asked how one might do such a holy deed.

"I'll show you," Rustico said, trying very hard to look holy while actually being extremely unholy.

What followed was a "spiritual lesson" that was definitely not in any religious handbook - a lesson that Alibech learned with increasing enthusiasm, believing she was doing important divine work.

"I don't understand why the devil keeps trying to escape hell," she would later comment. "If hell is so nice, why would he want to leave?"

Rustico, who was definitely not experiencing this as a spiritual exercise, eventually became exhausted by her zealous dedication to "serving God."

Eventually, a fire in Capsa killed Alibech's family, making her an unexpected heir. A young man named Neerbale found her, married her, and brought her home - much to Rustico's relief and Alibech's initial disappointment at being removed from her "sacred duties."

When asked about her time in the desert, Alibech innocently explained that she had been "putting the devil in hell" - a statement that caused her new community to laugh hysterically, turning it into a local inside joke.

And so ended Alibech's very unconventional religious education.
