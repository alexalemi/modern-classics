The Great Scam at Buonconvento

Let me tell you about the most incredible con job you've ever heard - a story of how one crafty servant totally outsmarted his wealthy boss.

In Siena, not too long ago, there were two guys named Cecco - Cecco Angiolieri, a well-to-do young man from a good family, and Cecco Fortarrigo, who was basically the medieval equivalent of that friend everyone warns you about.

Angiolieri was planning a big trip to meet a cardinal, hoping to improve his career prospects. He needed a travel companion, and despite his better judgment, he agreed to take Fortarrigo along. Fortarrigo swore he'd be the perfect servant - no drinking, no gambling, totally reliable.

Spoiler alert: He was lying.

They stopped in Buonconvento for lunch, and after the meal, Angiolieri took a nap. Big mistake. Fortarrigo immediately hit the tavern, started gambling, and lost not just his own money, but the clothes off his back. Desperate to win something back, he then stole money from Angiolieri's wallet while his boss slept.

When Angiolieri woke up and discovered he'd been robbed, he was furious. But Fortarrigo wasn't done. Instead of apologizing, he started this bizarre performance, talking about a pawned jacket and trying to distract Angiolieri from the theft.

Here's where it gets truly wild. As Angiolieri tried to leave, Fortarrigo ran after him - in his underwear - screaming that Angiolieri was a thief. He found some local farmers and convinced them that Angiolieri had stolen from him. The farmers stopped Angiolieri, and in the chaos, Fortarrigo actually managed to steal Angiolieri's clothes and horse.

So picture this: Angiolieri, who was supposed to be meeting an important cardinal, is now standing in his underwear, while Fortarrigo rides away on his horse, telling everyone he "won" the horse in a bet.

Angiolieri was so embarrassed that instead of going back to Siena, he rode to his relatives' place to get new clothes, completely humiliated.

And Fortarrigo? He rode back to Siena, bragging about how he'd totally pulled one over on his boss.

Sometimes, pure audacity beats careful planning every single time.
