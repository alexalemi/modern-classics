Day One

THE FIRST DAY OF THE DECAMERON: HOW SEVEN FRIENDS CAME TOGETHER DURING A TERRIBLE TIME AND DECIDED TO SHARE STORIES

Look, kind readers, I know this story starts in a dark place. I'm about to tell you about one of the most terrifying times in human history - the plague that devastated Florence. I'm warning you upfront: the beginning is tough. But stay with me. This story isn't just about suffering - it's about human resilience, hope, and the incredible ways people survive when everything falls apart.

Let me be direct: in 1348, a horrific plague hit Florence. This wasn't just a typical outbreak - this was a catastrophe that would change everything. The disease spread like wildfire, turning the city into a nightmare. People were dying faster than anyone could count. Entire families were wiped out. The social fabric was literally unraveling before people's eyes.

The plague showed up differently than previous outbreaks. Instead of instant death, people would develop swollen lymph nodes - some as big as apples, others like eggs. These "plague buboes" would spread across the body, turning into dark, terrifying bruises. Doctors were completely helpless. No medicine worked. People were dying within days of showing symptoms.

But here's the incredible part - amid all this horror, human beings found ways to survive. Some people isolated themselves, hoping to escape infection. Others partied wildly, believing they might die anyway. Some religious people prayed. Some abandoned all rules. Families fell apart. People stopped caring for each other out of pure terror.

In just a few months, over 100,000 people died in Florence alone. Imagine that - an entire city's population, gone. Palaces stood empty. Families disappeared. The richest and healthiest people died just as quickly as anyone else.

But this story isn't just about death. It's about how people respond when everything falls apart. And that's where our seven young friends come in.

One Tuesday morning, in a nearly empty church, seven young people - three men and four women - met by chance. They were all between 18 and 28, from good families, and smart enough to know they needed a plan. With the city falling apart around them, they made a radical decision: they would leave Florence and create their own little world of safety and hope.

Their names? I'll keep those private to protect them. But they were determined to survive - not just physically, but with their humanity intact. They would tell each other stories, laugh, support each other, and prove that human connection matters most, even in the darkest times.

And so their incredible journey begins...
