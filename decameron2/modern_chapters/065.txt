Most women would have panicked, but not Filippa. Instead of begging or running away, she decides to show up to court and face the charges head-on. When the provost asks her if the accusations are true, she doesn't even blink.

"Yep, it's true," she says. "I was with Lazzerino, and I'd do it again. But here's the thing - this law is totally unfair."

She then turns the entire courtroom upside down with her brilliant argument. "My husband always got what he wanted from me," she tells the judge. "So what am I supposed to do with my... extra energy? Waste it? Throw it to the dogs? Wouldn't it be better to share it with someone who loves me completely?"

The crowd loses it. They're laughing, cheering, totally on her side. And just like that, with her razor-sharp wit, Filippa not only saves herself from being burned alive but also gets the ridiculous law changed. From that day forward, the law would only punish women who were selling their "favors" - not those in passionate love affairs.

Rinaldo? He slunk out of court totally humiliated. Filippa? She went home a free woman, having turned a potential death sentence into the most epic mic drop in legal history.
