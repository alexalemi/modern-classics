THE SEVENTH STORY: A DEADLY MISUNDERSTANDING

In Florence, not too long ago, there lived a young woman named Simona. She came from a poor family and made her living spinning wool, but that didn't stop her from falling in love.

Her boyfriend Pasquino worked for a local wool merchant. They were both young, hardworking, and completely smitten with each other. Every time Simona wound yarn around her spindle, she'd think of Pasquino and sigh dramatically. And Pasquino? He was so focused on her work that he'd watch her spinning more closely than anyone else's.

One Sunday, they planned a secret meetup in a garden. Simona told her father she was going to a church event, then snuck out with her friend Lagina. In the garden, they met Pasquino and his friend Stramba. While Lagina and Stramba chatted nearby, Simona and Pasquino found a quiet spot under a huge sage bush.

Pasquino, feeling playful, picked a sage leaf and started rubbing it against his teeth. "This stuff is great for cleaning your teeth after eating," he told Simona. But moments later, something shocking happened. Pasquino's face changed, he lost his ability to speak, and then - incredibly - he died.

Simona was devastated. Stramba, seeing Pasquino's swollen, discolored body, immediately accused her of murder. Before she knew it, Simona was dragged to court, accused of poisoning her beloved.

At the courthouse, desperate to explain what happened, Simona demonstrated exactly what Pasquino had done - and rubbed a sage leaf against her own teeth. To everyone's shock, she collapsed and died the exact same way.

The judge was stunned. Eventually, they discovered the real cause: a massive, toxic toad was living beneath the sage bush, its poisonous breath making the leaves deadly. The bush was immediately cut down and burned, along with the toad.

Pasquino and Simona were buried together in the church of St. Paul, their tragic love story ending as mysteriously as it began.
