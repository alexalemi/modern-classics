THE FIRST STORY: How a Clever Widow Dodged Two Unwanted Suitors

Look, sometimes you've got to get creative when people won't take a hint. And nobody understood that better than Francesca, a sharp-witted widow in Pistoia who was seriously tired of being harassed by two persistent guys who just wouldn't leave her alone.

Rinuccio and Alessandro were both exiled from Florence and had somehow decided Francesca was their dream girl. Problem was, she was definitely not interested. But these guys kept sending messages, showing up uninvited, basically making a total nuisance of themselves.

So Francesca hatched a plan. And not just any plan—a brilliantly twisted plan that would get rid of both of them at once.

That same day, a notoriously terrible guy named Scannadio had died—and I mean terrible. This dude was so ugly and mean that even when he was alive, people were scared of him. He'd just been buried in the local church's cemetery.

Francesca saw her opportunity.

She called her maid over and said, "I've got a scheme. These guys claim they'd do anything for me? Let's test that."

Her plan was deliciously complicated: She would ask each guy to do something so weird and scary that they'd either prove their absolute devotion or finally back off.

To Alessandro, she sent a message: "If you really want my attention, I need you to do something for me. Tonight, go to Scannadio's tomb, put on his dead body's clothes, and pretend to be a corpse. Let someone carry you to my house. Don't move, don't speak—just go with it."

To Rinuccio, she sent a similar message: "Your chance to win me over? Come to the tomb tonight, pick up Scannadio's body, and bring him to my house. No questions asked."

Both guys—totally in love and totally desperate—were like, "Absolutely! I'll do anything!"

What happened next was a comedy of errors that would make any Hollywood screenwriter jealous. Alessandro dressed up in a dead man's clothes and waited in the tomb. Rinuccio came, grabbed what he thought was the corpse, and started lugging it through the dark streets.

But then the city watch showed up. Rinuccio panicked, dropped his "corpse" (which was actually Alessandro), and ran. Alessandro, wearing a dead guy's clothes, also took off.

Francesca, watching from her window, couldn't help but laugh. Her plan had worked perfectly. Both guys had failed their "mission" and would now leave her alone.

The next day, when people discovered Scannadio's empty tomb, rumors flew. Some even whispered about devils stealing the body.

When Rinuccio and Alessandro separately came to Francesca, hoping to impress her with their "brave" attempts, she shut them down cold. "You didn't complete what I asked," she said. "So sorry, not interested."

And just like that, she was free.
