The Seventh Story

Love, Loyalty, and a King's Kindness

Most people would agree that King Charles was an incredible leader, though not everyone was a fan. This story, though, is about another royal - King Pedro of Arragon - and a love so pure and brave that it would melt even the coldest heart.

In Palermo, Sicily, there lived a wealthy pharmacist named Bernardo Puccini, who had a beautiful daughter named Lisa. When King Pedro came to town and held a grand tournament, Lisa happened to be watching from a window. The moment she saw the king riding and competing, she fell completely and utterly in love.

The problem was simple: she was just a pharmacist's daughter, and he was the king. Her love seemed impossible, but that didn't stop her heart. She was so consumed by her feelings that she began to waste away, growing weaker each day, her family desperately worried about her mysterious illness.

Lisa knew she needed to do something. So she called for Minuccio, a talented musician known at the royal court, and shared her incredible plan. She wanted Minuccio to somehow tell the king about her love - before she died from the pain of keeping it secret.

Minuccio was moved by her passion. He worked with a local poet to create a beautiful song that would reveal Lisa's heart to the king. When he performed the song at court, everyone was stunned - especially King Pedro, who was captivated by the music and its hidden message.

When Minuccio privately told the king Lisa's story, Pedro was touched. He was a kind ruler who understood the power of true love. Instead of dismissing her, he decided to visit Lisa personally.

At her bedside, the king was gentle and compassionate. He held her hand and encouraged her, seeing beyond her social status to her incredible spirit. Lisa, overwhelmed by his kindness, began to recover.

But Pedro didn't stop there. He decided to help Lisa find happiness. With his queen's support, he arranged a marriage for her to a noble young man named Perdicone, giving the couple land and wealth as a wedding gift. As a final gesture, he kissed Lisa on the forehead and declared himself her lifelong knight.

From that day forward, King Pedro was known not just as a powerful ruler, but as a leader who understood the most important power of all - the power of compassion.
