The Impossible Wife: A Test of Love

The story of the Marquess of Saluzzo is not a tale for the faint of heart. It's about Gualtieri, a nobleman who decides to marry, but only on his own bizarre terms.

Gualtieri wasn't interested in getting married. His vassals - basically, the local people who worked for him - kept pressuring him to find a wife and have children. "I'll marry," he finally told them, "but only if I choose the woman, and only if you promise to respect and honor whoever I select."

The vassals agreed, probably thinking he'd choose some fancy noblewoman. Instead, Gualtieri surprised everyone by falling for Griselda, a poor peasant girl from a nearby village. She was beautiful, but more importantly, she was kind and humble.

On their wedding day, Gualtieri did something shocking. In front of everyone, he stripped Griselda of her old clothes and dressed her in beautiful wedding attire. "This is my wife," he announced. And just like that, a peasant girl became a marchioness.

Griselda was incredible. She adapted perfectly to noble life, becoming more graceful and intelligent than anyone expected. She wasn't just a good wife - she was an amazing leader who won the respect of everyone in the region.

But Gualtieri had a weird plan. He wanted to test Griselda's patience in the most cruel way possible. First, he told her that people were complaining about her low-class background and didn't want her children to inherit his title. When their first child - a daughter - was born, he pretended he was going to have the baby killed.

Incredibly, Griselda remained calm. "Do what you must," she told him. "I trust you."

The servant who was supposed to harm the child actually took her to be raised secretly by Gualtieri's relatives. The same thing happened with their son a few years later.

Finally, Gualtieri announced he was divorcing Griselda to marry another woman. He made her leave the castle wearing only a single shift - basically, almost naked. She walked away with dignity, saying she was always grateful for the life she'd been given.

But this was all part of Gualtieri's ultimate test. He'd secretly been raising their children. When he "brought home" his new bride, it was actually their now-teenage daughter. And Griselda, still loyal, helped prepare the wedding feast.

When the truth was revealed, everyone was amazed. Griselda's incredible patience and love had passed every impossible test. Gualtieri reunited with her, bringing back their children and declaring her his true wife - proving that true love transcends social class, pride, and even the cruelest of tests.
