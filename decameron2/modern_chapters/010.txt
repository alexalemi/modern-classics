THE EIGHTH STORY: How a Clever Performer Taught a Cheap Millionaire About Generosity

Lauretta leaned forward, ready to share a story that would make her companions laugh. "You won't believe this tale about how a quick-witted performer totally schooled a super-rich cheapskate in Genoa," she began.

Picture this: There was this guy named Ermino Grimaldi who was legendarily wealthy - we're talking crazy rich, like top 1% of Italy wealthy. But here's the twist - he was also impossibly cheap. And I mean CHEAP. While other wealthy Italians loved showing off their money with fancy clothes and lavish parties, Ermino pinched every penny. He was so stingy that people had basically renamed him "Ermino the Miser" instead of using his actual last name.

Now enter Guglielmo Borsiere, a professional entertainer who was nothing like today's performers. Back then, these guys weren't just comedians - they were diplomats, storytellers, and social critics rolled into one. They'd negotiate peace treaties, arrange marriages, and weren't afraid to call out bad behavior. Compare that to modern entertainers who mostly spread gossip and drama!

Guglielmo was super popular in Genoa. Everyone loved him, and he'd heard all about Ermino's legendary cheapness. So one day, he decided to meet this famous miser.

Ermino, despite being incredibly tight with his money, still had a bit of class. He welcomed Guglielmo and even gave him a tour of his brand new mansion. Then he did something ridiculous - he asked Guglielmo if he knew of something "never before seen" that could be painted in his fancy new hall.

Guglielmo, quick as lightning, replied, "Well, I could suggest something you've definitely never seen before: an image of Generosity."

The burn was instant. Ermino was so embarrassed that something inside him completely changed. "I'll have Generosity painted right here," he declared, "in a way that proves I absolutely understand the concept!"

And just like that, Guglielmo's witty takedown transformed Ermino. From that moment on, he became the most generous person in Genoa - hosting incredible parties, helping strangers, and becoming known for his kindness instead of his greed.
