The Fifth Story

Let me tell you about two guys from Florence who prove that you should never judge a book by its cover.

First, there was Messer Forese da Rabatta, a lawyer who looked like he'd been hit with the ugly stick. Short, awkward, with a face that could stop a clock - but holy smarts! This man knew the law so well that other legal experts basically considered him a walking encyclopedia of legal wisdom.

Then there was Giotto, an artist so incredible that he could paint something so lifelike, you'd swear it might just walk right off the canvas. He basically rescued art from a bunch of amateur painters who were more interested in catching people's eyes than actually creating meaningful work. Giotto was so good that he refused to be called a "master" - which, ironically, just made people respect him even more.

Here's the funny part: these two weren't exactly traveling in style. One summer day, they both happened to be returning from checking on their country properties, riding the most beat-up horses you could imagine. When a sudden rainstorm hit, they took shelter at a farmhouse belonging to a mutual friend.

After borrowing some seriously worn-out cloaks and hats - because that's all their friend had - they hit the road again, looking like a couple of soggy, muddy scarecrows.

As they rode, Forese couldn't help but size up his companion. Staring at Giotto's disheveled appearance, he burst out laughing. "Hey Giotto," he said, "if a stranger saw you right now, do you think they'd believe you're the greatest painter in the world?"

Giotto, quick as lightning, shot back, "Well, if they looked at you, would they believe you know how to read?"

Boom! Forese got exactly what he had coming - burned by his own attempt to mock his friend.
