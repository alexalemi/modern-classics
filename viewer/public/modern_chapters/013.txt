_Day the Second_

     HERE BEGINS THE SECOND DAY OF THE DECAMERON, WHEREIN UNDER
     THE LEADERSHIP OF FILOMENA, THE STORIES TOLD ARE OF THOSE WHO,
     AFTER BEING CHALLENGED BY VARIOUS MISFORTUNES, HAVE ULTIMATELY
     ATTAINED A JOYFUL OUTCOME BEYOND THEIR EXPECTATIONS.


The sun had already brought the new day with its light throughout the land, and the birds, cheerfully singing among the green branches, bore witness to this with their merry songs, when the ladies and the three young men, all arising, entered the gardens and, walking slowly and pressing the dewy grass, went wandering here and there, weaving lovely garlands and enjoying themselves for a long while. And just as they had done the previous day, so did they now; that is, after eating in the cool and dancing for a time, they retired to rest, and then, after the midday hour, came all, by command of their queen, into the fresh meadows, where they seated themselves in a circle around her.
