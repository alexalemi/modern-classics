_Day the Seventh_

     HERE BEGINS THE SEVENTH DAY OF THE DECAMERON, WHEREIN
     UNDER THE LEADERSHIP OF DIONEO, WE WILL DISCUSS THE TRICKS
     THAT WOMEN HAVE USED, EITHER OUT OF LOVE OR FOR THEIR OWN
     PROTECTION, TO DECEIVE THEIR HUSBANDS, WITH OR WITHOUT
     THEIR KNOWLEDGE


The stars had already disappeared from the east, except for the one we call the Morning Star, which still shone in the lightening dawn. The steward, having risen, made his way to the Ladies' Valley with a large group, in order to prepare everything according to his lord's instructions. The king, awakened by the noise of the packers and animals, did not delay long in rising. Once up, he had all the ladies and young men roused. The sun's rays had barely broken through when they set out on the road. The nightingales and other birds had never seemed to sing so merrily as they did that morning, and their chorus accompanied the group as they made their way to the Ladies' Valley, where they were greeted by many others who seemed to be celebrating their arrival. 

After walking around and admiring the place anew, it appeared even more beautiful than the day before, as the time of day was better suited to its loveliness. Then, after enjoying a breakfast of good wine and confections, not wanting to be outdone by the birds, they began singing, and the valley echoed their melodies, to which all the birds seemed to add new and sweet notes, as if they too did not wish to be surpassed. 

When the dinner hour arrived and the tables were set up near the lovely lake under the thick laurels and other fine trees, they sat down to eat as the king had arranged. As they dined, they watched the vast schools of fish swimming in the lake, which at times prompted conversation and observation. 

After they had finished eating and the food and tables had been cleared away, they sang even more merrily than before. Then, beds were set up in various places around the small valley, enclosed by the discreet steward with curtains and canopies of French serge. Those who wished, with the king's permission, could go and rest, while those who did not feel like sleeping could engage in their other usual pastimes. 

After a while, when everyone had risen and the time had come for them to gather together to tell stories, carpets were spread on the grass, as the king had commanded, not far from where they had eaten.
