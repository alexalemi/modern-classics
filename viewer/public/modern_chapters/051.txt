THE FOURTH STORY

[Day the Fifth]

RICCIARDO MANARDI FINDS HIMSELF WITH MESSER LIZIO DA VALBONA'S DAUGHTER, AND MARRIES HER, REMAINING AT PEACE WITH HER FATHER

After Elisa finished her story and the other ladies praised her, the Queen instructed Filostrato to tell his own tale. He began, laughing, "I have been scolded by many of you ladies for giving you sad stories that made you weep. So now I want to tell you something that will make you laugh a little. I'm going to share a story of a love that, after only some sighs and a brief fright mixed with shame, came to a happy ending.

Not long ago, there lived in Romagna a highly respected and well-bred gentleman named Messer Lizio da Valbona. In his old age, his wife Madam Giacomina gave birth to a daughter who grew up to be exceptionally beautiful and charming, beyond any other girl in the region. Since she was their only child, Messer Lizio and his wife doted on her and guarded her with great care, hoping to arrange a prestigious marriage for her. 

There was a young man from the Manardi family of Brettinoro, named Ricciardo, who frequently visited Messer Lizio's home and spoke with him often. Messer Lizio and his wife paid little attention to Ricciardo, treating him like a son. But Ricciardo, seeing the young lady and finding her very beautiful, graceful, and well-mannered, fell deeply in love with her, though he kept his feelings secret. 

The young lady soon became aware of Ricciardo's love and, without trying to avoid it, also fell in love with him. Ricciardo was overjoyed by this and wanted to speak to her, but held back out of fear. One day, feeling brave, he said to her, "I beg you, Caterina, do not let me die of love." She immediately replied, "I wish you would not let me die either!"

This response filled Ricciardo with courage and joy, and he said to her, "Nothing that would please you shall ever go undone for me. But it is up to you to find a way for us to be together, for you are so closely guarded."

"Ricciardo," she answered, "you see how strictly I am watched. I cannot think of any way for you to reach me. But if you can devise something I can do without shame, tell me and I will do it."

After considering various options, Ricciardo said, "My sweet Caterina, the only way I can see is if you were to lie or somehow make your way to the gallery next to your father's garden. If I knew you would be there at night, I would surely find a way to come to you, no matter how high it may be."

"If you have the courage to come there," Caterina replied, "I believe I can manage to be there."

They agreed, and shared a quick, furtive kiss before parting ways.

The next day, as May was drawing to a close, Caterina complained to her mother that she had not been able to sleep the previous night due to the intense heat. Her mother said she had not noticed any unusual heat. But Caterina insisted that young girls feel heat more strongly than older women, and asked if she could sleep in the gallery next to her father's room, where she could hear the nightingales sing and be in a cooler place. Her mother agreed to speak to Messer Lizio about it.

When Messer Lizio heard this, he grumbled that he would make her sleep to the sound of crickets rather than nightingales. But Caterina's mother persuaded him to allow it, arguing that a young girl should be indulged in such small pleasures. So a bed was made up for Caterina in the gallery, and that night, Ricciardo was able to climb up and join her, where they spent a wonderful night together.

In the morning, Messer Lizio discovered them sleeping together, naked and embraced. Rather than becoming angry, he saw an opportunity. He called his wife and said, "Your daughter has caught the nightingale she wanted to hear sing." He realized that since Ricciardo was a young, wealthy, and well-born man, he could make a good son-in-law. So he told Ricciardo that if he was willing to marry Caterina, he would forgive them.

Ricciardo, fearing for his life, gratefully agreed. There, in Messer Lizio and his wife's presence, Ricciardo took Caterina as his wife. Afterward, Messer Lizio and his wife left, saying the young couple should rest. Ricciardo and Caterina embraced again, and over the next few days, Ricciardo officially married Caterina in front of their families and friends. He then brought her with great celebration to his own home, where they continued their joyful and peaceful nightingale-hunting, both day and night.
