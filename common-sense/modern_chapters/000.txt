Common Sense

Addressed to the Inhabitants of America

On the following subjects:

    The origin and purpose of government in general,
    with brief remarks on the English Constitution.

    Monarchy and hereditary succession.

    The present state of American affairs.

    America's present ability, with some
    miscellaneous reflections.

A new edition, with several additions in the body of the work. To which is added an appendix, together with an address to the people called Quakers.

"Man knows no Master save creating Heaven
Or those whom choice and common good ordain."
— Thomson

Philadelphia
