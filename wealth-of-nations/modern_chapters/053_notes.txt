1. SENTENCE RESTRUCTURING: Smith's comparative passages (China, Egypt, India, Greece, Rome) involve enormously long compound sentences. Broke these into digestible units while keeping the comparative logic clear.

2. VOCABULARY: "Gentoo" → "Hindu"; "artificers" → "craftsmen"; "corn" → "grain"; "sovereign" → "government/ruler(s)" where appropriate; "opulence" → "wealth/prosperity"; "dear" → "expensive"; "conveniencies" → "convenience."

4. PROPER NAMES: Kept Quesnay, Mirabeau, Mercier de la Riviere, Colbert, Montesquieu, Pliny, Arbuthnot, Thucydides. "Lewis XIV" → "Louis XIV." "Peking" kept as period reference but could note Beijing.

5. TONE: This chapter shifts from careful philosophical analysis (Physiocracy critique) through historical survey (China, Egypt, etc.) to grand rhetorical summary (natural liberty). Preserved each register.

6. Removed all superscript endnote reference numbers.

7. The Mirabeau quotation comparing the Economic Table to writing and money is kept nearly verbatim — it's too wonderfully absurd to paraphrase.
