2. SENTENCE RESTRUCTURING: Broke apart Smith's long sentences with multiple semicolons, especially in the key passage about how profits differ from wages (the two-factory example), and in the section about the three sources of income.

3. REMOVED SUPERSCRIPT REFERENCES: Removed all editor's endnote numbers (225-245).

4. KEY CONCEPTS PRESERVED: The central argument that all prices resolve into three components (wages, profit, rent) is kept fully intact. The beaver/deer exchange example, the two-factory comparison, the grain-to-bread chain of production, and the Scotch Pebbles example are all preserved.

5. "REAP WHERE THEY NEVER SOWED": Preserved this memorable phrase about landlords -- it's one of Smith's most quoted lines from this chapter.

6. INTEREST AS DERIVATIVE INCOME: Carefully preserved Smith's important distinction that interest is always derived from profit, not an original source of income in itself.

7. CONFOUNDING OF INCOME TYPES: Preserved Smith's practical examples of how wages, profit, and rent get confused when the same person plays multiple roles (gentleman farmer, independent craftsman, gardener).
