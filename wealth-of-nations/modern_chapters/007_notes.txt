2. GRAVITATIONAL METAPHOR: Preserved Smith's beautiful analogy of natural price as a "center of gravity" -- this is one of his most famous metaphors and a precursor to the concept of economic equilibrium. The phrase "center of repose and continuance" is kept in spirit.

3. SUPPLY AND DEMAND MECHANISM: Carefully preserved Smith's full explanation of how market prices adjust to natural prices through the entry and exit of land, labor, and capital. This is effectively Smith's theory of supply and demand, laid out very clearly.

4. MONOPOLY CRITIQUE: Preserved Smith's sharp distinction between the monopoly price ("the highest which can be squeezed out of buyers") and the competitive price ("the lowest which sellers can commonly afford to take"). This is vintage Smith wit.

5. PUBLIC MOURNING EXAMPLE: Kept this vivid example of how a period of mourning simultaneously creates shortages (black cloth, tailors) and surpluses (colored silks) -- one of Smith's best concrete illustrations.

6. REMOVED SUPERSCRIPT REFERENCES: Removed all editor's endnote numbers (246-261).

7. SENTENCE RESTRUCTURING: Broke apart Smith's characteristically long sentences, especially in the explanation of how effectual demand regulates market price and the passage about apprenticeship laws.

8. PRESERVED FORWARD REFERENCES: Smith previews the next four chapters at the end -- this roadmap is kept intact.
