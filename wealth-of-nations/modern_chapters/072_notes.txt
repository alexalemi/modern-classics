Major changes in this final chapter:

1. This is the concluding chapter of the entire Wealth of Nations. Smith's famous final paragraphs on the British Empire have been carefully modernized while preserving their full rhetorical force.
2. "Stock" → "capital" consistently; "sovereign" → "government," "ruler," or "the state."
3. Removed all superscript reference numbers (1616-1645).
4. Smith's discussion of currency debasement and denomination manipulation made clearer with modern language while preserving the historical examples (Roman As, King John of France, Henry VIII).
5. "Bounty" → "subsidy" where applicable; "drawback" → "rebate."
6. "Corn" → "grain" in the discussion of Roman grain distributions.
7. "The present disturbances" → "the current troubles" (the American Revolution, which had just begun when Smith was writing).
8. "Negro" → "Black people" or "enslaved workers" depending on context.
9. "Plantations" → "colonies" where the meaning is clear; kept where it refers specifically to plantation agriculture.
10. Smith's remarkable passage about Ireland -- condemning the religious aristocracy as "the most odious of all distinctions" -- preserved with full force.
11. Smith's famous final paragraph, with its metaphor of the "golden dream" and the empire that exists only "in imagination," preserved with only light modernization. This is one of the most quoted passages in the book.
12. "Oeconomical" → "economical."
13. The detailed fiscal arithmetic (revenue projections for a unified empire) kept but presented more clearly.
14. Smith's comparison of how the colonies use paper currency vs. gold and silver is a fascinating early discussion of monetary theory, preserved in full.
15. The discussion of trade balances with different types of colonies (tobacco, northern, sugar) is preserved as excellent applied economics.
16. Smith's concluding argument -- that Britain should either make the empire real by granting representation, or abandon it -- is presented with the full weight and clarity it deserves as the book's final statement.
