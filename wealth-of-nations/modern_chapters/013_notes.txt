It was preserved in full with its moral force intact — Smith's point is that gold's value comes from food surplus, and the indigenous people's behavior was entirely logical.

7. MINING ECONOMICS: The detailed comparison of tin, silver, and gold mining — with specific tax rates, rent proportions, and smuggling incentives — was kept fully intact as it's essential to Smith's argument about how precious metals' prices are determined.
