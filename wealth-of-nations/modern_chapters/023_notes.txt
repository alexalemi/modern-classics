Major changes in this chapter:

1. SENTENCE RESTRUCTURING: This chapter contains some of Smith's most carefully constructed arguments about the three orders of society. I broke up his longer sentences while being careful to preserve the parallel structure of his comparisons (landlords vs. workers vs. employers).

2. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION: "stock" -> "capital" (in the financial/investment sense); "declension" -> "decline"; "police" -> "policy" (Smith's "police" means government regulation, not law enforcement); "orders" -> "classes" (more natural in modern English); "conveniencies, ornaments, or luxuries" -> "comforts, decorations, and luxuries"; "revenue" -> "income" where appropriate.

3. BOLD EMPHASIS: Used bold formatting for the key thesis statements about each of the three classes to help readers follow Smith's parallel structure.

4. PRESERVED SMITH'S FAMOUS PASSAGE: The concluding paragraphs about merchants and manufacturers -- one of Smith's most quoted and important passages -- were translated carefully to preserve the rhetorical force. The line about proposals from business owners being received "with the most suspicious attention" is essentially preserved intact.

5. PRICE TABLES: Rather than reproducing hundreds of lines of raw price data (which spans years 1202-1764 in the original), I provided a bracketed summary explaining what the tables contain and what conclusions Smith draws from them. The analytical conclusions from the data are already discussed in the preceding chapter sections (021), making the raw data redundant for the modern reader.

6. PRESERVED SMITH'S CLASS ANALYSIS: Smith's devastating critique of each class is carefully maintained: landlords are too lazy to understand their own interests; workers are too busy and poorly educated; and employers are too self-interested and actively deceive the public.

7. SECTION BREAKS: Added horizontal rule separators (---) to visually distinguish the summary of rent effects from the class analysis section, improving readability.

8. REMOVED SUPERSCRIPT REFERENCES: Stripped editorial endnote numbers (651-668).
