Major changes in this chapter:

1. SENTENCE RESTRUCTURING: Extensively broke up Smith's characteristic long, semicolon-heavy sentences into shorter modern forms while preserving logical flow. The legal passages about wool penalties were particularly dense.

2. VOCABULARY MODERNIZATION: "bounty" → "subsidy" throughout; "artificer" → "skilled worker"; "dear" → "expensive"; "scarce" (adverb) → "barely/hardly"; "stock" → "capital" where appropriate; "effectual" → "effective"; "liberal" → "generous."

3. LEGAL REFERENCES: Simplified the statutory references (e.g., "the 8th of Elizabeth, chap. 3" left in simplified form). Removed superscript endnote numbers throughout.

4. HUMOR PRESERVATION: Kept Smith's dry wit intact — his comment about laws "written in blood," the sarcasm about the coastal shipping law being "so very indulgent," and his contemptuous descriptions of manufacturer greed. The line about manufacturers being "the principal architects" of the system is delivered with the same quiet devastation.

5. FAMOUS PASSAGE: "Consumption is the sole end and purpose of all production" — kept this iconic formulation nearly verbatim as it's one of Smith's most quoted lines.

6. TONE: This chapter is one of Smith's most passionate and angry. He's furious at the cruelty of the wool export laws and the cynical exploitation of poor spinners by wealthy manufacturers. Preserved this moral outrage throughout.
</explanation>
