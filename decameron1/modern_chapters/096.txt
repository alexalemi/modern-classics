The Ninth Day

HERE BEGINS THE NINTH DAY OF THE DECAMERON, WHEREIN UNDER THE LEADERSHIP OF EMILIA, EACH PERSON TELLS A STORY ACCORDING TO THEIR PREFERENCE

The light, whose brilliance causes the night to flee, had already changed the entire eighth heaven from blue to a paler, greyish-blue color, and the flowers had begun to lift their heads along the meadows. Emilia rose and summoned the ladies and young men, who then followed the slow steps of the queen as they made their way to a small forest not far from the palace. Entering the forest, they saw wild goats, deer, and other animals, seemingly unafraid of the hunters due to the ongoing plague, standing and waiting for them, as if they had grown tame. The group amused themselves for a while, approaching the animals and making them run and jump. But as the sun rose higher, they decided to return. They were all adorned with oak leaves, their hands full of flowers and fragrant herbs, and anyone who saw them would have said they could not be overcome by death, or it would slay them merrily.

In this manner, they walked back, singing, chatting, and laughing, until they reached the palace, where everything was well-ordered, and the servants were filled with joy and cheer. After resting for a bit, they did not go to dinner until the young men and ladies had sung several lighthearted songs. Then, after washing their hands, the steward seated them at the table according to the queen's wishes, and they all ate joyfully. Afterward, they spent some time dancing and making music, and then, at the queen's command, everyone retired to rest.
