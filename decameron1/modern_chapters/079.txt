Regarding the last of the three things Pyrrhus required, Lydia had a harder time coming up with a plan. Nevertheless, being exceptionally clever and having Love make her even quicker to invent, she soon thought of how she could make it happen. Nicostratus had two young boys living with him, sent by their father so they could learn proper manners and etiquette in his household. One would serve him food at mealtimes, and the other would pour his drinks.

Lydia called the boys over and convinced them that they had terrible breath. She instructed them that when serving Nicostratus, they should keep their heads tilted back as much as possible and never tell anyone about this. The boys believed what she said and proceeded to do as she had taught them. After a while, Lydia said to her husband, "Have you noticed what those boys do when they serve you?" 

"Yes, I have," replied Nicostratus, "and I've been meaning to ask them why they do that." 

Lydia said, "Don't do that, because I can tell you the reason. I've kept it from you for a long time, not wanting to upset you. But now that I see others are starting to notice, there's no point in hiding it from you any longer. This happens to you because you have an absolutely terrible breath. I don't know what could be causing it, as it didn't used to be this way. This is a very unseemly thing for someone who associates with gentlemen, and we must find a way to cure it."

Nicostratus said, "What could this be? Could I have a rotten tooth in my mouth?"

"Perhaps," answered Lydia, and she led him to a window, where she examined his mouth closely. "Oh, Nicostratus," she cried, "how have you put up with this for so long? You have a tooth on this side that is not only decayed, but completely rotten. If you keep it in your mouth much longer, it will ruin the ones next to it. I advise you to have it pulled out before it gets worse."

"Since you think it's best," Nicostratus replied, "I will do it. Let's call a surgeon right away to remove it."

"Goodness, no!" Lydia exclaimed. "I couldn't bear for a surgeon to come here and do that to you. Those people are so barbaric when it comes to procedures like this. I can easily pull it out myself, without any surgeon, and I'll be able to release you from the pain right away, which a surgeon wouldn't do."

So Lydia had the proper instruments brought in, sent everyone else out of the room, and locked herself inside with Lusca. She made Nicostratus lie down on a table, and while the maid held him, Lydia forcefully pulled out one of his teeth, despite his loud cries of pain. Then, keeping the tooth she had extracted, she took out a horribly decayed tooth she had prepared beforehand and showed it to her husband, who was half-dead from the pain, saying, "See what you've had in your mouth all this time."

Nicostratus believed what she said, and now that the tooth was out, although he had suffered great pain and complained bitterly, he felt cured. After comforting himself and the pain subsiding, he left the room. Lydia then promptly sent the extracted tooth to her lover, who, now assured of her love, professed himself ready to do her every wish.
