DAY THE THIRD

HERE BEGINS THE THIRD DAY OF THE DECAMERON, IN WHICH, UNDER THE RULE OF NEIFILE, TALES ARE TOLD OF THOSE WHO, THROUGH DILIGENCE, HAVE ACQUIRED SOME LONG-DESIRED THING OR REGAINED SOME LOST GOOD

As the sun approached, the vermilion dawn began to turn golden-orange. On that Sunday morning, the queen arose and had her entire company rise as well. The steward had long since dispatched servants and supplies to the destination, and seeing the queen ready to depart, he promptly loaded the remaining belongings, as if breaking camp, and followed behind the ladies and gentlemen with the household goods and servants. 

The queen then made her way westward at a leisurely pace, accompanied and followed by her ladies and the three young men. They were guided by the song of nearly two dozen nightingales and other birds, traveling along a little-used path filled with green herbs and flowers that were just beginning to bloom in the coming sun. Chatting, jesting, and laughing with her company, the queen brought them, before midmorning, to a very beautiful and grand palace situated slightly above the plain on a small hill. 

They entered and thoroughly inspected the great halls and the unique and elegant chambers, all furnished with the necessary provisions. They greatly admired the place and considered its lord to be magnificent. Then, going downstairs and seeing the spacious and cheerful courtyard, the cellars full of the finest wines, and the cool, abundant water, they praised it even more.

Afterwards, as if desiring rest, they went to sit in a gallery that overlooked the entire courtyard, which was filled with flowers of the season and lush greenery. The attentive steward soon arrived and entertained and refreshed them with the most costly confections and choice wines.

Then, they were granted access to a walled garden adjoining the palace, and upon entering, it seemed to them altogether marvelous. The garden had wide, straight alleys lined with trellised vines heavy with blossoms, which filled the air with a rare fragrance. The sides of the alleys were bordered with red and white roses and jasmine, allowing one to walk in a delightful, fragrant shade, even in the midday sun.

The variety, quantity, and orderly arrangement of the plants were too numerous to recount. Suffice it to say, there was no pleasant plant that could thrive in our climate that was not found there in abundance. At the center of the garden was a fine lawn of the deepest green, dotted with perhaps a thousand different flowers and enclosed by the lushest orange and lemon trees, bearing both fresh fruit and new blossoms - a delight to both the eyes and the nose.

In the middle of the lawn stood a fountain of the whitest marble, sculpted with great artistry. From a figure atop a pillar in its center, a tremendous jet of water shot high into the sky, falling back into the crystal-clear basin with a delightful sound, powerful enough to drive a mill. The overflowing water then flowed through hidden channels, surrounding the lawn and running through most of the garden before exiting, turning two mills along the way to the great advantage of the lord.

The sight of this garden, its orderly design, its plants, and its fountain with the rivulets flowing from it, so pleased the ladies and gentlemen that they unanimously declared that if Paradise could be created on Earth, they could not conceive of a form more beautiful than this garden, nor any additional beauty that could be added to it.

As they joyfully wandered about, weaving the loveliest garlands from the varied foliage and listening to the songs of perhaps two dozen different birds singing in friendly competition, they noticed a delightful sight they had not yet taken in - the garden was filled with maybe a hundred different types of graceful creatures. Rabbits emerged, hares ran, kids and fawns grazed, and various other harmless animals went about their pastimes as if tame, adding even greater pleasure to the experience.

After thoroughly exploring the wonders of the garden, the queen had the tables set around the beautiful fountain. Having first sung a few songs and danced some dances, they sat down to a fine, orderly, and tranquil meal, served with delightful and delicate dishes. Growing even more cheerful, they then returned to making music, singing, and dancing until the queen deemed it time for those who wished to sleep to do so. Some went to rest, while others, captivated by the beauty of the place, remained, some reading romances, some playing chess or checkers, while the sleepers refreshed themselves. 

When the hour of none had passed and the sleepers had arisen and refreshed their faces with cold water, they all came, at the queen's command, to the lawn by the fountain and seated themselves in the usual fashion, ready to begin storytelling on the assigned topic.
