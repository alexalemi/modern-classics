KEY ARGUMENTS ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER -- must carry forward:

- CONTEMPORANEOUS PRODUCTION: Workers are NOT fed from past savings/capital. They are fed by the production of food and necessities happening simultaneously with their own work. The "idle heir" example proved this vividly -- inherited bonds are just the power to command wealth as others produce it.

- THE DEMAND PRINCIPLE: "The demand for consumption determines the direction in which labor will be expended in production." This was stated as a formal principle and will likely be referenced again.

- VIRTUAL PRODUCTION: A worker who makes jackknives and buys wheat is virtually producing wheat, because their demand for wheat directs labor toward wheat production. The jackknife-maker's labor effectively produces the wheat they consume.

- THE CURVED PIPE ANALOGY: Production and consumption connected like a pipe -- put water in one end, equivalent comes out the other. Workers "put in as they take out."

- NO ADVANCE OF CAPITAL: Firmly established that paying wages involves no advance of capital to workers. If anything, the advance goes from workers to employers (since labor precedes payment).
