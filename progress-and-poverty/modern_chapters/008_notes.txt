KEY TERMS AND REFERENCES ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:

- MALTHUSIAN THEORY: George has now moved from presenting it (Chapter 007) to attacking it with historical evidence. His core counter-argument: nowhere in history can poverty be traced to overpopulation; everywhere it can be traced to tyranny, oppression, bad government, and unjust institutions.
- "POSITIVE CHECK" / "PRUDENTIAL CHECK": Malthus's terms, used again in this chapter (Confucius passage). Briefly glossed.
- ESSAY ON POPULATION: George mocks its logical quality at length. He argues the book actually refutes itself -- Malthus's own survey of "positive checks" shows that vice and misery arise from causes other than overpopulation.

GEORGE'S ARGUMENT STRUCTURE: This chapter is the EVIDENCE phase of George's attack on Malthus. He has:
1. Argued that the core assumption lacks warrant (no evidence population tends to outrun food supply)
2. Shown that Malthus's own book refutes itself
3. Surveyed world population history to show decline is as common as growth
4. Examined the three strongest "overpopulation" cases (India, China, Ireland) and shown that in each case, poverty is caused by oppression, not too many people
5. Concluded that nowhere in history can poverty be traced to overpopulation

The next chapter should continue the attack on Malthus, likely moving from historical evidence to theoretical/analytical arguments about why population growth does NOT tend to outrun the food supply. George has promised to explain "why the natural increase of population does not produce want" -- this should come in subsequent chapters.

TONE: This chapter builds from analytical to passionately angry. The Ireland section is the emotional climax. George's outrage at the complacent attribution of Irish suffering to "natural law" is barely contained. This intensity should be expected to continue.
