"The mechanic, storekeeper, or professional man who has secured himself a house" -> "the tradesperson, shopkeeper, or professional who has bought a house and lot where they live." "His family" -> "their family." "Him" -> "the farmer" or "they" as appropriate. The farmer example avoids gendered pronouns while staying natural.

4. PRESERVED ELEMENTS: The endnote reference (56) was preserved. The Duke of Westminster and Astor family examples were kept as George's concrete illustrations. The Free Trade League broadside was kept as a colorful historical detail. George's closing rhetorical question ("How many people are there who fairly earn a million dollars?") was preserved with full force.

5. HISTORICAL CONTEXT: "Before the war" was clarified to "before the Civil War." The drayman was updated to "truck driver" for modern comprehension while keeping the scale analogy (small operator vs. large).

6. TONE: This chapter is more practical and concrete than the previous two, addressing real anxieties about who would win and lose. The translation maintains George's reassuring but intellectually honest approach -- he doesn't promise landowners won't lose anything, but shows the loss would be minor compared to the gain.
