Major changes and rationale:

1. SENTENCE BREAKING: George's preface contains several enormous sentences, particularly in the middle section where he summarizes the book's argument chapter by chapter. These multi-clause sentences (some exceeding 150 words) have been broken into shorter, clearer sentences while preserving the logical chain of reasoning.

2. VOCABULARY UPDATES:
   - "political economy" → "economics" or "mainstream economics" consistently
   - "subsistence" → "the means to support it" or "a bare living" or "the starvation point"
   - "in the name of political economy" → "that mainstream economics currently offers"
   - "laborsaving" → "labor-saving" (not present in this chapter)
   - "the current political economy" → "mainstream economics"
   - "statesmanship" → "practical policy"
   - "race improvement" → "improvement of the human race over generations" (avoiding racial connotations)

3. GENDER-NEUTRAL LANGUAGE:
   - "men" → "people" or "ordinary people"
   - "the masses of men" → "ordinary people"
   - "himself" → "themselves"
   - "laborers" → "workers"

4. IDENTIFYING REFERENCES: Added first names to Smith, Ricardo, Proudhon, and Lassalle on their appearance in the preface, since these are the first mentions in the book.

5. CLARIFYING PHRASES: Added brief clarifying language in a few places:
   - "the margin of cultivation" gets a brief gloss: "the point in production where rent begins" (George's own phrase, moved closer to the first mention)
   - "laissez faire" → "true free markets, in their fullest and deepest sense" to convey what George meant

6. PRESERVED: George's first-person voice, his confident tone, the logical roadmap structure of the preface, the emotional conviction, and the historical references (Irish land agitation, dates, publication history). The preface's function as both personal statement and book summary is maintained.

7. TONE: Kept George's characteristic mix of scholarly precision and personal conviction. The preface is relatively restrained compared to the more passionate chapters that follow, and the translation reflects that.
