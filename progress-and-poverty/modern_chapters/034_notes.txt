KEY TERMS AND REFERENCES ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:

- THE FOUR CANONS OF TAXATION: (1) Light burden on production, (2) Easy and cheap to collect, (3) Certain and predictable, (4) Equal and fair. George argues the land value tax wins on all four. These may be referenced in subsequent chapters.
- TAX SHIFTING / INCIDENCE: Key economic concept. Taxes on goods get passed along the supply chain (seller -> buyer -> consumer) with profit added at each step. Land taxes can't be shifted because land supply is fixed. The Manila cigar example ($70 total = $14 cost + $56 duty, but dealer must profit on the full $70) is George's most concrete illustration.
- SPECULATIVE RENT: Taxes on land values would destroy speculative holding of idle land, which George blames for both wasted land and economic depressions. The "dog in the manger" image recurs from Chapter 032.
- NATURAL MONOPOLIES: George argues railroads, telegraphs, and similar businesses are natural monopolies that should be publicly owned. This is a secondary argument but shows his broader reform vision.
- ADAM SMITH: Cited for the principle that taxes should be proportional to the revenue people enjoy under state protection. George turns this into an argument FOR land value taxation -- since land value is the value created and maintained by the community.
- THE CORRUPTION ARGUMENT: George's powerful case that complex tax systems corrupt public morals. The mock title for revenue laws is memorable. The New York Commission (Wells, Dodge, Cuyler) is cited as proposing a simpler alternative.

ARGUMENT STRUCTURE: The next chapter (035) deals with endorsements from prominent economists and objections to the land value tax. It's the final chapter of Book VIII. The tone shifts from systematic argument to more polemical engagement with critics and allies.

TONE: Systematic, prosecutorial, building to increasingly confident conclusions. The Chapter IV section on equality reaches genuine moral eloquence with "Then, but not until then, will labor get its full reward."

VOCABULARY CONSISTENCY: "land values" as the standard term for the tax base. "Rent" / "economic rent" as the income from land ownership. "Capital" in George's sense. "Production" as a key concern. "Speculation" / "speculative rent" as the enemy. Gender-neutral language throughout.
