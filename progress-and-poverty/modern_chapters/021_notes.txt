KEY TERMS AND REFERENCES ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:

- LABOR-SAVING = RENT-INCREASING: George's central argument in this chapter. Any improvement that saves labor, no matter what kind, increases the demand for land and thus increases rent. This is because (1) human desire is unlimited, (2) freed labor will be directed to producing more wealth, and (3) more wealth production means more demand for land.
- THE INTERCHANGEABILITY OF WEALTH: Saving labor in producing ANY form of wealth is equivalent to saving it in all forms, because of trade and the division of labor. This means improvements in manufacturing or transportation raise agricultural rent too.
- THE LOGICAL EXTREME: If labor-saving technology reached perfection, wages would be zero, interest would be zero, and rent would take everything. This thought experiment dramatically illustrates George's argument.
- LATIFUNDIA ANALOGY: Modern mechanized farming is creating the same kind of vast depopulated estates that slave labor created in ancient Rome -- with the same effect of displacing workers.
- MONOPOLY PROFITS vs. RENT: George distinguishes between temporary monopoly profits (from patents, railroad monopolies, etc.) and rent. As monopoly profits are competed away or charges reduced, the benefit flows to rent.
- FREE TRADE AND GOVERNMENT REFORM: Even improvements in government policy (like free trade) ultimately increase rent rather than wages or interest.

THE ARGUMENT SO FAR: Chapters 2 and 3 have now shown that BOTH forms of material progress -- population growth AND technological improvement -- tend to increase rent. The next chapter (022) will introduce a THIRD force: speculation in land, driven by the expectation of further increases in rent.

TONE: Analytical but building to passion. The "logical extreme" passage is one of the book's most dramatic moments. The next chapter should maintain this building momentum.

VOCABULARY CONSISTENCY: "margin of cultivation" / "the margin" used throughout. "rent" = economic rent. "wages" and "interest" in George's technical senses. "labor-saving" (hyphenated). "economists" for "political economists."

STRUCTURAL NOTE: George ends by noting that there's a THIRD cause "not yet considered" that pushes rent even higher -- speculation. This is the subject of Chapter 4 (022), the final chapter of Book IV.
