KEY TERMS AND CONCEPTS:

- THE THREE LAWS ALL DEPEND ON THE MARGIN OF CULTIVATION: This is the culminating insight of Book III's theoretical construction. Rent rises as the margin falls; wages and interest fall as the margin falls. All three laws spring from the single principle that people seek to satisfy their desires with the least effort.
- MAINSTREAM VS. TRUE LAWS: The side-by-side comparison is the structural heart of this chapter. Mainstream economics has disconnected laws (wages depend on capital-to-labor ratio, interest depends on supply/demand for capital). George's system has unified laws all centered on the margin of cultivation.
- THE LAW OF RENT AS FOUNDATION: George notes that accepting the law of rent (universally accepted since Ricardo) implicitly requires accepting his laws of wages and interest -- they're necessary consequences.
