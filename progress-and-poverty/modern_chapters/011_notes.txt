KEY TERMS AND FRAMEWORK ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:

- THREE FACTORS, THREE RETURNS: George has now formally established his terminological framework for Book III. Land -> Rent. Labor -> Wages. Capital -> Interest. These are MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVE categories. "Profits" is explicitly excluded as a confused term that mixes all three.
- THE NATURAL ORDER: Land, Labor, Capital (not Capital first). This reversal of the conventional order is George's methodological innovation. He will start his analysis from land/rent, not from capital. This ordering will structure the next several chapters.
- LAWS MUST CORRELATE: The true laws of distribution must fit together mathematically -- knowing any two should determine the third. The current laws (wages-fund for wages, margin of cultivation for rent, supply-and-demand for interest) don't fit together. George promises that his laws will.
- CAPITAL IS NOT NECESSARY: Labor on land can produce wealth without capital. Therefore rent and wages must form a complete system on their own, with interest as a subordinate addition. This will be important in the coming chapters.
- TOM, DICK, AND HARRY ANALOGY: Tom = Land, Dick = Labor, Harry = Capital. Tom and Dick are the original partners; Harry (capital) came in only as Dick's (labor's) assistant. This analogy may recur.
- THE WRONG STARTING POINT: George argues that classical economics went wrong by making capital the central figure and starting point, when the natural order begins with land. Adam Smith's specific misstep: abandoning "the produce of labor constitutes the natural recompense or wages of labor" in favor of a framework where capital employs labor.
