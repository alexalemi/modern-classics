KEY TERMS AND REFERENCES ESTABLISHED IN THIS CHAPTER:

- SIX REJECTED REMEDIES: George has now systematically demolished: (1) government economy, (2) education and thrift, (3) labor unions, (4) worker cooperatives, (5) government regulation/socialism, (6) land redistribution. Each fails for the same fundamental reason: as long as land is privately owned and monopolized, any gains in productivity or efficiency will be captured by landowners through higher rent. This is the recurring refrain.

- RENT AS THE ABSORBER: The core logic repeated in every section: any improvement in productive efficiency -> increased rent -> no benefit to labor. This principle, established in Books III-V, is applied here to each proposed remedy.

- "LAND AND LIBERTY!": George ends the socialism section with the Nihilist motto, previewing his own remedy. This is the bridge to Chapter 026.

- DE LAVELEYE'S PARADOX: The extended discussion of French/Belgian small holdings, where the system that supposedly distributes land more fairly actually produces worse conditions for laborers than England's concentrated system -- but prevents radical reform by giving peasants a stake in the status quo. George sees England's concentrated ownership as "full of hope" precisely because it makes the injustice so visible.

- CONCENTRATION AS THE DIRECTION OF PROGRESS: The chapter's final argument -- that all of modern development moves toward concentration, making proposals to subdivide land backward-looking and futile. This sets up George's remedy, which works WITH the trend toward concentration rather than against it.

- TONE SHIFT: This chapter has been systematic and patient -- methodically clearing the ground. Chapter 026 should be dramatically different: short, powerful, declarative. George is about to reveal his answer. The tone should shift from patient demolition to bold proclamation.

- FORMATTING: Chapter 026 ("The True Remedy") is very short (~640 words) and contains the book's central declaration: "We must make land common property." This should be formatted to carry maximum dramatic weight.

VOCABULARY CONSISTENCY: "economics" for "political economy." "Rent" / "economic rent" used consistently. "The margin" for marginal land. "Labor unions" for "combinations of workmen." "Workers" / "working people" for "workmen" / "laboring classes." "Productivity" and "productive power" used interchangeably. Endnote numbers preserved.
