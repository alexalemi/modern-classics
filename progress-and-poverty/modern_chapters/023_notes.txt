KEY TERMS AND CONCEPTS ESTABLISHED:

- SPECULATIVE RENT LINE vs. NORMAL RENT LINE: Central to George's explanation of booms and busts. The speculative rent line is where landowners set rents based on anticipated future values; the normal rent line is where they'd be based on current productive conditions. When the speculative line outruns the normal line, production is choked and depression follows.

- THE BOOM-BUST CYCLE: George has now laid out the complete mechanism: (1) Growing community -> rising land values -> speculation -> land values pushed beyond what labor/capital can pay -> production choked -> depression. (2) Depression continues until speculative values fall, efficiency catches up, or workers accept less. (3) New equilibrium -> renewed activity -> speculation begins again -> cycle repeats. Each cycle forces wages and interest a little lower.

- THE PYRAMID ANALOGY: The industrial system is like a pyramid resting on land. When the bottom layer (land-based production) is squeezed, the upper layers hold for a while (due to the elastic system of credit and advances), then collapse suddenly. This explains why depressions come as sudden shocks rather than gradual declines.

- THE FLYWHEEL ANALOGY: Speculation in produced goods acts like a flywheel -- smoothing rather than disrupting production. Only speculation in land (a thing of fixed quantity, not produced by labor) can cause systemic depression.

- OVERPRODUCTION vs. OVERCONSUMPTION: George has demolished both explanations by showing they're just two faces of the same coin -- the real problem is that production and consumption can't reach each other because labor is shut out from land.

- SAN FRANCISCO/CALIFORNIA: Used as the definitive case study. The transcontinental railroad triggered massive land speculation, which choked production despite the railroad being a genuine improvement. This pattern repeated nationwide.

- ENDNOTES: 40 (George's own pamphlet on California railroads), 41 (tenacity of speculative land values), 42 (prediction of renewed activity followed by another crash) -- all preserved.

TONE GOING INTO CHAPTER 024: George is now building to his grand summation. Chapter 023 solved the specific problem of recurring depressions; Chapter 024 will solve the bigger problem -- why poverty persists amid advancing wealth. The tone should shift from analytical triumph to sweeping, passionate conclusion. George's rhetoric intensifies significantly in Chapter 024.
