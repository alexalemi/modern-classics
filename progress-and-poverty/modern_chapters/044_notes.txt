This chapter is the philosophical and rhetorical climax of the entire book -- George's most sustained piece of oratory. Translation decisions:

1. STRUCTURE: The chapter moves through three distinct movements: (a) a summary of the central argument and its implications, (b) an extended hymn to Liberty that traces her influence through all of human history, and (c) a visionary crescendo imagining what could be. I preserved all three movements and their building intensity.

2. THE LIBERTY PASSAGE: This is perhaps the most famous section of the book. I preserved its rhetorical structure, its parallelism, and its sweep through history while modernizing the syntax. The key parallel constructions ("Where Liberty rises... Where Liberty sinks...") are kept intact. The historical survey from Moses through the French Revolution is complete and faithful.

3. VOCABULARY: "Political economy" became "economics" in the Conclusion section. "Politico-economic" became "economic." "Imbrutes" became "degrades." "Mal-adjustments" became "arrangements." "Noisome" became "filthy." "Surfeit" became "gorged."

4. GENDER-NEUTRAL LANGUAGE: "Men" consistently became "people," "humanity," or "humankind." "Bondsmen" became "subjects." "His bondsmen" restructured. "Womanhood" preserved as it refers specifically to women's experience. The Declaration of Independence quote is kept verbatim as a historical document.

5. RELIGIOUS/SPIRITUAL CONTENT: George's sincere religious language is preserved faithfully. The closing vision with its references to Patmos (St. John's Revelation), the City of God, and the Prince of Peace is translated without amplification or diminishment. "The Almighty" became "God" in one instance for naturalness but the passage retains its theological force.

6. ENDNOTES: No endnote markers appear in this chapter.

7. TONE MANAGEMENT: The chapter's emotional arc -- from measured summary to passionate argument to soaring vision -- is the most important thing to preserve. I let George be dramatic where he's dramatic, especially in the final paragraphs where he reaches the heights of his rhetoric.

8. SECTION HEADERS: Added sparingly to help readers navigate, placed at natural transition points.
