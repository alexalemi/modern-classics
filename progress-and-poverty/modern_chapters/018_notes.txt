- THE NET PRODUCT FORMULA: What matters is not total production but the net product after rent. Wages + Interest = Production - Rent. When rent rises, wages and interest must fall (in proportion, though not necessarily in absolute quantity -- as established in chapter 016).
- THE COPERNICAN ANALOGY: George compares his revolution in economics to Copernicus's revolution in astronomy. Mainstream economics, like pre-Copernican astronomy, uses elaborate ad hoc devices to explain phenomena that a simpler theory explains elegantly. But George's truth is MORE intuitive than Copernicus's, not less -- it's naturally perceived in simple societies and only obscured by complexity.
- LAND VALUES AS THE KEY VARIABLE: "Wherever land values are relatively low, wages and interest are relatively high. Wherever land values are relatively high, wages and interest are relatively low." This inverse relationship between land values and wages/interest is the empirical prediction of George's theory.
