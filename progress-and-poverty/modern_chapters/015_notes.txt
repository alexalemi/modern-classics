KEY TERMS AND CONCEPTS ESTABLISHED:

- THE LAW OF INTEREST: Interest is determined by the average power of increase that capital gains from reproductive use. Interest rises and falls with wages, and both are determined by the margin of cultivation. Interest = a corollary of the law of rent.
- WAGES AND INTEREST RISE AND FALL TOGETHER: This is a crucial point. George argues against the conventional view that wages and interest are opposed. They move in the same direction because capital is just stored-up labor.
- CAPITAL AS A FORM OF LABOR: "Labor impressed upon matter -- labor stored up in matter." The coal/heat metaphor is established. Capital's distinction from labor is "really just a subdivision."
- THE TWOFOLD VS. THREEFOLD DIVISION: George's final paragraph reveals his deeper view -- the real division is between rent (return to land/nature) and wages broadly conceived (return to all human effort, whether direct labor or capital). This is foundational for the rest of the argument.
- THE FLYWHEEL METAPHOR: Wealth acts as a flywheel between capital and consumer goods, absorbing and releasing capital as needed. The jeweler/diamonds example illustrates this.
- THE FOOL'S PARADISE THOUGHT EXPERIMENT: A world where capital produces wealth without labor, showing that even then, rent would eat into returns. Proves that interest falls as rent rises.

STYLE NOTES: Bold section headers used throughout. "Economics" for "political economy." Gender-neutral language. The Dick-and-Harry partnership example retained with original names. The formula: Production = Rent + Wages + Interest, but really Rent vs. (Wages + Interest).
